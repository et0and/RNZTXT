Watch live: 'A heart for his people': Hipkins farewells the 'King of Kotahitanga'

Former and current politicians are being welcomed at Tuurangawaewae to pay respects to Kiingi Tuheitia. Video

Former and current politicians are being welcomed at Tuurangawaewae Marae today to pay their respects to Kiingi Tuheitia.

Thousands of mourners have been gathering at Tuurangawaewae Marae to pay their respects to Kiingi Tuheitia Pootatau Te Wherowhero VII this week, with the people of Tainui epitomising the Māori value of Manaakitanga - or hospitality - during the tangihanga of Kiingi Tuheitia.

On Wednesday, politicians will pay their respects to Kiingi Tuheitia and take part in the two scheduled pōwhiri.

Labour leader Chris Hipkins, alongside former prime minister Helen Clark, have been welcomed alongside other iwi and this afternoon the coalition and the Greens will take part in the 2pm pōwhiri.

Acting Prime Minister Winston Peters will lead the coalition contingent arriving at Tuurangawaewae Marae.
