Severe gales up to 120km/h, heavy rain expected for Gisborne and Hawke's Bay

The regions are expecting more rainfall over the next day and a half than they have had the whole month of June.

Heavy rain warnings in the North Island on Tuesday. Photo: Supplied / MetService

Gisborne and Hawke's Bay can expect more rainfall over the next day and a half than the regions have seen for the the whole month of June.

Orange heavy rain warnings are in place from 10am Tuesday until 12pm Wednesday for Gisborne and from 10am Tuesday until 9pm Wednesday for Hawke's Bay, north of SH5.

Hawke's Bay about and south of SH5 had a orange heavy rain warning in place from 11am Tuesday until 3pm Wednesday.

MetService meteorologist Clare O'Connor said current models predict the heaviest rainfall will hit this afternoon.

Gisborne could see peak rainfall at a rate of 25 millimetres per hour, she said.

Orange strong wind warnings were also in place there, with severe gale southerlies gusting at 120 kilometres an hour in exposed places.

There is also a strong wind watch for Bay of Plenty, east of Whakatane, from 3pm on Tuesday until 6am on Wednesday.

Further periods of rain or showers were forecast through the remainder of Wednesday, Thursday and Friday, but MetService said intensities and amounts were expected to be much lower.

People were advised to clear drains and gutters ahead of heavy rain, avoid low-lying areas and drive with caution.
