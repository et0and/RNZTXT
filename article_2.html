Live weather updates: 'The wind seems worse than cyclone Gabrielle'

State of emergencies have been declared in the Heretaunga Ward in Hastings and Wairoa, due to the potential risk to lives and properties.

The flooded Hikowai River at Willowflat on Wednesday morning. Photo: Gisborne District Council

Hawke's Bay Civil Defence group controller Ian MacDonald says Wairoa is the biggest concern in the area right now.

State of emergencies have been declared in the Heretaunga Ward in Hastings and Wairoa, due to the potential risk to lives and properties.

State Highway 2 connecting Napier to Wairoa is closed due to slips and about 90 people have been evacuated. A slip on State Highway 38 earlier closed the road but it has since reopened.

MacDonald said flooding threats to the substation meant the south of Wairoa was without power.

The big issue was the sea large swells were preventing the rain from being able to get out the rivermouth, he said.

The water is backing up towards the town, he said.

He expected further evacuations on Wednesday.

Wairoa Mayor Craig Little said he spoke to the local supermarket operator and had been assured supplies would be able to get into the region.
