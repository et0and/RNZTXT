Australia claim SailGP victory on Kiwi waters

The Black Foils fell behind their rivals to finish fifth overall.

New Zealand SailGP Team helmed by Peter Burling in action as they hit a wave during racing on Race Day 2 of The Rolex SailGP 2025 Championship ITM New Zealand Sail Grand Prix in Auckland, New Zealand. Sunday 19 January 2025. Photo: Bob Martin for SailGP. Handout image supplied by SailGP Photo: Photosport/Bob Martin for SailGP

Australia have claimed SailGP victory on Kiwi waters, as Tom Slingsby steered his three-time champions home in Auckland and the home team failed to reach the final.

After struggling on the opening day, the Black Foils were one point outside the top-three contention for the final, but they were again astray in blustery winds on Sunday, falling behind their rivals to finish fifth overall.

Australia were the most consistent on day one and won the opening race of day two to secure their place in the final, with Great Britain almost as safe.

The real battle was for the third and final spot, with NZ, Spain, Italy and Denmark jostling across the last two races.

Photo: Photosport/Felix Diemer for SailGP

The Kiwis were midfleet in the second race of the day and were dead last off the startline in the third, but worked their way through the field for second - but it was not enough.

After almost toppling over in warm-ups, the Danes won the last two races and that still wasn't enough, as the champion Spaniards grabbed the last spot in third.
