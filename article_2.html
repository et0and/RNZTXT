Driver rescued after several hours trapped in car which hit house

Police received a report of a car colliding with a house on Riverside Drive in Merrilands about 9.15am.

Police have closed Riverside Drive while emergency services respond. (file photo) Photo:

One person has been rescued after becoming trapped inside a car for several hours after it crashed into a New Plymouth home.

Police said they received a report of a vehicle colliding with a house on Riverside Drive in Merrilands about 9.15am.

Fire and Emergency said crews rescued the sole occupant of the car who had been trapped underneath the house on Riverside Drive in Merrilands.

They say it took several hours to pull the car out with a fire truck due to the position of the car.

They don't believe anyone was inside the home, and said there were no other injuries.

FENZ confirmed power lines were knocked down during the crash and power was off in the area.

The spokesperson said the fallen lines also made it difficult for them to get to the driver safely.

St John said it sent three ambulances, a rapid response vehicle, and an operations manager to the scene.

One person had been taken to Taranaki Base Hospital in a moderate condition, St John said.

A police spokesperson said the road has been closed while emergency services respond.

"Motorists are to avoid the area if possible and expect delays."
