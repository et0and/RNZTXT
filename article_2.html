Police to axe 173 jobs in bid to save more than $50m over four years

Police say no frontline roles will be part of the cost-cutting measure.

Police Commissioner Andrew Coster. Photo: RNZ / Nick Monro

The police are slashing 173 jobs following the government's cost-cutting directive.

It is a slight increase on the original proposal it consulted with staff on in August, which floated getting rid of 170 roles.

Police say staff were informed of the final decision last week, and the changes relate to its corporate workforce - not sworn officers.

It estimates the restructure will save the organisation $51.6 million over four years.
