Live: Trump pardons 1500 people charged over January 6 Capitol riots

US President Donald Trump has wasted no time getting stuck into paperwork, signing mountains of orders on his first day back in the office.

Earlier today, Donald Trump was sworn in as the president of the United States of America for the second time.

He has wasted no time getting stuck into paperwork, signing dozens of orders on his first day back in office.

One of his most immediate priorities was to pardon about 1500 people who stormed the US Capitol on 6 January, 2021.

His supporters assaulted police and caused damage to buildings as they tried to prevent lawmakers from certifying his 2020 election defeat.

The new president expressed a wish for their immediate release if they had been jailed.

Trump has also signed several executive orders both at a rally with supporters and now in the Oval Office, where he is also signing pardons.
