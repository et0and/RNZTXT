Olympics live: NZ men's team pursuit miss medal chance, women qualify fastest

The Kiwi men will race for fifth or sixth while the women smashed the national record and are medal favourites.

Bryony Botha is part of the impressive women's team pursuit team. Photo: PHOTOSPORT

The Kiwi men will race for fifth or sixth while the women smashed the national record in the team pursuit.

Ally Wollaston, Bryony Botha, Emily Shearman and Nicole Shields set a new national record finishing in a time of 4 minutes 04.679.

New Zealand will race Italy in the first round after midnight with the winner getting the chance to go for the gold or silver. If they lose they will be among nations racing for the bronze.

The Kiwi quartet of Aaron Gate, Tom Sexton, Keegan Hornblow and Campbell Stewart beat Belgium this morning but other results mean they will race France for fifth and sixth tomorrow.

Meanwhile, Lisa Carrington, along with team mates Alicia Hoskin, Olivia Brett and Tara Vaughan, won the women's K4 500m and go straight through to the final.

Carrington and team mate Hoskin also won their K2 500m and are in the the semi-finals.

First-time Olympian middle-distance runner Maia Ramsden is through to the semi-final of the 1500m after coming sixth in her heat.
