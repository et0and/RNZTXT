Live: Hīkoi halts traffic ahead of Treaty Principles Bill reading

Thousands are moving down the country as Parliament prepares to debate the bill for the first time. Audio

Thousands of people have stopped traffic in Hamilton this morning as the national hīkoi made its way through the city.

The hīkoi is continuing even as the legislation they are protesting gets its first reading in Parliament on Thursday afternoon.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon said the bill was "simplistic" but they would abide their agreement to see it to select committee.

ACT leader David Seymour disagreed saying today was "fantastic".

In Hamilton, the hīkoi grew in size swelling to more than 6000 as people made their way from FMG Stadium to the city's gardens.

The hīkoi enters day four Thursday and will head off from Huntly, destined for Rotorua today, after it advanced through Auckland on Wednesday.
