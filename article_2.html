Evacuations as firefighters tackle several blazes

Some properties south of a large fire in Amberley are being evacuated, Fire and Emergency Canterbury says.

File photo. Photo: RNZ / Robin Martin

Some properties south of a large fire in Amberley are being evacuated, Fire and Emergency Canterbury says.

It is one of several fires in the Canterbury region today, Fire and Emergency says. Crews are also fighting four vegetation fires on Loburn Whiterock Road, near Loburn.

It says multiple tankers and three helicopters are responding to the Amberley fire on Racecourse Road, which was reported early this afternoon.

Some properties are threatened by the blaze in among vegetation which is being fuelled by tree stumps, tyres, grass and car wrecks.

FENZ says the affected area is about 1km long and 500 metres wide.

The Hurunui District Council has asked those being evacuated to make their way to Hurunui Library on Carters Rd in the town.

"There will be further updates about an evacuation location as soon as possible. Thank you for your cooperation and patience," it said in a post on social media.

Eight fire trucks, three tankers and three helicopters are attending the Loburn fires which are estimated to cover about a square kilometre.

Police are in the process of evacuating those affected.

Nearby residents who may be affected by smoke should close windows and doors and reduce outdoor exercise, a FENZ spokesperson said in a statement.

Residents are advised to call Healthline on 0800 611 116 if they have any concerns.

The council has also appealed to residents in both Amberley and Loburn to limit their water use and not to turn on appliances such as washing machines.

"We have diverted extra water supply to allow the emergency crews to fight the fire."

FENZ has appealed to people to stay away from the scene of the fire so they do not hamper efforts to bring it under control.
