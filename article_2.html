Search on for person missing from boat in Manukau Harbour

A second person on the boat was able to swim ashore and raise the alarm.

Coastguard said the search conditions on Tuesday night were challenging due to swell and darkness. Photo: Coastguard / Supplied

The search for a person missing from a boat in Auckland's Manukau Harbour is expected to continue at first light.

On Tuesday night Coastguard said two people on a boat got into difficulty near Puketutu Island.

One person swam back to shore and alerted police, but the other was still missing.

The boat was towed back to shore on Tuesday night. Coastguard said the search ended for the night.

Police confirmed they responded to a distress call at 5.50pm.

"Another person is believed to still be missing in the water."

Coastguard volunteers from Titirangi and Papakura were involved in the search.

Coastguard said the search conditions on Tuesday night were challenging due to the swell and darkness.

"But our volunteers that are out on the water on three rescue vessels are committed to doing all they can to try find the missing person."

Auckland Airport's hovercraft and Westpac Rescue Helicopter also earlier assisted in the search.
