Watch: Hīkoi arrives at Ihumātao

The hīkoi protesting the Treaty Principles Bill made its way from Auckland's North Shore to Ihumātao on Wednesday.

On day three of the hīkoi making its way to Parliament in protest of the Treaty Principles Bill, thousands crossed the Auckland Harbour Bridge.

Supporters cheered as the hīkoi crossed, with RNZ reporters at the scene saying the bridge began to rock under their feet as the wind picked up.

The kaupapa behind the march is as much about kotahitanga as the bill itself, organisers say.

After the bridge crossing, the hīkoi travelled to Bastion Point, before journeying to Ihumātao.

They will stay overnight at Rangiriri Paa.
