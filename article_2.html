Yanfei Bao: Discovery of remains brings hopes of closure

A Christchurch city councillor hopes the discovery of remains in the year-long search for Yanfei Bao will bring some closure.

Yanfei Bao was last seen alive in July last year. Photo: Supplied

A Christchurch city councillor hopes the discovery of remains in the year-long search for real estate agent Yanfei Bao will bring some closure to a case that has gripped the community.

The 44-year-old was last seen alive on 19 July last year at a property in Hornby, where she was due to show a potential buyer through a house.

A man charged with her murder is due to go on trial in October.

Bao's disappearance shocked and horrified the community and the country.

An extensive police search took place over months, centred on Lake Ellesmere and nearby suburbs.

Police now believe they have found Bao's body, after a recent tip-off led officers to find a shallow grave on a private farm in Greenpark yesterday.

Mark Peters is the councillor for Hornby and said her disappearance had gripped the community.

"It's not something you want to have happening in your neighbourhood, and not having the certainty of closure - it's not been a comfortable feeling."

He hoped yesterday's discovery would bring that closure.

Peters said the case has also opened up conversations about safety.

"It makes you think about when you are out door-knocking, or if you are in a profession like real estate when you are on your own, you have to make sure you're safe in what you're doing."

Bao had been working at Harcourts.

Managing director Bryan Thomson said in a statement they had seen the confronting news and their thoughts were with her family, friends and colleagues within their network.
