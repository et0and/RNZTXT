Downed powerlines on SH2 cause traffic backlog as bad weather hits North Island

Downpours are threatening to bring flash floods to parts of the island, with some facing heavy rain until Boxing Day.

Waka Kotahi warned motorists of traffic on SH2 northbound from Wellington after it was partially blocked by downed power lines on 25 December, 2023. Photo: Supplied / Waka Kotahi

Downpours and thunderstorms are set to hit large parts of the North Island, with the deluge potentially continuing into Boxing Day for some.

MetService has issued a severe thunderstorm watch, covering Northland, Auckland, Coromandel Peninsula, Waikato, Waitomo, Taumarunui, Bay of Plenty, Rotorua, Taupo and Taihape from Monday afternoon.

"These thunderstorms may become severe bringing localised rainfall rates of 25 to 40 mm per hour," MetService said.

"Rainfall of this intensity can cause surface and/or flash flooding, especially about low-lying areas such as streams, rivers or narrow valleys, and may also lead to slips."

The rain is expected to set in from the afternoon, with the watch lasting until 10pm.

A strong winds warning is in place over Remutaka Hill, on State Highway 2.

SH2 northbound from Wellington has re-opened after it was partially blocked by downed power lines, which were brought down by bad weather combined with probable salt corrosion.

Waka Kotahi said it might take some time for traffic - which was backed up for several kilometres - to ease.

Wellington Electricity said a fault crew had secured the stray wires and another crew was on its way to fix them.

The South Island may be affected by some of the storms too on Monday afternoon and evening.
