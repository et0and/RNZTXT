Live: Lisa Carrington and Aimee Fisher go head-to-head in kayak sprints

Follow all the latest updates from the Paris Games with RNZ's live blog.

New Zealand canoe sprint athletes Dame Lisa Carrington and Aimee Fisher after winning gold and silver in the K1 500 at the 2023 World Cup regatta in Hungary. Photo: Canoe Sport NZ

The pole-vaulting trio of Eliza McCartney, Olivia McTaggart and Imogen Ayris will chase Olympic glory on Wednesday night, with all three in the Paris Olympic Games final.

Just three times in Olympic history have two New Zealanders stood on different levels of the podium for the same event. Two world-class athletes could be about to do it again in Paris with Dame Lisa Carrington and Aimee Fisher both lining up in the Canoe Sprint K1 500.

The women's team pursuit are also on track for gold at the velodrome.

Ally Wollaston, Bryony Botha, Emily Shearman and Nicole Shields broke the national record and were just 0.4s off the world record in being top qualifiers and advancing to a semi-final against Italy.
