Cats rescued from blaze at Wellington vet and cattery given oxygen

The owner of a Wellington vet centre and cattery that was gutted by fire on Saturday night says emergency crews did an amazing job helping to rescue as many cats as possible.

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper says he does not know how many cats have survived or died in the blaze as they have not been able to get inside the building, which also contains the businesses paperwork. Photo: RNZ / Rachel Helyer-Donaldson

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper says at this stage he does not know how many cats have survived or died in the blaze.

The owner of a Wellington vet centre and cattery that was gutted by fire on Saturday night says emergency crews did an amazing job helping to rescue as many cats as possible.

Emergency services were called to the fire at the two storey building on Mahora Street in Kilbirnie at 11.50pm.

CareVets vet clinic and cattery in the Wellington suburb of Kilbirnie has been gutted by fire. Photo: RNZ / Rachel Helyer-Donaldson

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper said cats which were pulled out of the blaze were given oxygen and taken to the after-hours clinic.

He said he did not know how many cats had survived the blaze or died as they have not been able to get into the building and computers and paperwork were also inside.

There are two apartments above the vets but everyone got out safely.

Cooper said he and his staff had been on site since 3am on Sunday.

FENZ central shift manager Murray Dunbar said eight appliances and four specialist support vehicles attended the fire, which was put out around 2.30am.

The fire at CareVets Kilbirnie would be investigated, he said.
