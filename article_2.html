'Pool of blood' on road outside Far North school

Police, firefighters and paramedics were called to Settlers Way in Ōkaihau.

Settlers Way, Ōkaihau. Photo: Google Maps

A resident in a Far North town has described seeing a "pool of blood" on the road after an overnight incident.

Emergency services were called to the Far North town of Ōkaihau late on Tuesday night, but so far secrecy surrounds the incident.

Police, Fire and Emergency New Zealand and St John were called to Settlers Way, the town's main street, at about 10.30pm on Tuesday.

Fire and Emergency and St John were referring all questions to police, who were not commenting on the situation for now.

One resident told RNZ she saw a pool of blood on the road outside Ōkaihau College on Wednesday morning, and a second patch about 50 to 100 metres away marked with neon spray paint.

She said she saw police speaking to a staff member at the college, which was due to reopen on Friday after the summer break.

A police spokesperson said he needed to confirm "a number of facts" before he could provide any information about what had happened.
