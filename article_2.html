Live: Homes lose power as thunderstorm, risk of tornadoes moves across North Island.

Warnings are getting more severe as much of the country is either already being hit or about to face heavy rain. Audio

MetService's forecast for the day. Photo: MetService screenshot

MetService is warning there could be small tornadoes in coastal areas on Wednesday, as heavy downpours and thunderstorms are being forecast for parts of the country.

Heavy rain watches are in place for Northland (until 9am), Coromandel (until 2pm) and Bay of Plenty (until 10pm).

A watch is also in place for the Tasman, west of Motueka through until about 5pm. It says there will be periods of heavy downpours and the possibility of thunderstorms.

RNZ is New Zealand's statutory civil defence lifeline radio broadcaster, providing vital information and updates as they come to hand.

Residents can access RNZ National on 101.4FM or 756AM in Coromandel; 819AM in Bay of Plenty and east of Opotoki; 101.6FM or 1116AM in Nelson and Motueka; and 567AM West of Takaka.

The National Emergency Management Agency (NEMA) advises that in times of crisis or civil defence emergency a battery powered radio or a car radio remain essential lifelines if power is out and other forms of communication are unavailable.
