Corrections staff allegedly bribed with sex and money

Six people have been charged with corruption and bribery after a three-year probe by police into the jail.

Rimutaka Prison. (File image) Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

Corrections staff were allegedly bribed with sex and money to take contraband into Rimutaka Prison.

Six people have been charged with corruption and bribery after a three-year investigation by police into the Upper Hutt jail.

A 50-year-old man, who cannot be named, appeared in Hutt Valley District Court on Tuesday for allegedly accepting sex twice from a woman named Jacinta Cherry, 37, as a bribe to smuggle contraband into the prison.

But Cherry, who is charged with bribing the man, failed to appear and Judge Tim Black refused her lawyer's request for name suppression. He also issued a warrant for her arrest.

A current Rimutaka inmate is allegedly party to offering that bribe, while three men have been charged with accepting money to bring in contraband.
