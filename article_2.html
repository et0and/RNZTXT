Watch live: Papua New Guinea PM and Luxon speak at meeting

PNG PM James Marape and Christopher Luxon are holding a signing ceremony and media conference following a bilateral meeting

The Prime Minister of Papua New Guinea, James Marape, and Prime Minister Christopher Luxon are holding a signing ceremony and media conference following a bilateral meeting.

It is due to begin at 7pm.

Marape arrived in New Zealand on Tuesday morning for a three-day state visit.

The two prime ministers were set to discuss to discuss trade, investment, agriculture, fisheries, education and security as well as other areas of mutual concern between the two nations.

New Zealand signed a Statement of Partnership (2021-2024) with Papua New Guinea under former Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern.

"This is being refreshed this week," a statement from Marape's office said.

This is Marape's first official visit to New Zealand following his re-election as prime minister in the last national elections in 2022.

According to the PNG government, the visit signals a growing relationship between the two countries, especially in trade and investment, cultural exchange, and the newly-added Recognised Seasonal Employer (RSE) scheme that New Zealand has extended to Papua New Guineans to work in Aotearoa.
