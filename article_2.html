Plane collides with Blackhawk helicopter midair, crashes into US river

The Federal Aviation Administration has confirmed that the jet collided with a helicopter on its approach to an airport in Virginia.

An American Airlines flight takes off from onald Reagan Washington National Airport DCA. (File pic) Photo: AFP / NurPhoto / Nicolas Economou

A small commercial aircraft has crashed in the Potomac River near Reagan International Airport in Virginia.

The Federal Aviation Administration has confirmed that the jet has collided with a helicopter on its approach to the airport.

The Washington DC police say it is not their helicopter.

Reuters reports the FAA says a PSA Airlines Bombardier regional jet collided in midair with a Sikorsky H-60 (Blackhawk) helicopter on its approach.

The White House said it appeared to be a military helicopter and President Donald Trump was aware and was monitoring the incident.

A multi-agency search and rescue operation is underway in the river, Reuters reports.

Fireboats were on the scene, the Washington fire department said.

The airport said late on Wednesday that all takeoffs and landings were halted at the airport as emergency personnel responded to an aircraft incident.

The National Transportation Safety Board said it was gathering more information on the incident.

CNN reports the PSA/AA codeshare had flown from Wichita.

There has not been a commercial airline crash in the United States for 15 years.

The aircraft has a capacity of 68 passengers.
