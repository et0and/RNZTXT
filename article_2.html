10 households evacuated as fire threatens homes

The fire is currently contained, but it is unclear whether evacuees will be able to return to their properties tonight, FENZ says.

Ten households have been evacuated in Te Puna after a shelterbelt fire broke out.

The fire is currently contained but is estimated to have burned around 3.5km of shelterbelt, Fire and Emergency NZ said.

Shelterbelts are rows of trees planted to provide wind breaks and improve micro climates.

Fire and Emergency (FENZ) said crews were called about 3.20pm on Wednesday to "reports of multiple shelter belts on fire" near Snodgrass Road.

At 6.30pm, there were 16 fire engines at the blaze with multiple support vehicles, it said.

It was not clear whether the evacuees would be able to return to their homes tonight, FENZ said.

Crews will be working overnight and a helicopter will continue working to extinguish the fire until twilight.

A large fire has broken out in Te Puna, Bay of Plenty, threatening homes and properties. Photo: Supplied / Chelsea Norman via New Zealand Proud

Newnham Park Innovation Centre managing director Ian Jeffrey said the fire was still flaring up at 5.30pm.

Photo: Supplied / Renee Adkins

Wind and the tinder-dry hedgerows were two reasons the fire was still burning, he said.

"The fire brigade will be there for a while," he said.

His business was near to Snodgrass Road, but he was not worried about the fire getting out of control, he said.

Police have advised the community to avoid the area as emergency services work to control the fire.

"If your home is impacted by smoke, please keep your windows and doors closed, and turn off any air conditioning units," FENZ said.

Snodgrass Road is currently blocked and police are considering other road closures.

A manager at a Te Puna business said the fire was far from the village centre, but they could see smoke.
