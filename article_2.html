Easter weekend traffic: Auckland motorway blocked

Aucklanders heading south are likely to face heavy traffic on the Southern Motorway from Manukau to Bombay.

Heavy traffic on the southbound lanes of the SH1 in Auckland. Photo: Google Maps

Traffic on Auckland motorway SH1 southbound near Pahurehure Photo: Waka Kotahi NZTA

Aucklanders heading south out of the city may face heavy traffic on the southbound lanes of the Southern Motorway from Manukau to Bombay.

Traffic was also heavy around Wellsford, north of Auckland.

Meanwhile, traffic is backed up on the southbound lanes of the Waikato Expressway at Taupiri, just north of Hamilton.

A stretch of SH1 between Lichfield and Tokoroa is closed.

Traffic is moving slowly on SH2 near Mangatāwhiri.

Up north, the Brynderwyn Hills stretch of motorway linking Auckland to Northland has opened temporarily for the Easter weekend.

Further south, traffic is building up around Ōtaki in Kāpiti.

In the South Island, there are queues both north and south of Christchurch, near Kaiapoi, and Rolleston and Rakaia.

Airports are expected to be chock-a-block all Easter weekend, with hundreds of thousands of travellers taking to the sky.

Air New Zealand said about 227,000 travellers would fly with the airline this weekend.

Anyone flying over the weekend was asked to get to the airport a little earlier than normal to allow time to get through security.
