Department of Conservation proposes 130 job cuts

It is the latest government department to learn its fate as part of cost cutting measures.

Photo: RNZ/ Nick Monro

RNZ understands about 130 roles could go at the Department of Conservation, with a number of these already vacant positions.

It is the latest department to learn its fate as part of the government's cost cutting measures.

More than 1000 jobs are already set to go across the sector.

Department of Conservation (DOC) staff gathered for a meeting early Wednesday afternoon.

DOC, which manages nearly a third of New Zealand's total land area, has already had its funding cut this year by 21 percent.
