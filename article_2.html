'I want to be as naked as legally possible' - The many looks of Laneway 2025

The whiplash of Gen Z's style was on full display at Auckland's Laneway Festival on Thursday.

Grace, 22, at Laneway Festival today. Photo: Serena Solomon/RNZ

The whiplash of Gen Z's style was on full display at Auckland's Laneway Festival on Thursday.

The skirts were very short. The bike pants were also very short, offering not much more coverage than underwear. The jorts - shorts cut below the knee - were baggy. Bikini tops were plentiful. There were lace and sheer dresses with underwear a key and visible part of the outfit. The leather boots were high and chunky.

"I want to be as naked as legally possible," said Grace, 22.

Like many others, she was at Laneway to see its headlining artist Charli XCX, who won three Grammys on Sunday night including for dance/electronic album. Her 2024 album Brat kickstarted a cultural phenomenon during the Western Hemisphere summer - known as Brat summer - exhorting fans to ditch the manicured life and embrace the messy.

Charli XCX's influence - chunky boots, tiny skirts that show a cheeky slice of buttocks, wearing bikinis in places that are not the beach - could be seen amongst the estimated 50,000 festivalgoers.

Some couldn't help but accent what they wore with a touch of lime green, the prominent colour of Charli XCX's album cover for Brat, her breakthrough album of 2024.

"Girls can wear whatever they want and she [Charli XCX] is showing that," said Liang, who wore baggy jorts with a bikini top.
