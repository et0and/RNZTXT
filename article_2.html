Enchanter trial: Skipper not guilty of breaching duties

Five men died after a monster wave capsized the boat near North Cape. The judge says he could not be sure skipper Lance Goodhew could have forseen that coming.

Enchanter skipper, Lance Goodhew, on trial in Whangārei on 6 May, 2024. Photo: RNZ / Peter de Graaf

A Northland charter boat skipper has been found not guilty following a trial relating to the Enchanter tragedy that claimed five lives off North Cape in 2022.

Lance Goodhew, of Mangōnui, was charged by Maritime New Zealand with breaching his duties as a worker on the vessel, putting his passengers and crew at risk of serious injury or death.

The harrowing, three-week-long trial was held in the Whangārei District Court in May, with Judge Philip Rzepecky delivering his verdict this morning.

Judge Rzepecky said the terrible tragedy was caused by a significant rogue wave in otherwise benign conditions.

"I cannot rule out the possibility that the cause of this tragic maritime casualty was just a terrible accident, and not a cause from which anyone was actually responsible for.

"Therefore I'm not satisfied that the prosecution has proven its case against Mr Goodhew in respect of a single charge beyond reasonable doubt."

He could not be sure Goodhew could have foreseen the wave given the information he had at the time.
