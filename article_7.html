Dairy company Synlait makes $96m first-half loss

Dairy company Synlait has made a $96 million first half loss, and announced a review of its North Island assets.

File photo. Photo: Eiliv Aceron for Unsplash

Dairy company Synlait has made a $96 million first half loss, and announced a review of its North Island assets.

The speciality milk producer was carrying a lot of debt, but said it had secured an extension to a $130 million debt repayment until 15 July, after placing itself in a trading halt last week.

It has also secured $30 million in short-term funding to the end of June, along with amendments to its banking arrangements.

Synlait was also planning to raise addition capital, though its share price was trading at a significant discount.

It was yet to find a buyer for DairyWorks business, and was in arbitration with major customer, A2 Milk, after it cancelled Synlait's exclusive supply agreement.

The full year result was expected to be significantly down on the year earlier.

Synlait's trading halt lifts this morning.
