Legal action launched against insurance giant for overcharging

The Financial Markets Authority has started legal action against insurance giant IAG for overcharging consumers.

Photo: 123rf

The Financial Markets Authority has started legal action against insurance giant IAG for what it says is of overcharging consumers.

The FMA alleges the company failed to properly apply multi-policy discounts and overcharged 269,000 clients $35 million stretching back 20 years.

It says IAG self reported the issue and has moved to repay affected customers.

The FMA is seeking a court declaration of wrongdoing and the imposition of a financial penalty.
