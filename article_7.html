Consumer confidence tumbles amid global chaos

The Westpac-McDermott Miller Consumer Confidence Index is down eight points.

Photo: 123rf

Consumer confidence has taken a tumble amid concerns about the uncertainty for the local and global outlook.

The Westpac-McDermott Miller Consumer Confidence Index is down eight points to 89 in the three months ended March.

A reading below 100 is pessimism.

Senior economist Satish Ranchhod says the increased trade tensions and related volatility in financial markets has cast a shadow over the future.

He says households are still struggling with financial pressures from the cost of living such as food, insurance and rates.

Wellington is the most pessimistic region in the survey.
