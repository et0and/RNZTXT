Stuff TV bulletin replacing Newshub gets new name

Over the years it has had different names ranging from 3 National News, to 3 News, and recently Newshub.

Samantha Hayes will present the new bulletin. Photo: Supplied / Lee Howell IDC

Stuff has revealed the logo and name of its new 6pm TV bulletin that will replace Newshub at 6 from July.

It will be now be called ThreeNews.

Over the years it has had different names ranging from 3 National News, to 3 News, and recently Newshub.

Stuff produced ThreeNews will launch on 6 July 2024 on ThreeNow and TV Three. Photo: Stuff

Samantha Hayes, who currently hosts Newshub at 6 will present the new bulletin - which will be hour-long on weekdays and half an hour in weekends - on TV channel Three and the 3 Now app when it begins on 6 July.

"We are producing something that will be both familiar and fresh, a great marriage of the Three brand and Stuff's live and lively news ethos," Stuff Digital managing director Nadia Tolich told Stuff.

It has hired several senior reporters and producers from Newshub and will also use Stuff journalists around the country for the programme.

In April, Newshub's owners Warner Brothers Discovery (WBD) announced it had made a deal with Stuff to create a replacement 6pm news bulletin.

WBD had announced the closure of Newshub in March to curb losses it described as unsustainable.
