TVNZ's E Tū union members unanimously reject restructure proposal

TVNZ staff are railing against the broadcaster's proposal to slash almost 70 jobs, and programmes including Sunday and Fair Go.

Photo: RNZ/Calvin Samuel

TVNZ staff are railing against the broadcaster's proposal to slash almost 70 jobs, and programmes including Sunday and Fair Go.

Staff were told of the plans last week, but E Tū union members have today unanimously rejected the proposal.

E Tū negotiations specialist Michael Wood says members are angry about both the suggested changes, and shoddy consultation.

They understand change is needed, but want the opportunity to share alternative ideas, rather than being dictated to by their employer, Wood said.

The union has asked TVNZ for more time to provide feedback, better collaboration, and to share the financial information it used to inform the proposal, he said.
