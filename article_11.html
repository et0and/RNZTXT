What's inside the $400,000 Oscar goodie bags?

Holidays, cosmetics and home renovation vouchers were amongst the lush loot.

The goodie bags most-expensive trip includes a villa in the Maldives worth over $23,000. Photo: Facebook/Travel Buddy

While only a few will go home with the ultimate prize, not all who miss out on a 2025 Oscar award will go home empty handed.

An entertainment marketing company Distinctive Assets has given some seriously luxurious swag bags to Oscar nominees worth a whopping $385,000, according to Business Insider.

So what's inside? Vacations, cosmetics and other prizes from over 60 brands.

The "Everyone Wins" gift bags included several trips, with the most-expensive being a villa in the Maldives worth over $41,000.

Other trips included a five-night luxury wellness retreat in Sri Lanka worth $15,000 and a 3d Virtual Wellness Retreat valued at $6000.

The bags also included $45,000 of body-contouring treatments and Miage's full line of skincare products.

But the most valuable item was $90,000 worth of home renovation project management, which Distinctive Assets said was transferrable to those affected by wildfires.

The selected nominees would also receiving $6000 in disaster recovery services and 10 more one year vouchers to share with those in need.

Distinctive Assets company founder Lash Fary. Photo: Supplied/Swag Assets Instagram

In a statement, company founder Lash Fary said while the gift bags were fun, they were also meant to give back.

"While our gifts may be famous for being fun and fabulous, they also serve as a means to elevate small businesses, minority-owned brands, female entrepreneurs and companies that give back.

"This year, on the heels of the historically tragic LA fires, we have found even more ways that our celebrity swag can do good in our community."

Fary said the "incredible" gift bags - going to some of the top 25 acting and directing nominees - were to "brighten someone's day if they do go home empty handed on Oscar night".

"Also including some simple but significant ways for these nominees to give back," he said in a video on social media.

The company has been gifting out the goodie bags at the annual awards ceremony for the last 23 years.

The brands pay for the items to be included in the prize packs.
