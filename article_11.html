Emmys 2024: The must-see red carpet looks

Here are some of looks from the second Emmys red carpet of the year held at the Peacock Theater in Los Angeles.

Photo: AFP

It's Emmys time - again.

After being delayed last year due to the writers' and actors' strikes, the Emmy Awards ceremony for shows released between June 1, 2022, and May 31, 2023, was held in January.

Now we're back for another Emmys ceremony - this time to present awards for shows released between 1 June, 2023 to 31 May, 2024.

It's all a bit confusing, but the upshot is that we get two awards shows this year - and two red carpet events.

Here are some of looks from the second Emmys red carpet of the year held at the Peacock Theater in Los Angeles.

Kiwi-born Anna Sawai is up for an Emmy for her role in Shōgun. She wore a red dress by Vera Wang, paired with sparkling Cartier jewellery and styled by Karla Welch. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AFP

Rita Ora and her husband, Taika Waititi. Waititi's two shows What We Do in the Shadows and Reservation Dogs are both nominated in the Outstanding Comedy Series category. Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

Father and son, Dan and Eugene Levy, hosts of the event. Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

Devery Jacobs proving that you can wear a suit and a tutu at the same time. Photo: Frazer Harrison / Getty Images / AFP

Selena Gomez in a dress that deserves to have a team of assistants dedicated to constantly fanning out the train so it looks like this all night. Photo: Amy Sussman / Getty Images / AFP

Ayo Edebiri wore a custom Bottega Veneta strapless gown, featuring a multicolored print and a thigh-high leg slit. The Bear star has a Best Actress in a Comedy Series nomination. Photo: Frazer Harrison / Getty Images / AFP

We're pretty sure Nicola Coughlan's dress is made out of sequins, but we're not ruling out the possibility that it's actually made from frozen of moonbeam shards. Photo: Frazer Harrison / Getty Images / AFP

Sex Education's Gillian Anderson. Photo: Amy Sussman / Getty Images / AFP

Jennifer Aniston wore a strapless Oscar de la Renta gown composed entirely of clusters of white pearls and small crystals arranged in different symmetrical patterns. Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

The Bear's Jeremy Allen White, who is up for Best Actor in a Comedy Series, wore a sleek black tuxedo. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AFP

Meryl Streep wore a baby pink suit with pussy-bow blouse, with boxy double-breasted blazer and pleated wide-leg pants. Streep has been nominated for an Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series for her role on Only Murders in the Building. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AFP

Mindy Kaling wore a black Gaurav Gupta gown with a sculpted off-the-shoulder detail and cascading beading. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AFP

Reese Witherspoon and Laura Dern who starred in Big Little Lies together. Witherspoon, in Dior, was nominated for her work on The Morning Show. Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

Country music legend, Reba McEntire, Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

Dakota Fanning in a strapless champagne-colored gown by Armani Privé. Fanning is nominated for a Best Supporting Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series for Movie for her work on Ripley. Photo: Frazer Harrison/Getty Images/AFP

Scottish actor, Alan Cumming, host of The Traitors. Photo: Frazer Harrison / Getty Images / AFP

Abbott Elementary star, Tyler James Williams. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AFP

We think the designer of Catherine O'Hara's dress was a spider who loves roses and has a very rare condition that means their web comes out looking like a string of diamonds. But that's unconfirmed at this stage. Photo: Amy Sussman / Getty Images / AFP

Lily Gladstone Lily rocks a chest plate on the red carpet. She's is up for Best Supporting Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie for Under the Bridge. Photo: Amy Sussman/Getty Images/AF

Aja Naomi King nominated for Best Supporting Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie for her performance in Lessons in Chemistry, Photo: Frazer Harrison / Getty Images / AFP
