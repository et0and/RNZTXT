Sam Cane faces lengthy break after serious injury

The All Blacks captain may have at least a couple of months on the sideline after reportedly suffering a serious injury in Japan.

New Zealand All Blacks captain Sam Cane poses in his new Suntory Sungoliath uniform, 2023. Photo: AFP

All Blacks captain Sam Cane faces at least a couple of months on the sideline after reportedly suffering a serious injury playing for Japanese club Suntory Sungoliath.

Suntory, who sit third on the Division 1 table, have also lost Springbok wing Cheslin Kolbe to injury.

Japan-based rugby journalist Rich Freeman reports that Cane injured his back and could be out of action for up to ten weeks.

Cane was out of the game for several months after breaking his neck in 2018, while he missed the start of last year's World Cup with a back problem.

The 32 year old is on sabbitical in Japan and will miss the Super Rugby Pacific season.

Sungoliath having some bad luck with their imports.Gareth Anscombe forced to leave club before playing a game and I am now hearing on the grapevine that Sam Cane could be out for 10 weeks (back) and Cheslin Kolbe for 4-6 weeks (hand).#rugbyjp pic.twitter.com/y24JmiATZD

Both Cane and Kolbe, who has a hand injury, missed last weekend's round of fixtures.

With a ten-week recovery Cane is set to miss most of the rest of the season which finishes in May.

The All Blacks first game of the season is against England in early July.
