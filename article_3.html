Protester taken away in handcuffs after blocking Wellington road

The man was trying to disrupt traffic by parking on Boulcott Street in central Wellington.

The man was taken away in handcuffs after blocking Boulcott Street with his car. Photo: RNZ / Mary Argue

A climate protester has been taken away in handcuffs by police after parking his car on a central Wellington street.

Michael O'Hara, who said he was from Engines Off, said he would remain parked in the middle of Boulcott Street on Thursday morning until the police forced him to move.

He arrived just after 7am on Thursday.

Initially other traffic moved around his silver Toyota Clavia.

Photo: Mary Argue/RNZ

Within half an hour three police cars and four officers arrived.

They talked to O'Hara before putting him in handcuffs and into the back of a police car.

By 7.30am traffic was freeflowing.
