New Zealand golfer aces iconic hole

Ryan Fox has aced the iconic 17th Island Green hole at the $40 million Players Championship in Florida.

Ryan Fox of New Zealand celebrates after holing his tee shot on the 17th hole for a hole in one during the first round of The Players Championship at TPC Sawgrass, 2024 Photo: AFP

Ryan Fox has recorded a rare feat in golf, hitting a hole in one.

Fox has aced the iconic 17th Island Green hole at the $40 million Players Championship in Florida.

The 37-year-old New Zealander etched his name into the history books as he became just the 14th player to record a hole-in-one at the 17th at the Players Championship.

He also eagled the 16th.

HOLE-IN-ONE FOR @RYANFOXGOLFER! He lights up the Island Green @THEPLAYERS pic.twitter.com/4839JZqb6z

However, he has had an up and down round and has also recorded three bogeys.

He is currently two under par through 10 holes.

Xander Schauffele and Rory McIlroy are the current leaders.
