Black Caps score convincing win over Pakistan to open Champions Trophy

Centuries from Will Young and Tom Latham guided the Black Caps to a comfortable 60-run win over hosts Pakistan in Karachi.

New Zealand's Will Young Photo: PHOTOSPORT

Centuries from Will Young and Tom Latham have guided the Black Caps to a comfortable 60-run win over hosts Pakistan in Karachi in the opening match at the Champions Trophy.

After being asked to bat first New Zealand scored 320 for five in their 50 overs with Young making 107 and Latham finishing unbeaten on 118.

Glenn Phillips added 61 off 39 balls late in the innings.

Young played in place of the injured Rachin Ravindra.

In reply the Black Caps bowlers picked up regular wickets to restrict Pakistan to 260, dismissing them in the 48th over.

Will O'Rourke picked up three wickets and Mitchell Santner and Matt Henry two each.

Khushdil Shah top scored with 69, while Babar Azam made 64.

New Zealand's next game is against Bangladesh on Monday.
