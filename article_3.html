Gloriavale leader apologises for historic abuse

Overseeing Shepherd Howard Temple said Gloriavale would listen to victims and support them to heal and receive redress.

Overseeing Shepherd Howard Temple Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

A Gloriavale leader has made a public apology to the community's many victims for the historic abuse they suffered.

In July last year, the Royal Commission of Inquiry into historic Abuse in Care requested Gloriavale apologise to its members.

Overseeing Shepherd Howard Temple delivered an apology to the community on Sunday.

He said the church acknowledged abuse happened within the church between 1950 and 1999.

The church's members were entitled to a safe and supportive environment, and Howard Temple said it failed to provide that.

He said Gloriavale would listen to victims and support them to heal and receive redress.
