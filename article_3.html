Coromandel scrub fire grows overnight

There were three helicopters and four ground crews fighting the blaze on Sunday morning.

Crews are responding to a scrub fire in Kūaotunu. Photo: Supplied

A scrub fire on the eastern Coromandel peninsula has grown since Saturday night.

Fire and Emergency is continuing to fight the fire, which broke out yesterday near Kūaotunu.

It had three helicopters and four ground crews at the scene on Sunday morning.

The fire had grown to approximately 600 metres by 600 metres in size, from about 300 metres by 600 metres on Saturday.

Incident Controller, Shane Bromley, says Black Jack Road will be closed all day, with stop go traffic management in place so residents can access their homes.

There have been no evacuations.

A strong southerly wind is blowing smoke from the fire out to sea.

It is not yet known how the fire started.
