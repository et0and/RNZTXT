Navy dispels concerns Manawanui is leaking oil

The Navy says HMNZS Manawanui, which sunk off the coast of Samoa, is not leaking oil.

The Manawanui sank off the south coast of the island of Upolu on Saturday. Photo: Profile Boats / supplied

The Navy says HMNZS Manawanui, the ship which sunk off the coast of Samoa, is not leaking oil.

Navy Commodore Shane Arndell told Checkpoint on Tuesday the Manawanui was resting 30 metres underwater on a reef off the south coast of Upolu, and was not leading.

He said divers on Tuesday confirmed the tanks were holding and there was no leakage, despite claims from locals oil had impacted marine life.

The Samon government earlier on Tuesday said no leaks had been found.

Nearly 1000 tonnes of diesel fuel were on board HMNZS Manawanui when it sank, Rear Admiral Garin Golding told Morning Report.

A heavy spill kit was being loaded on board vessel the Canterbury, due to depart to Samoa on Wednesday.
