Pope diagnosed with pneumonia in both lungs

The 88-year-old pontiff has shown the onset of bilaterial pneumonia, the Vatican says.

The 88-year-old pontiff was admitted to hospital in Rome on 14 February Photo: Photo by Filippo MONTEFORTE / AFP

Pope Francis has shown the onset of bilaterial pneumonia and his respiratory infection "continues to present a complex picture", the Vatican says.

The 88-year-old pontiff has been suffering from a respiratory infection for more than a week and was admitted to Rome's Gemelli hospital on 14 February.

Francis, who has been pontiff since 2013, has had influenza and other health problems several times over the past two years. As a young adult he developed pleurisy and had part of one lung removed, and in recent times has been prone to lung infections.

Earlier the Vatican said doctors had changed treatment for Pope Francis' respiratory tract infection to tackle a "complex clinical situation" and that he would will remain in hospital for as long as necessary.
