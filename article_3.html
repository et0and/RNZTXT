Traffic disruptions on SH1 as tangi for Head Hunters gang leader unfolds

State Highway 1 was briefly blocked during the funeral of a gang leader in Foxton today.

The body of Head Hunters gang leader William "Bird" Hines is transported along SH1. Photo: RNZ / Jimmy Ellingham

The police say they are maintaining a strong police presence in the ManawatÅ« town where hundreds of gang members have gathered for a funeral.

Bikers arrive for the funeral of a gang member at Foxton on 27 November 2023. Photo: RNZ

The tangi of Head Hunters gang leader William "Bird" Hines started around 11am.

Police are keeping the crowd contained. Both patched gang members and members of the public are evident at the scene.

RNZ's reporter in Foxton said the main highway was briefly blocked as Hines' coffin was transported along the road.

He described the procession as orderly, but significant in size.

Meanwhile, smoke from a bike burnout could be seen billowing through the crowds and many appeared to be filming the procession as it went past.

Photo: RNZ / Jimmy Ellingham

The police said officers dealt with a number of driving-related offences overnight but there were no significant problems.

Assistant Commissioner Mike Johnson said the police Eagle helicopter would remain in the area to provide support as required.

Meanwhile, there is a traffic management plan in place and lane restrictions outside the Motuiti Marae, on State Highway 1.
