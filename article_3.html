Fieldays fashion: What they wore on the opening day

Fieldays visitors put their best fashion foot forward on the Mystery Creek event's opening day. RNZ's Marika Khabazi was there to capture the action.

Attendees at this year's Fieldays in Mystery Creek, Hamilton put their best fashion foot forward on opening day.

RNZ's Marika Khabazi was there to capture the action.

Logan is wearing grandfather's Swandri and 10-year-old cowboy boots and fielddays hat. He comes here every year to see what’s new. Photo: Marika Khabazi

Natasha is wearing an Outback Trading coat. It’s her favourite coat which she bought at Equifest. She comes to learn new things by talking to other people. She been to innovation club which she enjoyed. Photo: Marika Khabazi

Brianna and Zoe. Briana (left) is wearing Swandri jersey. Zoe is wearing a Stoney Creek big dog jacket and Stoney Creek thermal tights which are good for hunting and for keeping her warm. They come to field day for good deals on clothes. Photo: Marika Khabazi

Kelly is wearing an Outback Traders jumper from her favourite field day shop and her favourite Red Bands matching with her daughter's. Photo: Marika Khabazi

Leone Lewis is wearing a field day hat she bought last year. She has a farm so came here to buy new things such as animal supplements. Photo: Marika Khabazi

Janice is wearing Mi Piaci boots as gum boots are cold. Tartan tights because they are comfy and lots of travel is involved. She comes here at least every couple of years to see innovation such distilleries and machinery. Photo: Marika Khabazi

James is wearing Hunters Element and gum boots. He comes here to socialise and try new tools. Photo: Marika Khabazi

For her Fieldays debut, Marika is wearing her work Kathmandu jacket and a dress paired with oversized gum boots. Photo: RNZ
