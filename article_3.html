Youths tear apart roof of justice facility

The group say they're upset about not being let out, while Oranga Tamariki say they have made no specific demands. Audio

Photo: Maia Ingoe/RNZ

A group of young people occupying the rooftop of their youth justice facility could be seen ripping out insulation and wood from a small piece of pulled-up roofing.

The group, on the roof at Korowai Manaaki in south Auckland's Wiri, claimed they were protesting because they weren't let outside. They also said they had not been fed.

Photo: Maia Ingoe/RNZ

Oranga Tamariki Acting deputy chief executive youth justice services and residential care Iain Chapman said a group of 13 youths accessed the roof at around 6:40pm on Monday.

Nine were coaxed down overnight and on Tuesday morning, while four remained. However, an RNZ reporter at the scene said just two could be seen on the roof now.

"We are continuing to work with police to resolve this matter," Chapman said. "We can confirm there are no concerns for public safety."

One young person was led past the front of the building by two guards, with arms behind his back. Another climbed down over a wall, out of sight - but appeared to be off the roof.

Police officers who were earlier walking the perimeter have now gathered in front of the building.

Photo: Stuff/David White

Despite initial reports suggesting the youths were demanding Big Mac combos from McDonald's, cigarettes and a getaway car, Chapman said the group "have made no requests or demands to our staff on site".

A small piece of roofing appeared to have been pulled up and damaged, while two youths appeared to have climbed through a hole made in one of the buildings, before re-emerging.

Police said they were supporting Oranga Tamariki.

Speaking to Newstalk ZB, Prime Minister Christopher Luxon said authorities will not cave to the youths' demands.

"They're not coming down for KFC," Luxon said.

Photo: Maia Ingoe/RNZ

"These are some of the most serious and hardcore young offenders."

It's not the first time young people have climbed on to the roof of Korowai Manaaki, a similar incident in July 2023 was blamed on staff shortages.

A damning report in July found staff at the facility were smuggling in contraband, roughing up children, and silencing any informants.
