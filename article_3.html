'Strong' 4.8 quake shakes South Island, Wellington

The 9km-deep tremor struck near the town of Seddon.

An earthquake near Seddon on 21 March was initially registered as a magnitude 4.6. Photo: Supplied/GeoNet

A magnitude 4.8 quake has rattled the top of the South Island.

The 9 kilometre deep quake caused 'strong' shaking near Seddon about 8.20am on Friday, GeoNet said.

Thousands of people from Wellington to Christchurch reported feeling the quake, mainly describing it as weak or light, with the majority of reports in Marlborough, Nelson, Wellington and Kapiti.
