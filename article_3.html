Live: Rishi Sunak concedes the election to Labour

The Conservative leader and former British Prime Minister says it has been a "difficult night".

Rishi Sunak says the "British people have delivered a sobering verdict tonight". Photo: ADRIAN DENNIS

Conservative leader and former British Prime Minister Rishi Sunak has concedes the UK election to Labour and says it has been a "difficult night".

He has called Labour Party leader Sir Keir Starmer to congratulate him on his victory.

"The British people have delivered a sobering verdict tonight, there is much to learn... and I take responsibility for the loss."

The governing Conservatives are facing major losses to their left and right as Reform surges and Labour cruises to victory.

Labour is expected to win 410 seats with the Conservatives on 144, the BBC reported.

Polls opened at 7am (Thursday 6pm NZ time) and closed at 10pm on Thursday night (9am this morning NZ time).
