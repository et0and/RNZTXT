Ellerslie death: Suspected homicide victim named

The man killed in a suspected homicide in Auckland earlier this week has been named by police.

Police said a scene examination would take two or three days. Photo: RNZ / Finn Blackwell

The man killed in a suspected homicide in Auckland earlier this week has been named by police.

He was 66-year-old Fuk-Fu Joseph Kwok.

His body was found on Celtic Crescent in Ellerslie around 8pm on Monday, he was pronounced dead at the scene.

A post-mortem revealed injuries "consistent with foul play", police said, and that he likely died at the weekend.

Enquiries at the scene and nearby were ongoing.

Police say Mei Han Chong was missing from her house where her husband was found dead on 6 November, 2023. Photo: Supplied / Police

"Police continue to appeal to the public for any information that may assist us with our investigation," Beard said.

They can be contacted via the 105 phone service or online at https://www.police.govt.nz/use-105, using Update My Report, referring to file number 231106/8053.

Kwok's wife, 67-year-old Mei Han Chong, was reported as missing. Police earlier said they were keeping an open mind about her potential involvement in his death, saying she might also be a victim.
