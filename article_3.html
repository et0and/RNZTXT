Live weather: Vehicles stranded in snow, Canterbury residents urged to avoid travel

A wild day of weather has led to flooding in Tararua District and road closures in the South Island.

It has been snowing down to sea level at Pegasus, north of Christchurch. Photo: RNZ/ Sally Murphy

A wild day of weather has led to flooding in Tararua District and road closures on the South Island.

Canterbury Police are urging motorists to consider delaying unnecessary travel this afternoon, with conditions poor around the region.

State Highway 73 is closed from Springfield to Castle Hill, with numerous vehicles reported stuck at Castle Hill and the Lake Lyndon area, police said.

The Tararua District town of Pahiatua has experienced flooding, with the mayor saying it cut off some houses and closed roads.

Other alpine areas are also experiencing heavy snowfall.

Elsewhere across the country there have been reports of surface flooding and snow.

Travellers are urged to use NZTA's Journey Planner before hitting the road in some regions.

Reduced speed limits are also in place on the Auckland Harbour Bridge because of strong gusts of wind.

The Transport Agency said vehicles travelling over the bridge need to take care, especially high-sided ones and motorbikes.

It said there may also be lane reductions and motorists should consider using the Western Ring Road - Highways 16 and 18 - as an alternative route.
