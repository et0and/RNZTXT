Tiwai blaze burns through 1200 hectares of scrub

The land the fire is burning is valuable conservation wetland.

People are being asked to stay clear of the Peninsula and access roads to allow crews to work safely. Photo: Supplied / FENZ

Firefighters are continuing the battle to contain a large vegetation fire burning on Tiwai Peninsula, near Bluff.

The fire, which began on Thursday, was burning over 1200 hectares of scrub and valuable conservation wetland.

Up to 40 firefighters were at the scene on Thursday, as the fire grew in size due to higher temperatures and winds.

Two crews remained on site overnight to monitor the blaze.

Several fire crews will be back on the scene at about 7am on Friday, Fire and Emergency New Zealand confirmed.

The fire poses no immediate threat to people, homes, the Tiwai Point smelter, or the Tiwai Bridge, a spokesperson for Fire and Emergency said.
