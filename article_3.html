Earthquakes shake lower North Island

The 4.3 and 4.2 magnitude quakes were each felt by more than 3700 people.

Two earthquakes have been felt in the lower North Island on Friday night. Photo: Supplied / GeoNet

Two earthquakes in quick succession have shaken the lower North Island.

The first, a 4.3 magnitude quake 10km east of Whanganui, struck at 10:20pm Friday.

It had a depth of 52km.

Two earthquakes in quick succession this evening. The first a M4.3 near Whanganui at 10:20pm and the second a M4.2 at 10.21pm near Taihape. We've received over 3700 felt reports from the shaking. Whanganui M4.3 https://t.co/RpMbLpCWm9 Taihape M4.2 https://t.co/9uMVgzFL1o pic.twitter.com/bYAk07gShe

One minute later, a 4.2 magnitude quake struck 10km north-east of Taihape, at a depth of 31km.

GeoNet said it had received more than 3700 'felt' reports for each.

The earthquakes caused only light shaking, GeoNet said.
