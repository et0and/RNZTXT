Live updates: Kiingi Tuheitia lies in state at Tuurangawaewae

The gates of the marae were now open to the entire country, and will remain so until the tangihanga ends.

Photo: Supplied / Waikato Tainui

At least 13 iwi, including Ngāi Tahu, Ngāpuhi, Mataatua waka and Te Atiawa, will be represented in the first contingent of manuhiri expected at Tuurangawaewae Marae on Monday to pay tribute to Kiingi Tuheitia.

The Māori King, Tuheitia Paki (crowned Te Arikinui Kiingi Tuheitia) has died at the age of 69.

The first pōwhiri for what was expected to be one of the largest groups so far will take place at 8am.

On day three of the tangihanga for Kiingi Tuheitia, guests included the Governor-General, members of the Rātana Church, and iwi including Te Arawa, Ngāti Tūwharetoa, Ngāti Kauwhata and Ngāti Hine.

Mourning will continue at Tuurangawaewae for the next three days.

The gates of the marae were now open to the entire country, and will remain so until the tangihanga ends.

Kiingi Tuheitia's successor was expected to be announced on Thursday.
