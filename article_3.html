Watch live: Coalition confirms $24m for Gumboot Friday

The move comes a week ahead of Budget 2024 and meets a commitment set out in coalition agreements.

The coalition government has confirmed this year's Budget will include a $24 million spend over four years for the I Am Hope initiative Gumboot Friday, providing counselling to young people.

Mental health campaigner Mike King joined Deputy Prime Minister Winston Peters and Mental Health Minister Matt Doocey to confirm the decision at Parliament on Wednesday morning.

Peters said the money would enable the charity to provide access to free mental health counselling services for more than 15,000 young New Zealanders per year.

Doocey said prevention and early intervention were a critical part of dealing with mental health, and Gumboot Friday played a vital role in enabling young people with mild to moderate mental health needs access to counselling services earlier.

King said every cent would be reported and accounted for.

"Every single cent, $24 million, is going directly to councellors. Mike King is not putting a single cent in his pocket, I Am Hope Foundation is not putting a signle cent in its pocket, this is about the kids," he said.
