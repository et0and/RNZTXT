Sprinter Danielle Aitchison claims Paralympics silver

It's NZ's second medal of the Games, adding to cyclist Anna Taylor's silver.

Danielle Aitchison in Paris in 2023. Photo: AFP / Hervio Jean-Marie

Sprinter Danielle Aitchison has claimed a silver medal in the women's 200m T36 final at the Paris Paralympic Games.

It was the third Paralympic medal of the 23-year-old's career and New Zealand's second silver at these Games.

Cyclist Anna Taylor clinched the first medal for New Zealand earlier in the week.

Aitchison, who is based in Hamilton, earlier broke the 200m Paralympic record in the qualifiers, with a time of 28.21 seconds.

However, Chinese sprinter Yiting Shi went one better in the final, clocking 27.50 seconds to take the gold.

Aitchison was just 0.14 seconds behind.

"It was definitely a hard race, I felt pretty strong coming out of the blocks and on the home straight I was neck-and-neck with Shi, it was a serious race," she said.

"I was a bit disappointed with the last five metres and got really fatigued.

"I'm feeling tired - I had a late night (after the evening heats) and didn't get to bed until this morning. I am happy with the time, which was great."

Aitchison, who has cerebral palsy, has been coached by Alan McDonald in 2018.

She won a silver medal in the women's 200m T36 and bronze in the women's 100m T36 at the Tokyo Paralympics in 2021.

Aitchison and Shi, the current world champion, are long-time rivals, with both frequently placing on the podium and besting each other's record times at international meets.
