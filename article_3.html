Seymour 'achieved what I wanted' in 10-minute meeting

ACT leader David Seymour has met with New Zealand First leader Winston Peters for another 10-minute meeting at NZ First's temporary offices.

ACT leader David Seymour leaves NZ First's offices after a brief meeting with Winston Peters. Photo: RNZ / Craig McCulloch

ACT leader David Seymour has met with New Zealand First leader Winston Peters for another 10-minute meeting at NZ First's temporary offices.

Emerging from the meeting, he told reporters not to read too much into the short timeframe, saying he had achieved what he wanted to.

On Thursday, Peters had gone to ACT's headquarters at Parliament for a 10-minute meeting.

As he arrived at his own party's offices on Friday he said nothing to waiting reporters, but NZ First second-in-command Shane Jones said he personally was "not involved in any three-way assignations today".

"Have patience, it's all in the Bible," he said.

Policy discussions were all under a cone of silence, he said.

"It'll soon be revealed, I know people may be anxious. Yesterday I said Shakespeare, today it's in the Bible."
