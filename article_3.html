Aesha Scott, Paddy Gower and The Gone big winners at 2023 NZ TV Awards

The great and the good of Aotearoa's small screen industry have been recognised at the 2023 New Zealand Television Awards.

New Zealand superyacht stewardess Aesha Scott stars in the Bravo reality series Below Deck Photo: Maree Wilkinson Photography

The Kiwi star of a reality show about life on luxury superyachts has been named Personality of the Year at the 2023 New Zealand Television Awards - Ngā Taonga Whakaata O Aotearoa.

Aesha Scott, who stars in Below Deck Down Under, won the publicly-voted award, which was announced at a glittering event at Auckland's Viaduct Events Centre on Tuesday night.

Earlier this year, Scott told Kim Hill that delivering five-star service with TV cameras around 24/7 was tough.

"It's not all fun and games but I'm determined to keep doing it as long as my sanity will let me," she said.

Nearly 40 awards were announced during the night, recognising excellence in Aotearoa's TV industry across news and entertainment.

1News took out the award for Best News Coverage for their reportage on the impact of Cyclone Gabrielle, and 1News' Thomas Mead was named Reporter of the Year.

Patrick Gower won Best Presenter: News and Current Affairs for his work across Newshub Live at 6pm, Paddy Gower Has Issues, AM, The Project NZ and Paddy Gower On…. His co-star Karen O'Leary won Best Presenter: Entertainment for her work on Paddy Gower Has Issues.

Sunday was named Best Current Affairs Programme and Taskmaster NZ won Best Entertainment Programme.

Antonia Prebble was named Best Actress for her role in Double Parked, and Dominic Ona-Ariki was named Best Actor for his work in One Lane Bridge.

Crime drama The Gone won five awards including Best Drama, Best Cinematography (Drama/Comedy Drama), Best Original Score, Best Costume Design and Best Makeup Design.

Princess of Chaos, based on the 2013 political scandal that erupted when then-mayor of Auckland Len Brown was discovered to have had an affair with council advisor Bevan Chuang, won awards for Best Editing (Drama/Comedy Drama), Best Post Production Design and Best Director (Drama/Comedy Drama).

NZ Wars, Stories of Wairau, which was produced for RNZ by the Aotearoa Media Collective and Great Southern Television was named Best Māori Programme.

Oscar Kightley was honoured as the 2023 TV Legend.
