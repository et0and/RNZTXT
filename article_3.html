Live: Ex-tropical cyclone Alfred downgraded to 'tropical low'

Violent winds have toppled power lines as Alfred inches towards Australia's eastern coast.

Photo: AFP/DAVID GRAY

Ex-tropical cyclone Alfred has been downgraded to a 'tropical low', according to Australia's Bureau of Meteorology.

Violent winds have toppled power lines as Alfred inches towards Australia's eastern coast, swelling rivers, sparking evacuation orders and leaving about 39,000 homes without electricity.

It crossed the islands off the coast of Queensland overnight, crawling towards the densely populated coastline at "walking speed", government forecasts said.

No deaths have been reported, but the impacts are already being felt across southeast Queensland and northern New South Wales.

Emergency services were searching Friday night for a man who has been washed into floodwaters in northern New South Wales.

Families have stocked up on essential items and have boarded their homes.

More than 60 disaster-response personnel from New Zealand have headed to Australia to assist with the cyclone response.

Gold Coast Pasifika community member Oketi Laurenson told Pacific Waves that the Pasifika community on Gold Coast are feeling anxious.
