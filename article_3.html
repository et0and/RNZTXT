Councillor stripped of roles after revealing confidential plan

Queenstown's Niki Gladding revealed a plan to discharge treated sewage into the Shotover River.

Member of Aotearoa Water Action as well as the Queenstown Lakes district councillor Niki Gladding. Photo: Facebook/Niki Gladding

A Queenstown councillor who revealed a confidential council plan to discharge treated sewage into the Shotover River has been stripped of her responsibilities.

Last week, Niki Gladding revealed the council was planning to invoke emergency powers to pump at least 12,000 cubic metres of treated sewage into the Shotover River per day.

An extraordinary meeting was called for this afternoon regarding concerns she had breached the council's Code of Conduct

During the meeting, Gladding said it was unfair for the council not to follow due process.

The vote was not unanimous, but the council voted to strip Gladding of her committee roles.
