Injunction served on council to stop Māori ward vote

Protesters gathered outside the district council buildings where a meeting will take place to decide the fate of its Māori ward.

A protest in Kaipara as Te Rūnanga o Ngāti Whātua served the Kaipara District Council with an injunction to stop a planned vote on Maori wards. Photo:

Te Rūnanga o Ngāti Whātua have served the Kaipara District Council with an injunction in an attempt to stop the planned vote on the future of Māori wards.

Kaipara District Council is holding an extraordinary meeting to decide the fate of its Māori ward.

Te Rūnanga o Ngāti Whātua representative Deborah Nathan told people gathered outside the council building in in Mangawhai that Rūnanga legal representatives had served the council with an injunction on Wednesday morning.

A protest in Kaipara as Te Rūnanga o Ngāti Whātua served the Kaipara District Council with an injunction to stop a planned vote on Maori wards. Photo: RNZ

Councils have until 6 September to decide whether to drop the wards. If they don't reach a decision, a binding referendum must be held alongside the election.

Kaipara Mayor Craig Jepson has been called to resign by iwi groups over his stance to remove the Te Moananui o Kaipara Māori ward, currently occupied by councillor Pera Paniora.

If the ward was disestablished, it would be the first under the new rules.
