Paul beats Tyson in flat fight

While it was never going to live up to the hype, few predicted that Tyson would be so subdued, landing just 18 punches.

Former US heavyweight boxing champion Mike Tyson and US YouTuber and boxer Jake Paul face off ahead of their November fight in Texas. Photo: TIMOTHY A. CLARY/AFP

Jake Paul has beaten boxing legend Mike Tyson by unanimous decision to win an intergenerational heavyweight battle in Texas that failed to live up to its enormous hype.

With one scoring it 80-72, and two judges giving it 79-72 to Paul, it was a relatively easy win for the much maligned former child star.

The bout between the 27-year-old social media influencer-turned-prizefighter and the 58-year-old former heavyweight champion was streamed live on Netflix and played out in front of a sold-out crowd at AT&T Stadium in Arlington.

While it was never going to live up to the hype, few predicted that Tyson would be so subdued, landing just 18 punches.

Paul dominated the former heavyweight champ, using his fitness to bounce in and out of range at will, while peppering Tyson with stiff shots.

After appearing strong in the opening round, Tyson faded fast and was soon being toyed with by the man almost three decades his junior.

With an audience of a quarter billion expected to watch, fans were left frustrated with glitches and buffering.

Netflix was down for thousands of users in the United States, outage tracking website Downdetector.com said, just as viewers tuned in.

After hs loss, Tyson said it was time for the public to start respecting Paul.

He would not rule out a return to the ring, suggesting a fight with Jake's brother Logan Paul.

The pair buried any animosity and Paul said Tyson would forever be a legend of the sport.

"This man is an icon and it was an honour to fight him."

-Reuters/RNZ
