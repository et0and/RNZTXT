Dame Tariana Turia to be laid to rest today

Dame Tariana died on Friday, aged 80.

Dame Tariana Turia Photo: RNZ

The founder of Te Pāti Māori Dame Tariana Turia will be laid to rest at her marae at Whangaehu today.

Dame Tariana died on Friday, aged 80, after earlier suffering a stroke.

Thousands have arrived in the small settlement south of Whanganui over the last four days to pay their respects.

Whānau spokesperson Che Wilson said Tuesday's proceedings would begin with karakia followed by a church service to send off the woman affectionately known among her iwi as Auntie Tari.

The nehu, or burial, was expected to take place around 11am.

Wilson said the whānau were encouraging people to stay behind after the nehu for the hākari, or feast, which usually signaled the end of a tangihanga.

He said the village of Whangaehu may be small, but the entire Māori community on both sides of the Whanganui river had come together to farewell one of their own.

"You can't be from one side of a river and not from the other side of a river, or connected to the many rivers along here, so ... as a wider rohe (region) we're supporting each other to ensure we can do the things we need to do to honour our Queen."
