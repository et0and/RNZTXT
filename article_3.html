Hundreds evacuated as Indonesia volcano erupts

An ash column went 2.5km into the sky as Mount Ruang continued erupting for a second day.

Lava and smoke spews out of Mount Ruang as seen from Sitaro, North Sulawesi, Indonesia. Photo: Handout / Center for Volcanology and Geological Hazard Mitigation / AFP

Hundreds of people have been evacuated after Indonesia's Mount Ruang erupted for the second day.

Indonesia's volcanology agency said the eruptions were triggered by recent earthquakes in the area.

There were no reports of deaths or injuries, state news agency Antara reported.

The country's geological agency said the eruption pushed an ash column of up to 2.5km into the sky.
