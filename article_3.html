Live: Crowds arrive to pay respects to Kiingi Tuheitia

Many have arrived at Tuurangawaewae Marae to mourn the loss of the Māori king, including Police Commissioner Andrew Coster.

Visitors, including the Police Commissioner Andrew Coster, being welcomed onto Tuurangawaewae Marae on the second day of the tangi for Kiingi Tuheitia. Photo: Screenshot / Kiingitanga

Thousands are expected to pay their respects to Kiingi Tuheitia at Tuurangawaewae Marae on Saturday.

The Māori king died in hospital in the early hours of Friday morning after heart surgery.

His body was then taken to the marae, where it will lie in state for at least five days before being buried on Taupiri mountain.

Police Commissioner Andrew Coster has been seen paying his respects, while Prime Minister Christopher Luxon was expected to do so later on Saturday afternoon.

Tainui would be looking after the kiingitanga over the next few days, with the new monarch likely to be crowned on the final day of Kiingi Tuheitia's tangi.
