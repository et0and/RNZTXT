Cats rescued from blaze at Wellington vet and cattery given oxygen

The owner of a Wellington vet centre and cattery that was gutted by fire on Saturday night says emergency crews did an amazing job helping to rescue as many cats as possible.

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper says he does not know how many cats have survived or died in the blaze as they have not been able to get inside the building, which also contains the businesses paperwork. Photo: RNZ / Rachel Helyer-Donaldson

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper says at this stage he does not know how many cats have survived or died in the blaze.

The owner of a Wellington vet centre and cattery that was gutted by fire on Saturday night says emergency crews did an amazing job helping to rescue as many cats as possible.

Emergency services were called to the fire at the two storey building on Mahora Street in Kilbirnie at 11.50pm. It took more than two hours for firefighters to put the blaze out.

CareVets vet clinic and cattery in the Wellington suburb of Kilbirnie has been gutted by fire. Photo: RNZ / Rachel Helyer-Donaldson

CareVets Kilbirnie owner Nick Cooper said firefighters had entered the cattery to get the animals out.

There had been about ten cats at the clinic the night of the fire, but staff don't know the exact number has they had not been able to get into the building where the computers and paperwork are, he said.

Some of the cats that were pulled out of the burning building were given treatment at the scene, before being taken to they city's after-hours veterinary clinic.

"Our nurses put a couple of cats on oxygen straight away - the ambulance helped us, and they've recovered, and they've gone to our other clinic now," Cooper said.

There are two apartments above the vet clinic, and at least six people were in the building the night of the fire, he said, but everyone got out safely.

Cooper said he and his staff had been on site since 3am on Sunday, and he praised everyone who had helped respond: "Wellington After Hours Vet, Wellington Free [Ambulance], the fire service, all fantastic, absolutely - and my team of course,"

He expected that once he is able to get into the building, he would know more how many of the cats are accounted for.

FENZ central shift manager Murray Dunbar said eight appliances and four specialist support vehicles attended the fire, which was put out around 2.30am.

The fire at CareVets Kilbirnie would be investigated, he said.
