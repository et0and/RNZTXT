Police investigating alleged 'inappropriate' touching of student on bus

Police say the student was indecently assaulted on an Auckland bus.

Police were seeking the public's assistance in identifying a man following an indecent assault aboard a bus in Grey Lynn. Photo: NZ Police

Police have received information in their investigation into an indecent assault of a high school student while on a bus in Auckland.

"We acknowledge the public for a very swift response to our appeal this afternoon," police said.

Police said they were no longer seeking information on a man's identity.

Earlier on Wednesday, police sought help from the public to identify a man after a high school student was allegedly indecently assaulted on an Auckland bus

The alleged assault happened just after 8am on bus 18 travelling Great North Rd in the suburb of Grey Lynn, police said.

Detective Senior Sergeant Martin Friend said a man got on the bus at a stop near King St.

"He has sat down next to a high school student and touched her inappropriately," Friend said.

"This was witnessed by a passenger who quickly intervened and stopped what was taking place."

The man was told to get off the bus and exited at a stop near the intersection with Maidstone St.

"The witness did an incredible job in coming to the assistance of the student," Friend said.

The witness and another passenger supported the girl, walking her safely to school, he said.

Appropriate welfare support is being put in place for the student.

"Our witness has also obtained a photo of the man we would like to speak with, which Police is releasing to the public," Friend said.

He said the police are confident the public will know who the man is.

Police ask anyone with information to get in contact. You can update police online now or call 105 using the reference number 250312/9531.

Information can also be provided anonymously via Crime Stoppers on 0800 555 111.
