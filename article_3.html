1000 protesters at Parliament call for immediate and permanent ceasefire in Gaza

Multiple groups are behind today's demonstration, including Palestine Solidarity Network Aotearoa and Justice for Palestine.

Protesters are calling for an immediate and permanent ceasefire in Gaza. Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Around 1000 people on Parliament's front lawn are calling on the government to demand an immediate and permanent ceasefire in Gaza.

A woman who spent the first 12 years of her life there addressed the crowd.

She said Gaza used to be like Wellington, full of love and light.

Te Pāti Māori co-leader Debbie Ngarewa-Packer and Green Party co-leader Marama Davidson with someone speaking at the rally. Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Now her old home is rubble and her family left behind being bombed daily, she said.

Multiple groups are behind today's demonstration, including Palestine Solidarity Network Aotearoa and Justice for Palestine.

They say more than 10,000 children and infants have been killed since the start of the attack on the Gaza Strip on 7 October - the equivalent of 36 average sized New Zealand primary schools.

More than a thousand protesters sung waiata to close the demonstration.

Samira Zaitoun from one of the groups behind the protest, Justice for Palestine, told the crowd songs could express emotions that could not be put into words.

The singing was followed by further chants of "Free, free Palestine".
