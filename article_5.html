Bid to stop Wellington Airport share sale fails

An eleventh-hour attempt by three Wellington City Councillors to stop the sale of the council's airport shares has failed.

Photo: Ruth Hill/RNZ

An eleventh-hour attempt by three Wellington City Councillors to stop the sale of the council's airport shares has failed.

The attempt was made during the final vote on the capital's long-term plan.

Councillor Nikau Wi Neera had tried to table an amendment to the plan calling for a full investigation of retaining ownership of the council's holding in Wellington International Airport Ltd.

But after consulting with council officials, mayor Tory Whanau ruled out a vote on the amendment, saying the decision was the result of lengthy consultation.

Her ruling was in turn challenged - half the 16-person council voted against it - but that did not meet the required 75 percent threshold.
