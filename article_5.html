Government unveils plans to simplify lending rules

Following through on election promises, the Consumer Affairs Minister has unveiled plans for the finance sector.

Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

The Government plans to simplify consumer credit rules and regulation of the financial sector.

Consumer Affairs Minister Andrew Bayly said the current rules for consumer lending were too complicated and had resulted in creditworthy people being denied loans.

In a speech to the Financial Services Council on Wednesday morning, Bayly said the criteria for lower risk lending would be relaxed as a priority, with a more detailed review of penalties and disclosure rules.

The Financial Markets Authority will take over responsibility for consumer finance laws from the Commerce Commission, he said.

Among other planned changes are streamlining the regulation of financial service companies to remove duplication and lower compliance costs.

"These changes are not about lessening requirements for appropriate conduct.

"It's about ensuring that financial institutions have clear guidance from the FMA, and take responsibility to meet their obligations more effectively without compromising on conduct standards."

Bayly also said the Government planned to review the Companies Act and Kiwisaver settings.

"The Companies Act is an important piece of legislation... But it's now 30 years old. I think aspects of it are out of date and could be improved."

He said that was a longer term project.
