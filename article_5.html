Live: 'This has really just killed Wellington': Local MP on job cuts

With nearly 5000 public sector jobs being axed so far, and more to come, Tamatha Paul says the capital is feeling it.

Tamatha Paul. Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

Wellington Central MP Tamatha Paul says public service job cuts are causing carnage in the capital.

RNZ has calculated nearly 5000 roles have been axed, or are proposed to go.

And the government is not ruling out further job losses beyond what it calls its baseline savings exercise - directing ministries to save up to 7-and-a-half percent of their budgets.

National's pledge to fund 13 cancer treatments from this year is looking increasingly shaky - with numerous ministers saying they cannot do everything in one Budget.
