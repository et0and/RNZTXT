Army gets first woman chief as new Defence Force leadership announced

Commodore Garin Golding will be promoted to Rear Admiral and Brigadier Rose King to Major General - the first ever woman in that role.

Air Vice-Marshal Darryn 'Digby' Webb, Major General Rose King and Rear Admiral Garin Golding, heads of the airforce, army, and navy. Photo: Craig McCulloch/RNZ

The government has unveiled its Defence Force leadership with new chiefs for the navy and army.

Brigadier Rose King will be promoted to Major General - the first woman appointed in that role - and Commodore Garin Golding will become Rear Admiral.

Air Vice-Marshal Darryn 'Digby' Webb had been air force chief since last year and would continue in the role - with all three being appointing for three years from 27 August.

They report to Defence Force Chief Air Marshal Tony Davies.

In a statement, Defence Minister Judith Collins said she looked forward to working closely with all three.

"These accomplished leaders are the New Zealand Defence Force's most senior officers in their service, bringing a wealth of experience and strategic insight and being dedicated to serving our country," she said.

Commodore Golding had been NZDF maritime component commander since 2021.

Brigadier King has been acting chief of army since June. From 2022 to 2024 she was the deputy chief of army.

Webb had been chief of air force since 2023. From 2021 to 2023 he was assistant chief of defence - strategic commitments and engagements.

He was perhaps most well known to New Zealanders for his role during the Covid-19 pandemic overseeing the quarantine facilities.
