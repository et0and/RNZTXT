Auckland man due in court after several people injured in vehicle collision

The 22-year-old is facing at least seven charges after a vehicle collision in Half Moon Bay which saw numerous people hospitalised.

Photo: 123rf.com

A man is facing at least seven charges after a vehicle collision in Auckland which saw numerous people hospitalised.

In a statement, police said the incident occurred on Prince Regent Drive in Half Moon Bay around 5.30pm on Sunday.

Those hospitalised as a result of the vehicle collision remain in hospital, police said.

They did not specify how many people were in hospital.

The 22-year-old arrested in relation to the incident was expected to appear in Manukau District Court on Monday.

"The man arrested by police at the time is so far facing seven charges, including wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm and injures with intent to cause grievous bodily harm.

"Police understand this incident has caused great concern amongst the community, and we are continuing to investigate the circumstances of what took place."

Police acknowledged the witnesses who had assisted in the investigation for far, and was asking anyone else who may have seen it to get in touch.

People can call police on 105 or go online using 'Update My Report'. The file number is 231210/5003.
