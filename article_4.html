Sublime with Rome will visit NZ one last time

The ska-punk band have announced new NZ dates after being forced to postpone last summer, amid news they were calling it quits.

The ska-punk band announced they are calling it a day at the end of 2024. Photo: Supplied

Californian ska-punk rockers Sublime with Rome are heading to New Zealand this summer for their last ever world tour.

The band, who were forced to postpone last year, kick off a five-date New Zealand tour at Rock the Bowl, at the Bowl of Brooklands in New Plymouth on 29 December.

Joining them on the bill at The Bowl is monster electronic act, Pendulum, who will close the festival on the main stage with their infamous DJ set.

Local acts Home Brew, Ladyhawke and Elemeno P are also named for the New Plymouth show.

Sublime with Rome have announced five last shows for NZ. Photo: Supplied

Sublime with Rome will head to Coroglen, near Whitianga, on 30 December, with support from the Flaxxies.

They'll see in the new year at Brew Year's Eve - Brewtown in Upper Hutt on 31 December, before starting the new year off at Black Barn in Havelock North on 2 January.

Finally they will play at Bay Park Arena at Mount Maunganui on 3 January- both dates will have Elemeno P in support.

In 2023, Sublime with Rome frontman Rome Ramirez shared his "absolute displeasure" in postponing their new Zealand shows over summer due to "unforeseen circumstances". It came amid news the band were calling it quits at the end of 2024.

A statement from Ramirez to Billboard at the time read: "After almost two remarkable decades, I am announcing my departure from Sublime with Rome at the close of 2024. The upcoming performances over the next year will allow us to reflect on countless incredible memories together!"
