Junior doctors strike for second time in two weeks

Emergency departments remain open but some appointments have been cancelled and the patients notified.

Junior doctors on strike earlier in May. Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

Surgery and appointments are being called off around the country as junior doctors go on strike again on Thursday morning.

Junior doctors were walking off the job at 7am, for the second time in two weeks - this time for two days.

In the earlier one-day strike, hundreds of procedures and clinics were unable to take place.

Te Whatu Ora said it had already postponed some complex procedures to try and ease pressure on hospitals.

But it said emergency departments were open and people should turn up for appointments unless they've heard otherwise.

The doctors said they were protesting against pay cuts for about 600 colleagues in specialities with big staff shortages.
