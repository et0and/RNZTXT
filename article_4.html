6.7 magnitude quake strikes Macquarie Island region

No tsunami alert has been issued after the earthquake which was 10km deep.

The blue dot symbolises where the earthquake hit. Photo: US Geological Survey

A 6.7 magnitude earthquake has struck in the Macquarie Island region, southwest of New Zealand.

The US Geological Survey reports the quake was 10 kilometres deep.

No tsunami alert has been issued.

There are no permanent inhabitants on Macquarie Island, although the Australian Antarctic Division station is occupied year round.
