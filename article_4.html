Man in custody after threatening staff with gun at Auckland shop

Police remain on Wood Street and the surrounding areas.

Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

A person has been taken into custody after threatening staff with a gun at a shop in South Auckland.

Police had made enquiries to find the man in Papakura before a person matching the description was sighted back at the same premises on Wood Street around two hours later.

The man was found and taken into custody just after 5.30pm.

A Foodstuffs spokesperson said the offender was apprehended at New World Papakura but they believed the incident may have taken place at a nearby business.

No staff or member of the public at New World Papakura was threatened, they said.

The supermarket was evacuated for about 20 minutes.

Police remain on Wood Street and the surrounding areas.

They say there is no ongoing risk to the public.
