One house evacuated in Auckland bush fire

Several firetrucks and two helicopters are responding to a large vegetation fire burning through bush in a remote part of southeast Auckland.

Fire in Kawakawa Bay in Auckland on 30 March, 2024. Photo: Supplied / Jazmyn Meiklejohn

One home has been evacuated as a 600-metre-wide vegetation fire rips through a remote part of southeast Auckland.

Fire and Emergency said it responded to the blaze in Kawakawa Bay just after 1pm with multiple fire trucks, water tankers, and two helicopters.

The agency said they are working to control the fire near Raukura Point and police said they have helped evacuate one house in the area.

A witness near the fire, Lucan McArdle, said it appears to have started on what had been a stand of pines.

"When we first arrived we could see the fire actually happening.

"It was on the stumps from the old pine trees that were cut down a few years ago. Those were kind of what was on fire, and yeah, it would have been all the pine needles which have gone up."

McArdle said it looks like the worst of the blaze is over.
