Suspicious blaze destroys derelict building in Levin

Several fire crews have attended a fire in an abandoned building which has been destroyed in the blaze in the town centre.

Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

Several fire crews have attended a fire in an abandoned building which has been destroyed in the blaze in the Levin town centre.

Fire and Emergency said it was alerted to the fire just before 4am today. Crews found a two-storey building on Bath Street well involved when they arrived.

A spokesperson said, at its peak, six fire appliances, a ladder truck, and a command unit attended from six fire stations.

Now two fire trucks are attending and one crew will be present for much of the day.

The spokesperson said the building was scheduled for demolition and the fire was being treated as suspicious.
