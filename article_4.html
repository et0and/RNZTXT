Man who killed butcher in head-on crash sentenced for manslaughter

The man's blood alcohol level was more than double the legal limit, he had taken MDMA, and was driving recklessly the morning after.

Photo: RNZ / Dan Cook

An Otago man who killed Royalburn Station's head butcher in a head on crash has been sentenced to three years and eleven months in prison in the Invercargill High Court.

Hayden Richard Hurst, 29, pleaded guilty to manslaughter after he hit Outrega Anderson's car of State Highway 6 near Gibbston in December last year.

Justice Lisa Preston says Hurst's blood alcohol level was more than double the legal limit and he had taken MDMA during a work Christmas party in Queenstown, and he was driving recklessly the morning after.

Hurst was also disqualified from driving for four years after release and ordered to pay Anderson's family $10,000 in reparation.

Anderson's family says it was a senseless act that left them with a true life sentence, with overwhelming and unbearable grief some days.

They describe Anderson as a beautiful, kind and compassionate soul.
