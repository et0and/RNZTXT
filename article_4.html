Early childhood centre seeks to overturn move to cancel licence

The Early Childhood Council spoke out against the ministry move as heavy handed.

Country Kindy will remain open for now after court action against its licence cancellation was filed. Photo: RNZ / Jimmy Ellingham

A rural ManawatÅ« early childhood centre facing closure over alleged curriculum violations will remain open for now after court action was filed, RNZ understands.

Country Kindy, between Feilding and Palmerston North, was to have its licence cancelled by the Ministry of Education from 5pm on Monday.

However, it is understood its doors will remain open after court action has been taken seeking to overturn the ministry's move.

The ministry said the centre was issued a provisional licence last year but failed to comply with some of the conditions of this.

Last week, however, the Early Childhood Council, which represents more than 1300 early childhood centres, spoke out against the move as heavy handed.

Chief executive Simon Laube last week said the council was working frantically on extending that timeframe.

The centre has 22 children enrolled.

Laube last week said if the closure went ahead those children would have to find other early childhood centres to attend.

A well-attended public meeting showed there was community support for Country Kindy.

While it was disputed that there were curriculum violations, Laube believed if there were, the ministry should work with Country Kindy to fix such problems rather than cancelling its licence.

The Ministry of Education last week said Country Kindy was placed on a provisional licence in September because of 17 "non compliances" with early childhood regulations and rules.

Since then Country Kindy has failed to comply with seven conditions of the provisional licence, including "curriculum and governance" and "management criteria and regulations".
