Around 15,000 people expected at NZ Trotting Cup at Addington

Crowds are flocking into Christchurch's Addington Raceway for the country's premier harness racing event.

From left, Maree Hurst, Debbie Handisides and Marian Lippiatt set for a "girls' day out". Photo: RNZ / Adam Burns

Crowds are flocking into Christchurch's Addington Raceway for the country's premier harness racing event.

The New Zealand Trotting Cup is one of the Garden City's biggest annual sporting and social events, with a crowd of about 15,000 expected at Addington on Tuesday.

Alongside an afternoon of racing, fashion and entertainment are major features of the day.

Revellers can enjoy the silent disco that will run throughout the day and the Crossing Cup Fashion marquee will again be a popular showcase for the more stylish of patrons.

Patrons in the event green space. Photo: RNZ / Adam Burns

Debbie Handisides said it was her fourth time at the races.

"I'm pumped," she said.

"Haven't been for a few years and I'm with great company.

"I'm just here to have a great time, be together ... it's a girl's day but also have a look at everyone else having a good time and win some money."

A diverse range of attire on show among this group of patrons. Photo: RNZ / Adam Burns

The running of the 121st New Zealand Cup is expected at about 5.45pm.

It is also the first time in 15 years that the Cup will be worth $1 million.

This is the first time the country's two biggest pacing and trotting races, the New Zealand Cup and the $400,000 Renwick Farms Dominion Trot are to be held on the same day.

Both will be raced over 3200 metres.

Previously, the Dominion was held on Show Day, during the Friday of Cup week.
