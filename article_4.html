Prisoner seriously injured by fire in cell at Spring Hill Corrections

A prisoner was taken to hospital in a serious condition due to smoke inhalation from the fire.

Photo: Corrections / Chai Patel

A man is in a serious condition after a fire in his prison cell at Spring Hill Corrections Facility in the Waikato.

The corrections facility's acting general manager Alan Lamb said the fire happened at 4.45pm on Friday, but for "security reasons", he could not say how it started.

The prison's sprinkler system activated and extinguished the fire, Lamb said, and staff responded immediately to remove the man from his cell.

"There was no threat to the wider security of the prison," he said.

Due to smoke inhalation an ambulance was called for the man, Lamb confirmed, and he was taken to hospital.

No other staff or prisoners were impacted, he said.

"The prisoner who started the fire will face internal misconduct charges."

St John confirmed it responded to the area on Friday afternoon and took a person with serious injuries to Waikato Hospital.

Fire and Emergency northern shift manager Hayley Prince said they sent three fire trucks to the facility at 5:12pm.
