Man pleads not guilty to murdering Dunedin's Gurjit Singh

The 33-year-old technician appeared in Dunedin High Court via audio-visual link on Tuesday morning, facing one charge of murder.

Police at the scene of the homicide investigation on Hillary Street in Liberton. Photo: RNZ / Tess Brunton

The man accused of murdering Dunedin man Gurjit Singh has pleaded not guilty.

The 27-year-old's body was found at his home late last month.

His death was initially treated as unexplained, but it became a homicide investigation after forensic evidence indicated he died from multiple stab wounds by a sharp weapon.

The 33-year-old technician appeared in Dunedin High Court via audio-visual link on Tuesday morning, facing one charge of murder.

He has been remanded in custody until 30 April with interim name suppression.

The maximum penalty is life in prison.
