Severe thunderstorm watch covers much of North Island

The watch issued by MetService extends from Waikato down to Taihape.

Photo: Romolo Tavani / 123RF

A severe thunderstorm watch is in place for much of the North Island today.

MetService is warning scattered heavy showers and thunderstorms are expected from 2pm until 8pm.

Watches are in place for Waikato, Waitomo, Taumaraunui, and Bay of Plenty.

Some thunderstorms might be slow moving and produce localised downpours of up to 40mm of rainfall, MetService says.

It has also issued a severe thunderstorm watch for Rotorua, Taupō, Taranaki, and Taihape.

It says driving conditions may be hazardous, with surface flooding and poor visibility in heavy rain.

Meanwhile, New Zealand avoided extreme temperatures seen globally for the month of January, but Niwa's principal scientist says that should change in the coming months.

Last month was the world's warmest on record, according to the EU's Copernicus Climate Change Service.

There was a 1.75 degrees Celsius increase globally, but regional variants meant New Zealand instead had its coldest January since 2017.
