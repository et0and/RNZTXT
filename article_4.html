Rally under way over TVNZ's job cut plans

E tū union is holding the rally for journalists and supporters, who have been asked to wear the colour orange.

'Sunday' presenter Miriama Kamo speaks at a rally outside TVNZ headquarters in Auckland as part of a campaign protesting the broadcaster's planned job cuts. Photo: RNZ / MARIKA KHABAZI

A rally is under way outside TVNZ headquarters in Auckland as part of a campaign protesting the broadcaster's plans to axe programmes and cut jobs.

Earlier this month TVNZ announced plans to cut up to 68 jobs - and scrap several long-running shows, including Fair Go and Sunday.

E tū union is holding the rally for journalists and supporters, who have been asked to wear the colour orange.

Those attending the 'Save Our Stories'-themed rally were asked to wear orange. Photo: RNZ / MARIKA KHABAZI

The theme of the rally is 'Save Our Stories' and speakers from impacted TVNZ shows will speak to the crowd.

E tū says an open letter with over 11,000 signatures will be presented to TVNZ's chief executive and board urging them not to proceed with the cuts.
