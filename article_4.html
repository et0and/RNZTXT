Fisherman lucky to be alive after rescue, livesavers say

The two-metre swells and incoming tide were too extreme to get a rescue boat close enough, but swimmers and a jetski were able to step in.

Bethells Beach. Photo: RNZ / Nick Monro

Surf Life Saving says two fishermen are lucky to be alive after a difficult rescue at Auckland's Bethells Beach on Sunday.

A fisherman was swept off the rocks at O'Neill's Bay just before 7.30pm.

Two others were able to bring the unconscious man back onto the rocks, and one went to the beach to call for help.

A spokesperson for Surf Life Saving said the two-metre swells and incoming tide were too extreme to get a rescue boat close enough, but rescue swimmers and a jetski were able to save the two remaining fishermen.

They said the men were lucky to survive considering they were not wearing lifejackets.
