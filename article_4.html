Ten whales stranded at Farewell Spit in Golden Bay

Two of the whales have died but the rest are still alive after becoming stranded.

The latest stranding of whales at Farewell Spit. Photo: Facebook / Project Jonah

Ten pilot whales have stranded at Farewell Spit in Golden Bay.

Eight were still alive after becoming stranded on Tuesday.

Department of Conservation said it would aim to refloat the whales at high tide on Wednesday afternoon.

Golden Bay Operations Manager Ross Trotter says he's unsure if it's the same pod that beached at the spit a month ago, but says it's clear this is not the pod's first stranding.

"They are showing signs of having been sunburnt and have healed over, so possibly in the last few weeks they have been beached somewhere else."

Trotter said there were plenty of volunteers keeping the whales cool, with the aim to refloat the pod around high tide this afternoon.

A pod of pilot whales was refloated several times in December.

Not all of them survived.

Among those that did, some were seen in the area even after being refloated again.
