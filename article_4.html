TVNZ looks to axe award-winning shows

The Sunday programme, the long-running consumer affairs show Fair Go and several news bulletins may be axed in the proposed restructuring, sources say.

Miriama Kamo. Photo: RNZ / Luke McPake

TVNZ is proposing to axe its long-running current affairs programme Sunday, hosted by veteran broadcaster Miriama Kamo.

It is part of plans to cut dozens of jobs at the broadcaster.

Staff are learning which programmes are affected at a series of meetings on Friday.

TVNZ said a proposal had been presented to Sunday staff which could result in cancellation of the programme.

RNZ understands the 1News Midday and Tonight bulletins may also be canned, and consumer affairs programme Fair Go could to be cut too.

A hui for all news and current affairs staff is due to be held at one this afternoon, following the individual programme meetings.

Fair Go is one of New Zealand's longest running and most popular television series.

The consumer affairs show, which investigates complaints from viewers, first aired in April 1977 and is just shy of its 47th birthday.

During a 2021 interview with RNZ's Afternoons programme, original host and creator Brian Edwards said he was inspired by a BBC programme called That's Life.

He said the team behind Fair Go wanted to protect people from rip offs and scams in their daily lives.
