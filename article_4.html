Hawke's Bay locals warned over gang leader's funeral

Police are expecting a large number of gang members to be in Hawke's Bay for the funeral of a Mongrel Mob leader.

Photo: RNZ / Cole Eastham-Farrelly

Police are warning Hawke's Bay locals that a large number of gang members are gathering for a funeral.

A number of gang members met at the Palmerston North Hospital on Tuesday where a high-ranking Mongrel Mob leader had died over the weekend.

The man's funeral would be held in Napier over the following days, police said, and more gang members would be visiting the region.

A police spokesperson said extra staff from across the Eastern District and neighbouring districts had been called to help monitor the funeral.

They reiterated that visitors wearing gang insignia would be arrested under the recent Gangs Act.

One person had already been arrested for wearing a Mongrel Mob t-shirt on Tuesday, but police determined he was not part of the group attending the funeral.
