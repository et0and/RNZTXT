Residents evacuated after fire in Auckland apartment building

A fire in an apartment on the 10th floor was extinguished by the sprinkler system.

Fire and Emergency were at an Auckland apartment building that had smoke coming out of it. Photo: Lucy Xia/RNZ

At least 100 residents were evacuated from a central Auckland apartment block after a fire Thursday morning.

The fire has now been extinguished.

Smoke was coming from the apartment building. Photo: Emilia Sullivan/RNZ

Before 6am smoke could be seen coming out of the top of the building, and from a vent on the roadside.

Five fire trucks were at the building at the corner of Cook and Nelson streets.

Fire and Emergency said they were called to the incident around 5.30am.

More than 100 residents were evacuated. Photo: Lucy Xia/RNZ

It said the fire was in an apartment on the 10th level and that it had been extinguished by the sprinkler system.

Evacuated residents were allowed to return to their homes.

Fire and Emergency were at the scene. Photo: Belinda McCammon/RNZ
