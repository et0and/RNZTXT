The recycling rule changes coming to Auckland

The city's kerbside recycling is set for changes as new national standards come into play.

Photo: Supplied / Auckland Council

Changes to Auckland's kerbside recycling are set to hit from next month as new national standards come into play.

From 1 February, only paper and cardboard, glass, tin cans and plastic types 1, 2 and 5, will be accepted for recycling, Auckland Council announced on Thursday.

Aresol cans, tetrapacks, and all other types of plastics will no longer be able to go in the recycling bin.

Auckland Council said this would reduce contamination of recycling loads by non-recyclable items, such as nappies or bagged rubbish, which costs $3 million every year to sort.

Auckland's planning, environment and parks committee chairman Richard Hills said putting the right items in recyle bins would help increase the quality of material collected and reduce disposal costs.

"Reducing the use of single use items is the best way to reduce waste, following that, it is recycling right."

Auckland Council collects about 132,000 tonnes of recycling every year from kerbside recycling bins, which are sent to facilities onshore and offshore.

The changes, announced by the previous Labour government last year, meant the same items will be accepted for kerbside recycling across New Zealand.

Previously, no one item was recycled by all councils.
