Man who held elderly woman at knifepoint gets more than 5 years in prison

A man who held an elderly woman at knifepoint in her retirement village home and stole her car has been sentenced to five years and seven months in prison.

Kalib Kerr-Millar has been sentenced to five years seven months in prison. Photo: RNZ / Marika Khabazi

A man who held an elderly woman at knifepoint in her retirement village home and stole her car has been sentenced to five years and seven months in prison.

Kalib Kerr-Millar was silent as Judge Katie Elkin imposed the sentence at the Christchurch District Court.

The home invasion happened in 2022, when the 27-year-old entered an elderly woman's home in Darfield, and held a knife to her throat before fleeing in her car.

Kerr-Millar pleaded guilty to a raft of charges including aggravated burglary and assault with a weapon.

Judge Elkin said the impact of the ordeal on the victim's emotional well-being had been significant.
