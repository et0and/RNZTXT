Auckland tsunami map refreshed

Fewer Auckland residents would be at risk in the event of a tsunami than the council previously thought.

The updated Auckland tsunami map. Photo: Auckland Council

Fewer Auckland residents would be at risk in the event of a tsunami than the council previously thought.

Auckland Emergency Management (AEM) has updated the region's tsunami map in the wake of new data which it says showed less of the city's coastline would be at threat.

AEM head of operations John Cranfield said the data used more accurate modelling of tsunamis which replicated earthquakes, and the movement of water, onto the region's shores.

It showed fewer residents living on Auckland shoreline, Hauraki Gulf Islands, and Aotea Great Barrier Island would need to evacuate.

But Cranfield said, while there was a lower risk, much of Auckland's shoreline was still in the red.

He encouraged all Aucklanders to check if they operated in or around any of the zones.

"We've still got a huge job, as does everyone. The key thing is really preparation - be prepared. The catchline is really, 'know your zone,' so know the zone you live, work or play in, and you can do that by visiting our website."

Knowing the natural signs to evacuate was also an invaluable skill, he said.

"If its long or strong, get gone," he re-iterated.

Auckland's north-east coastlines, the Hauraki Gulf Islands, were especially still at greater risk in the case of a tsunami, as was Orewa because of its denser coast population, Cranfield said.
