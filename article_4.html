Two men arrested in relation to incident that saw police threatened

One man threatened to retrieve a gun during the confrontation, police say.

The firearm that was seized by police on Thursday. Photo: Supplied / NZ Police

Two men are due to appear in Rotorua District Court on Friday in relation to an incident that saw police threatened.

Police said when they arrived at a Rotorua address where an assault had been reported, they were confronted by a number of gang members.

They said the gang members approached them aggressively, with one man threatening to retrieve a gun.

The officers left the house for safety reasons and were unable to investigate the reported assault, police said.

Police were able to make follow-up inquiries on Thursday and searched three addresses in relation to the events.

A 21-year-old man was arrested and charged with disorderly behaviour, obstruction and unlawful assembly.

Police also seized a firearm at that address.

Another 19-year-old man at a second address was charged with disorderly behaviour, obstruction and unlawful assembly.

A slug gun was seized from the third address, police said.

Police said their inquiries were ongoing.
