Teen charged after failed attempt to flee supermarket theft

A West Auckland teen has been arrested after three people were injured by a fleeing vehicle last month.

The driver tried to flee, but crashed into another vehicle with two people inside. Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

A West Auckland teen has been arrested after three people were injured by a fleeing vehicle last month.

Police say a woman ran to a waiting vehicle in Henderson after allegedly stealing from a supermarket three weeks ago.

The driver tried to flee, but crashed into another vehicle with two people inside.

Police claim the driver stole a handbag from the vehicle he crashed into, before driving off at speed, injuring the two occupants and a third person who was trying to intervene.

Detectives have arrested a 19-year-old Ranui man, charging him with three counts of aggravated assault and one of theft.

A 21-year-old woman has also been summoned to court over the initial shoplifting.

The teen is expected to appear in Waitākere District Court next week.
