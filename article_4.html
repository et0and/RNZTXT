Government begins weekly reporting of school attendance figures

Schools should aim to have 94 percent of their pupils in class every day, David Seymour says.

Associate Education Minister David Seymour. Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

The government has started weekly reporting of school attendance figures.

Associate Education Minister David Seymour said a new interactive website will updated every week to show how many children were at school on a given day.

Seymour said the information will help schools and parents spot problems and measure improvements.

He said schools should aim to have 94 percent of their pupils in class every day.

School reporting of daily attendance data will be mandatory from the start of next year, Seymour said.
