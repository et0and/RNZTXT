Firefighters contain blaze near Ōtaki Beach

Three rural fire crews have been monitoring hot spots overnight.

A fire at Ōtaki Beach on Saturday 23 March. Photo: Supplied / Michelle Duff

Fire crews have contained a vegetation fire inland from Ōtaki Beach, on the Kāpiti Coast, on Saturday night.

Fire and Emergency said in a statement it was alerted to the fire, behind the former Ōtaki Health Camp, just after 5pm on Saturday.

It burnt through about 5ha of heavy scrub, which Fire and Emergency (FENZ) said made firefighting efforts challenging.

Some people reported they could see it from as far away as Paraparaumu - a distance of 14km.

FENZ said the top priority was keeping the fire away from houses and this had been successful.

Three rural fire crews have been monitoring hot spots overnight, along with a FENZ Drone Team.

Crews will be back on the fire ground on Sunday to put the fire out completely.
