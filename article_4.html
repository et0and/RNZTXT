Disruptive passenger forces Air NZ flight diversion

The aircraft was met by police in Wellington before continuing to Dunedin.

Chief operational integrity and safety officer David Morgan said incidents like this were distressing for customers. Photo: Supplied/ Air NZ

An Air New Zealand Flight from Auckland to Dunedin has been forced to divert after an incident involving a "disruptive passenger".

Chief operational integrity and safety officer David Morgan said in a statement incidents like this were distressing for customers.

"We have a zero tolerance policy for any disruptive behaviour onboard our aircraft."

The aircraft was met by police in Wellington before continuing to Dunedin.
