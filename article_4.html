Power still out for hundreds across North Island

Hundreds of homes in the North Island remain without power following strong winds on Saturday.

A lightning strike on Saturday night left about 280 homes without power in Masterton. Photo: 123RF

Hundreds of homes in the North Island remain without power following strong winds and stormy weather on Saturday.

PowerCo's website showed crews are working to restore electricity to more than 800 properties in the Bay of Plenty, Manawatu, Whanganui and Wairarapa regions.

More than 200 were without power in Paengaroa, some cut off after a tree fell on a power line. Dozens in Cambridge, Te Puke and Stratford were also without power.

A lightning strike on Saturday night left about 280 homes without power in Masterton.

More than 2300 properties in Tauranga, New Plymouth and Manawatū-Whanganui lost power on Saturday. In Hamilton, 600 properties were without power, 580 in the Eastern Bay of Plenty and dozens west and northern Auckland.

A MetService strong wind watch was in place on Sunday morning for parts of the North Island, including Taupō, Taihape, inland Hastings District and Central Hawke's Bay District.
