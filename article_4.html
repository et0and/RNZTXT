Armed police surround Auckland motel

Two men have been arrested after reports of an assault at a motel in Greenlane on Tuesday.

File photo Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Two men have been arrested after reports of an assault at a motel in the suburb of Greenlane in Auckland on Tuesday.

Armed police with dogs descended on the Abelia Inn in Green Lane West around 11am.

A police spokesperson said they were called after it was reported a man had been assaulted.

Two men were arrested and police were considering charges against them.

No one was injured during the incident.

About a dozen armed police officers were outside the motel, a worker at a neighbouring motor lodge told RNZ.

The worker said police had told them it was not safe to be outside and to stay indoors.

A man working at another nearby business, said he couldn't see the inn from his office, but noted there were "often" police turning up there.

"It's not surprising to me - it's used as emergency housing and there's often issues," he said.
