Man dies after fall following police interaction, investigation launched

Investigators are now trying to piece together exactly what happened.

(File photo) Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

A man died in central Auckland last week after suffering a head injury from a fall, following an interaction with the police.

Police were called to a business in Boston Road, in Grafton, on Monday afternoon, as a man there was acting in an agitated way toward staff and paramedics, acting superintendent Sunny Patel said.

"Police officers arrived and instructed the man to leave ...however following this, the man has fallen and suffered a serious head injury.

"The victim was transported to hospital where he underwent surgery, however he has since died."

A critical incident investigation has been begun, and investigators are now trying to piece together exactly what happened, Patel said in a statement.

The Independent Police Conduct Authority had also been notified.

"Our sympathies are with the man's family and friends at this time, and we are providing support to the man's family," Patel said.
