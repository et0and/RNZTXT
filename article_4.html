Further delays for Auckland-Northland drivers after crash closes SH1 in Mata

The crash comes after the Brynderwyn Hills were closed for restoration and a truck rolled on a detour route.

Road closures in Northland on Monday. The black symbols show the closure of State Highway 1 at Mata and the Brynderwyn Hills, while the exclamation mark shows the closure of Paparoa-Oakleigh Road. Photo: Supplied/NZTA

State Highway 1 is closed near the Northland township of Mata after a two-vehicle crash.

It is expected to cause further delays for motorists travelling between Auckland and Northland, after the highway was closed through the Brynderwyn Hills and a detour route was blocked when a logging truck rolled.

Two people were believed to have suffered serious injuries in the Mata crash, which happened about 2.33pm between Totara Road and Hewlett Road, police said.

The state highway had been closed in both directions.

"Motorists are advised to expect delays, or to avoid the area if possible," police said.

Diversions were being put in place between Springfield Road and Salmon Road.

The serious crash unit had been notified.

Monday marked the first day of the closure of State Highway 1 through the Brynderwyn Hills for restoration work after damage caused by Cyclone Gabrielle.

Just after midday Monday, a detour route - Paparoa-Oakleigh Road through Mangapai - also had to be closed after a logging truck rolled.
