Incident at Plant Food and Research facility

The incident is believed to involve chemicals, FENZ says.

Emergency services respond to an incident involving chemicals at Plant and Food Research. Photo: RNZ / Rayssa Almeida

An Auckland research institute has been evacuated after an incident involving chemicals this afternoon.

Fire and Emergency (FENZ) said they were altered to the incident after a fire alarm went off at about 12.44pm.

A Plant and Food Research spokesperson confirmed the incident was at their facility on Mount Albert Road, and said all staff were evacuated from the building.

The facility, on Mt Albert Road, is a government-owned Crown Research Institute.

Emergency services respond to an incident involving chemicals at Plant and Food Research. Photo: RNZ / Rayssa Almeida

FENZ said the incident was believed to involve chemicals.

They were attending with eight pumps, three aerials and three specialist appliances.

Hato Hone St John Ambulance were called to the incident at 1.48pm, and had one rapid response vehicle, one ambulance and an operations manager on the scene.

Police were assisting FENZ at the scene.

FENZ were investigating the cause of the fire alarm.
