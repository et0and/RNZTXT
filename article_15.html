Jacinda Ardern to receive damehood from Prince William

The former prime minister had not been able to attend an investiture ceremony in New Zealand, so was invited to join one at Windsor Castle next week instead.

Dame Jacinda at a previous ceremony at Buckingham Palace in 2018. Photo: AFP

New Zealand's former prime minister Jacinda Ardern will formally receive her damehood from Prince William at a ceremony in Windsor Castle in London next week.

She was appointed a Dame Grand Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit in the 2023 King's Birthday Honours for her services to the state, a few months after her shock resignation as prime minister.

Ardern's official citation listed her leadership in response to the 15 March terrorist attacks and the Covid-19 pandemic "positioning New Zealand as having one of the lowest Covid-19 related death rates in the Western world".

Dame Jacinda was in the UK for a meeting of the prince's Earthshot Prize, of which she is a trustee.

In a statement, she said she had not been able to attend an investiture ceremony yet having been away from home, but had received an offer to join one in the United Kingdom instead.
