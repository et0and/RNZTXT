Ex-cop and convicted rapist Brad Shipton has died

The former police detective convicted of raping Louise Nicholas in a case that had far-reaching consequences, has died aged 65.

By Emily Brookes of

Brad Shipton had been in a rest home suffering from early-onset dementia. Photo: Stuff / Phil Reid

Brad Shipton, the former police detective convicted of raping Louise Nicholas in a case that had far-reaching consequences for New Zealand law and policing, has died aged 65.

Nicholas, his victim told Stuff she had been informed that Shipton had died in April.

"He's been dead to me for a long time," she said.

Shipton had been in a rest home suffering from early-onset dementia.

Shipton confessed to his role in Nicholas' gang rape in 2009, decades after the offences and when he had already served three years for the crime.

At the time he said his whole life had been "full of disgraceful, disgusting behaviour" and he knew he had ruined his victim's life.

Nicholas first made her claims in 2004, in an explosive interview in The Post (then the Dominion Post).

That came after years of investigation by Post reporter Robert Kitchen that would ultimately lead to revelations that a senior officer, John Dewar, had perverted the course of justice in order to secure mistrial acquittals for other policemen, including Shipton, over the course of several trials.

This included not disclosing to a jury that Shipton was already in jail at the time of the trial for an earlier rape.

Dewar himself was eventually convicted on four charges of attempting to obstruct or defeat the course of justice.

Nicholas has gone on to become a renowned campaigner for victims of rape and sexual violence.

This story was first published by Stuff.
