Live weather: Wairoa flooding 'very, very sad' - mayor

Hundreds of people have been affected by flooding on the east coast of the North Island, with more rain expected over the next few days. Follow all the latest developments with RNZ's live blog.

Flooding on Black Street in Wairoa yesterday. Photo: RNZ / Calvin Samuel

A state of emergency remains in place in the Hawke's Bay town of Wairoa, after heavy rain and strong winds hit the East Coast.

Four hundred properties, including 100 homes, were flooded on Wednesday, with many of them never having flooded before.

Seventy-five people stayed in the town's evacuation centre last night.

Mayor Craig Little said while the town worked hard to clear debris from roads yesterday so people could access their homes, there will be a lot of infrstructure repair rquired, including on the roads.

"It's very, very sad. This has never happened, they have never flooded where they've been."

The state of emergency will remain in place until at least high tide, which is due shortly after 9.30am.

Further south, an evacuation order is still in place for about 140 homes in the township of Haumoana, south-east of Hastings.
