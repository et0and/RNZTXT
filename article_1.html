Council mulls u-turn in berm mowing stoush

The council has tabled options to reinstate the service after it was controversially removed to save money.

John Murphy's Levin berm. Photo: John Murphy

Horowhenua District Council will re-examine it's controversial decision to stop mowing road side berms in the coming month.

The council trimmed back on the service in July - saving ratepayers in the district nearly $250,000 a year.

At the time one Levin resident told RNZ he would refuse to mow his berm in protest.

Next month the council will table a report of options to reinstate the service.

Council minutes cited the significant public reaction to and a "noticeable impact on the appearance of townships".
