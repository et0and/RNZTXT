Live: Survivors of abuse march on Parliament

Dozens are taking part in the hīkoi ahead of the release of the Royal Commission of Inquiry's final report.

Photo: RNZ / Calvin Samuel

Survivors of abuse in state and faith-based institutions are taking part in a hīkoi to Parliament ahead of the release of the Royal Commission of Inquiry's final report.

The report weighs 14 kilograms and is set to contain well over 100 recommendations.

More than 2300 survivors gave evidence to the inquiry, as the commissioners examined the prolific and systemic abuse which occurred in state and faith-based institutions from 1950 to 1999.

The final report will be made public at 4pm.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon is expected to make a formal apology to survivors in November.
