Coromandel Peninsula highway closed after fatal crash

A major road on the Coromandel Peninsula has been closed after a fatal crash, and travellers are being told to take other routes.

The crash was between Te Puru and Valder Place, Waiomu, on the west side of the Coromandel Peninsula, police said. Photo: Supplied/ NZTA

A major road on the Coromandel Peninsula has been closed after a fatal crash, and travellers are being told to take other routes.

Emergency services are responding to the crash on State Highway 25 between Thames and Coromandel town, reported just before 9am on Friday.

One person was dead after the two car crash, police said.

While St John said it treated four patients with minor injuries at the scene.

At 10.30am, police said the road would be closed for at least several hours.

"Motorists travelling between Thames and Coromandel are asked to delay travel or take an alternative route where possible, as the road is expected to be closed for some time," a police spokesperson said.

The narrow coastal road winds up the west side of the Coromandel Peninsula and is heavily used during the peak summer holiday period.
