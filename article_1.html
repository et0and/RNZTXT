Live: Thousands without power, schools closed, as heavy snow hits Central Otago

Around 3000 homes are without power and some schools closed as the South Island braces for heavy snow. Audio

Snow in Idaburn on Friday. Photo: Supplied / Rowena McDiarmid

Parts of the South Island are in for some snow and despite some reports that Auckland will see snow next week, it is unlikely.

Canterbury high country and inland Otago are bracing for heavy snowfall on Friday morning with orange warnings in place.

MetService said up to 25 centimetres of snow is forecast to settle above 600 metres in Otago, and up to 20cm in Canterbury, south of the Rakaia river.

Meanwhile, an orange heavy rain warning is in place for Westland until noon, and snow warnings in place until 3pm.

MetService meteorologist John Law told Morning Report there was a "fair bit of snow" already coming through on the webcams on Crown Range Road and at Queenstown Airport.

Moisture resulting from the cold air and slow-moving front made for the "perfect conditions" for snow to continue piling up throughout the day.

Law said the heaviest snowfall expected was up to 25cm in inland parts of Otago.

"That's still high enough to run across the passes, say the Crown Range Road, places like the Lindis Pass as we head further north as well, that snow moves up into places like Arthur's and Porters Pass. so well worth keeping that in mind if you're planning your journey across those higher passes of the South Island today."

Snow in Alexandra. Photo: Supplied / Zara Morris

Snowfall in some central Otago towns is causing power outages to about 3000 homes.

Aurora Energy general manager operations and network performance Matt Settle told Morning Report all their crews in the area were heading out to make inspections.

"It is the snow at the moment, there are no other unusual things happening on the network and we believe all the outages at this point are snow related."

It wasn't clear what damage had been caused, he said.

"When we do have a snow dump in the area it does generate some hazards for field crews and restricts their access and mobility and does make it a little bit challenging to identify all the causes."

Weight and load on lines were the most common issues with snow and they wouldn't take too long to repair once found, Settle said.

"Our field crews are very experienced at this."

Mount Aspiring National Park, Alexandra, Omakau, Crowell, and Clyde areas were all experiencing unplanned outages, affecting about 3500 homes.

Repairs were expected to be needed at five sites involving three major lines and two lesser lines, he said.

About 1100 homes in Alexandra are without power, according to the Aurora Energy website.

There are also 850 homes without power near Lauder, almost 800 homes without power around Cromwell, and 650 homes are without power near Clyde.

Lauder resident Darryl Jones said he lost power to his home around 5.15am and there had been a "decent dump" of snow.

He was not quite snowed in but "you wouldn't want to be driving at the moment".

Jones said there was not much that could be done given the weather and that he might go for a wander and snap some pictures.

Snow in Clyde. Photo: Supplied / Julie Cairns-Gee

Toast was off the menu with the power out but a cup of tea was still possible with the log burner.

It was a "golden rule" to always have plenty of firewood in central Otago, Jones said.

He had lived in the area for about 30 years and it was not unheard of to get a decent dump - but it was heavier than it had been in recent years.

The snow has caused schools in Central Otago and Queenstown to be closed today.

In a post on Facebook, Queenstown Primary School said "school is closed today - Friday 13 September due to adverse weather".

Alexandra Primary School was also closed.

"Our local primary schools have decided to CLOSE today due to the power outages around the area and the continued snow. Apologies for the inconvenience that this will cause some of our families," it said on Facebook.

Snow and ice has closed State Highway 85 from Lyeburn to Palmerston, the New Zealand Transport Agency website shows.

"Road users are advised to avoid the area of delay your journey if possible."

State Highway 94 from Te Anau to Milford is also currently closed due to avalanche hazard assessments.

"It is likely that the road will reopen at approx 11am this morning, after road snow clearing. Please drive with caution as there are plough trucks operating along the highway from Te Anau to Milford Sound."

Meanwhile, Canterbury could see snow up on the foothills and higher areas - but it was likely to be wetter than anything on the plains.

And in response to reports that Auckland could see snow next week?

Law said next week would see "good, strong southwesterlies" and cold air being pushed up the country.

Snow may be seen on the Desert Road and central plateau but not Auckland.

"I think next week is going to be a reminder that spring is a fairly volatile season with the weather changing pretty quickly."
