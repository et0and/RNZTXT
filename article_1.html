Four people injured in alleged stabbing incident at Sydney church

A man is in custody after at least four people were injured in an alleged stabbing incident at a church in Sydney's south-west on Monday night.

Photo: 123RF

A man is in custody after at least four people were injured in an alleged stabbing incident at a church in Sydney's south-west on Monday night.

NSW Police said officers were called to Welcome Street in Wakeley about 7:10pm, following reports a number of people had been stabbed.

NSW Ambulance said a man in his 50s has been taken to Liverpool Hospital with stab wounds.

Three others are being treated at the scene, including a man in his 30s and two others with lacerations to the arm and hand.

NSW Police said the injuries are non-life threatening.

"A large police response is underway and the public is urged to avoid the area," NSW Police said in a statement.

One male was arrested by police at the scene.

He is being interviewed.

Several police and ambulance vehicles went to the scene of the attack.

In vision from the scene, dozens of people could be seen leaving the church property and gathering on the street outside.

The ABC understands the incident happened at Christ the Good Shepherd Church.
