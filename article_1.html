Live weather: Wairoa resident has 'no idea where I'm going to go' after home flooded

Hundreds of people have been affected by flooding on the east coast of the North Island, with more rain expected over the next few days. Follow all the latest developments with RNZ's live blog.

Marie Tuahine. Photo: Calvin Samuel

Wairoa kuia Marie Tuahine says she and her daughter and five mokopuna - the youngest just ten months' old - had to walk through knee deep water in the dark on Wednesday morning, after the evacuation warning sounded.

The council says more than 400 homes have been flooded - 100 seriously - in the district.

And those families are now staying with family or friends or at the Wairoa Memorial Hall which is where Tuahine is.

She told Morning Report she was a bit disorientated waking up to the alarm going off. He niece was banging on the window trying to wake them up and tell them there was flooding.

Her daughter carried the two youngest to the car because the water was too deep.

"The water was up to my knees and it's sloshy brown, you just couldn't see anything."

The carpets throughout the house are covered in silt and slush.

"I have no idea where I'm going to go."

There are no vacancies in the two motels in Wairoa, she says.
