Black Caps putting up a fight in Wellington

Australia have lost three quick wickets after lunch and are 142-7, with a lead of 346.

Glenn Phillips celebrates the wicket of Australia's Alex Carey. Photo: Photosport

The Black Caps hero of day two has come to the party again, this time with the ball.

Glenn Phillips made a fighting half century yesterday afternoon as his team-mates collapsed around him and his side conceded a 205 run first innings lead.

Australia has a lead of 350 but now have just two wickets in hand thanks to a five-for from Phillips.

Phillips now has 5/38 with the off-spinner dismissing Travis Head and Mitchell Marsh in consecutive balls.

Pat Cummins and Mitch Starc and currently at the crease.

Australia had gone to lunch on 113 for four, after nightwatchman Nathan Lyon, who frustrated the host with 41, and opener Usman Khawaja were dismissed in the morning.
