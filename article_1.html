Treaty Principles Bill to be introduced to Parliament this week

The government intends to introduce the Treaty Principles Bill this week, according to a memo sent to lawyers by the Waitangi Tribunal.

The bill brings to life ACT Party policy, to enshrine what the Treaty means into law. Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

The government intends to introduce the Treaty Principles Bill this week, according to a memo sent to lawyers by the Waitangi Tribunal.

The Bill was set to be introduced on 18 November, but will now be put to the House on Thursday, before a national hīkoi opposing it is held next week.

Associate justice minister David Seymour, who is responsible for the bill, had no comment.

In response, the Tribunal has released its second interim report into the bill.

The report calls the bill the worst, most comprehensive breach of Te Tiriti o Waitangi in modern times - and if enacted, the bill would end any formal relationship between the Crown and Māori.
