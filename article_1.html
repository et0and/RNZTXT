Civil Defence watching as heavy rain, thunderstorms loom

Heavy rain warning has been issued for Hawke's Bay and most of the North Island may also face deluge.

Thunderstorms are possible for Northland, Auckland, Coromandel, Bay of Plenty and Gisborne. Photo: MetService

Heavy rain is forecast to lash parts of the North Island this week, and places like Northland and Gisborne could reach warning criteria.

A warning has already been issued for Hawke's Bay, meaning streams and rivers could rise, with the possibility of slips and surface flooding.

From midday Monday, there is also a heavy rain watch for Northland.

Northland Civil Defence said it was monitoring the situation.

Heavy rain watches have been issued for Auckland, including Great Barrier Island, Coromandel Peninsula, Bay of Plenty west of Kawerau, and Rotorua, with the likelihood of thunderstorms for later in the day.

From Tuesday morning there are heavy rain watches for Gisborne, Wairarapa and Tararua.

The watches could be upgraded to warnings, MetService said, and more areas could be affected.

On Monday, heavy rain was expected to be widespread across the country, MetService meteorologist Ngaire Wotherspoon said.

Thunderstorms were also possible for Northland on Monday evening, she said.

Gisborne and Hawke's Bay's heavy rain watch lasts until 6pm Wednesday.

The government is changing rules to make it easier for lines companies to remove trees near powerlines, to prevent outages during high winds and storms.

The move would ensure the power stayed on, particularly during severe weather events, Energy Minister Simeon Brown said. The changes will come into effect in September.
