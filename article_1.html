'Come out now and show us your hands': Police surround Auckland property

The Armed Offenders Squad is approaching the property behind shield as a cordon around the area widens.

Police at the scene in Tutuki Street in Waterview on Wednesday afternoon. Photo: RNZ/ Calvin Samuel

The police armed offenders squad have surrounded a property on Tutuki Street in the Auckland suburb of Waterview and are ordering someone to come out.

Officers using a loudspeaker could be heard saying: "Come out now and show us your hands", while the Armed Offenders Squad is approaching the property behind shields from the street.

Police cordons have been set up at the incident and include Fir Street, while the police Eagle Helicopter was hovering above and an ambulance was also at the scene.

Officers were widening the cordon at 4:30pm, and asking members of the public to move further down Fir Street away from Tutuki Street, as it was "not safe where you are standing anymore."

Photo: RNZ/ Calvin Samuel

Police commenting on the operation said officers were armed as a precaution.

A spokesperson asked members of the public to stay clear of the cordons.

Nearby Great North Road remained open for traffic.
