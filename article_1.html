Te Whatu Ora data breach accused appears in court

The case was put back to the afternoon after police indicated they would oppose the accused man's release on bail.

Barry Young, 56, in Wellington District Court on Monday morning. Photo: RNZ / Ruth Hill

A Te Whatu Ora employee accused of stealing Covid 19 data and using it to spread misinformation about vaccine-related deaths has appeared in court.

Barry Young, 56, is charged with accessing a computer system for dishonest purposes, which carries a maximum penalty of seven years' prison.

Te Whatu Ora has alleged Young leaked the data, which was published on an overseas website.

Young appeared briefly in Wellington District Court on Monday morning.

His case was put back to the afternoon after police indicated they would be opposing his release on bail, because of the risk he could continue to spread misinformation.

Defence lawyer Matthew Hague has told the court the police had no evidence Young presented any risk.

Judge Andrew Nicholls said he would consider the application once it was made, and make a decision on Monday afternoon.
