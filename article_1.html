Dolphins sink one of Aotearoa's biggest sailing events on first day

Racing has been abandoned entirely after dolphins that earlier delayed the start did not move on.

SailGP is being held in Lyttelton across Saturday and Sunday. Photo: Felix Diemer for SailGP / Handout image supplied by SailGP

The first day of the SailGP racing event in Lyttelton has been abandoned after dolphins were sighted on the course.

The race was due to get under way about 3pm, but was delayed after the dolphins were spotted.

After they had not moved on about two hours later, the race was called off entirely.

Organisers had predicted a record-breaking crowd of 11,000 visitors for each day of the weekend's racing.

Grace Uivel from Ata Ceramics earlier said SailGP had been a "wonderful" event for the town.

Uivel said there had been a buzz around town. "It's a beautiful day and we've had a lot of really friendly visitors."

SailGP returned to Lyttelton for the second time after Auckland pulled out of hosting the event.
