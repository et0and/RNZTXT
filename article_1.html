Labour's Rachel Boyack confirms 26-vote win in Nelson recount

Rachel Boyack has been confirmed to have won the Nelson electorate by a margin of 26 votes.

Rachel Boyack last week after the final election results were announced. A judicial recount has since confirmed her win in the electorate. Photo: RNZ / Samantha Gee

Rachel Boyack has been confirmed to have won the Nelson electorate by a margin of 26 votes.

The margin is three votes smaller than it was in the Electoral Commission's final vote count last week.

In a statement, Boyack said she was delighted to have been confirmed as the winner, but acknowledged the campaign run by her opponent.

"It is the privilege of my life to be the MP for Nelson and I'm looking forward to continuing my work as a strong advocate for the Nelson region," she said.

"I acknowledge Blair Cameron from the National Party who ran a strong campaign in Nelson."

"I'm looking forward to getting back to Parliament and working alongside my colleagues under Chris Hipkins' leadership to hold the new government to account. Labour will be a formidable opposition and I intend to play my part as a strong local MP for Nelson - ensuring investment continues in public transport, the Nelson Hospital Upgrade, housing and significant economic development projects."

She also acknowledged Labour colleagues who would not be returning after the party's election results.
