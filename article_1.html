Live: Questions in NZ as allies accuse China of mass hacking

Spy agencies are expected to brief MPs about whether NZ might also be caught up today.

GCSB minister Judith Collins. Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Questions are being raised about whether New Zealand might also be caught up in cyber campaigns that have prompted the US and UK to introduce new sanctions against China.

In 2021 New Zealand revealed it had been victim to 'malicious cyber activity' undertaken by the Chinese Ministry of State Security.

The US Justice Department and FBI say millions of Americans' online accounts have been caught up in a Chinese hacking plot that targeted US officials.

And in the UK, deputy prime minister Oliver Dowden accused China of being responsible for targeting the Electoral Commission and MPs.

MPs on New Zealand's Security and Intelligence committee are meeting on Tuesday and are expected to be briefed by the heads of the SIS and GCSB.
