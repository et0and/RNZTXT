Ministry of Education puts 100 school building projects on ice

Deferring the builds and rescoping 110 others will save $2 billion, the ministry says.

Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

The Ministry of Education has put 100 school building projects on hold.

It said deferring the builds and rescoping 110 others will save $2 billion.

The plans will be reconsidered for the next government budget.

The ministry reviewed 352 building projects across 305 schools, with an expected cost of $4.6b.

It has approved 142 of them to go ahead with no changes.

Some are only in the design and planning stage, and will need more funding before they can proceed to the next step.
