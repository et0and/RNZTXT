Whales refloated after stranding on East Coast beach

A pod of false killer whales has been refloated after stranding at a beach near Māhia on the East Coast.

People rushed to the Tairāwhiti beach to help the stranded whales. Photo: Supplied/ Facebook

A pod of false killer whales has been refloated after stranding at a beach near Māhia on the East Coast.

Project Jonah manager Daren Grover said there was concern that the whales would not swim out to sea and strand themselves again as the tide went out.

The team at the beach were creating a "human chain" in the shallows, splashing and making noise to prevent the whales from running aground again.

Earlier, a video posted on a Facebook community page showed the whales thrashing in the shallows of a rocky beach and waving their tails, surrounded by water.

Grover said the beach was isolated and a major stranding for an area where there were not many people nearby to help.

He warned that any locals wanting to help should not get in the water with the stranded whale because it could be unpredictable and dangerous.
