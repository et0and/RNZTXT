Watch: Simon Watts outlines 'ambitious' climate plan

The climate change minister is explaining the five pillars to the government's climate strategy at an event in Auckland. Watch live.

Climate Change Minister Simon Watts is outlining five pillars to the government's climate strategy at an event in the Auckland.

The announcement is being livestreamed at the top of this page.

Watts said the three-page strategy was a "comprehensive" and "ambitious" plan. It aimed to reduce the impacts of climate change and prepare for its future effects.

"The strategy is built on five core pillars and underscores the government's commitment to delivering on our climate change goals," Watts said in a statement.

Watts said many in New Zealand, including households and businesses were already feeling the effects of climate change.

"We have seen what severe weather can do to infrastructure and property, and how that disrupts our supply chains and communities.

"Our government has committed to meeting our climate change targets - reducing net emissions is one of the nine government targets to achieve better results from the public service."

Watts also said the government would soon consult on its Emissions Reduction Plan for 2026 through to 2030.

"The Emissions Reduction Plan will set out policy proposals across the five pillars and focus on the largest drivers of emissions in New Zealand - energy, transport, agriculture, and waste sectors."
