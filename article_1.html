Live: Out of control blaze doubles in size to nearly 1000 hectares

Convoys are being escorted across a major state highway in Canterbury as crews continue to try to contain a fire from the air and ground.

An out of control fire in Canterbury has doubled in size to nearly 980 hectares as crews battle to contain it.

The blaze was not contained about 11am, Incident Commander Brian Keown says.

SH73 was closed overnight, but reopened at midday with stop-go signs in place and motorists requiring an escort to pass through.

"We have nine helicopters alongside three fixed-wing aircraft attacking the fire from the air, while 15 trucks, tankers and support vehicles are fighting the fire on the ground."
