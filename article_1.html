Homeowner taken to hospital after confronting boys in burglary attempt

Five boys aged between 11 and 13 have been arrested after a burglary in the Auckland suburb of Remuera.

File photo. Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

Five boys aged between 11 and 13 have been arrested after a burglary in the Auckland suburb of Remuera on Wednesday evening.

Senior Sergeant David Mayes said a Ohinerau Street homeowner returned home to find five people had broken into their home about 9pm.

"When confronted, the suspects have attempted to flee.

"The property owner tried to stop the suspects and was assaulted by one of them."

Police said officers, with the help of an Eagle helicopter and the dog unit, were able to find all five suspects and took them into custody.

The boys have been referred to Youth Aid in relation to the burglary and assault.

Mayes said the homeowner was taken to hospital as a precaution.

"They have since received medical treatment for minor injuries and are now recovering at home.

"We understand the impact property crime can have, however, would caution people against taking matters into their own hands.

"In general, the best thing to do is to avoid a confrontation, maintain observations, and call police."
