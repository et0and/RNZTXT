Live: Choppers, planes heading back to fight Castle Hill fire

Efforts are ramping back up to fight the massive blaze, which has seen some Cantabrians evacuated.

Nine helicopters and three planes are due back in the air on Friday morning as efforts to fight a massive Canterbury scrub fire ramp back up.

An overnight crew of firefighters have been fighting the blaze from State Highway 73 as it has been deemed too dangerous to go off road in the dark.

On Thursday night, firefighters encouraged Castle Hill residents to leave their homes as the 535-hectare scrub fire burned.

A community meeting was scheduled for 1pm Friday at the Tawera Memorial Hall in Springfield in case people were still evacuated at that point.
