Large fire breaks out at waste transfer station

Eleven crews are responding and Fire and Emergency has set up a command point at the scene.

Photo: RNZ / Nate McKinnon

A large fire is burning at a waste transfer station in the Auckland suburb of Onehunga.

Fire and Emergency was called to Enviro NZ's Pikes Point Transfer Station about 4.20am on Wednesday.

It describes the blaze as a compound fire on Captain Springs Road.

It said 12 crews were responding and a command point has been established at the scene.

The fire is about 30 metres high, 50 metres long and 33 metres wide.

St John said it sent one ambulance to the scene, but could not yet say if anyone was injured.
