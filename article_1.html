Inflation hits lowest level in 3.5 years

Economists expect the Reserve Bank to continue slashing rates, with another 50 basis point cut expected next month.

Photo: RNZ

Consumer price inflation has slowed to its lowest level in three and a half years.

Stats NZ said the annual inflation rate for the September quarter was 2.2 percent - falling to the Reserve Bank's 1 to 3 percent target band for the first time since March 2021.

The annual rate was 3.3 percent in the June quarter.

The consumer price index rose 0.6 percent in the September quarter on the back of higher rates, food and rents, which offset cheaper fuel, and childcare costs, Stats NZ said.

The reimposition of prescription charges lifted medical costs, but the Family Boost scheme drove the fall in education charges.

Local body rates rose more than 12 percent, the highest since 1990.

With inflation falling back in the 1-3 percent target zone, economists expected the Reserve Bank to continue slashing rates, with another 50 basis point cut expected next month.
