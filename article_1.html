Live: Power cuts reported in Auckland, warning for Wellington motorists

A power outage for parts of Mt Eden has been resolved, while SH6 on the West Coast has reopened.

Photo: Facebook / Grey District Council

Vector Electricity says a power outage seemingly caused by lightning has been resolved.

Residents in Mt Eden reported power outages at around 2pm today, immediately after an exceptionally loud thunderstrike.

But Vector says it is still investigating the exact cause of the outage and can't confirm if it was related to the storm.

It says power was restored by around 3pm.

Wellington drivers are being warned downpours forecast for this afternoon could affect the motorway into the city.

A southerly change later today could bring thunderstorms and possibly hail, as well a heavy rain.

The Transport Agency says drivers on State Highway 2 between Petone and Ngauranga should take care from 5pm through to around 3am on Tuesday.

Wellington City Council says it has reports of minor flooding on streets in Newtown, Rongotai and at the Basin Reserve.

Manwhile, flooding has closed State Highway 6 on the South Island's West Coast as rain looms around the country.

There are also heavy rain watches in place for Northland, Auckland, Coromandel and Bay of Plenty, with possible thunderstorms forecast Monday.
