Crews respond to bush fire in Auckland bay

Several firetrucks and two helicopters are responding to a large vegetation fire burning through bush in a remote part of southeast Auckland.

Fire in Kawakawa Bay in Auckland on 30 March, 2024. Photo: Supplied / Jazmyn Meiklejohn

Several firetrucks and two helicopters are responding to a large vegetation fire burning through bush in a remote part of southeast Auckland.

Fire and Emergency New Zealand said it was alerted to the blaze in Kawakawa Bay just after 1pm.

A spokesperson said the fire on Kawakawa Bay Coast Road near Raukura Point is 600 meters by 400 meters in size.

Six firetrucks, two helicopters and three water tankers are working to control the fire.

They said no properties are at risk at this stage, and police are also helping in the response.
