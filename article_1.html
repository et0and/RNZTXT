Live: Communities still isolated, large slips and water supply limited

The Transport Agency still has numerous road warnings around Otago and Southland. Follow RNZ's blog for the latest weather updates.

Emergency response teams in Otago say floodwaters in the city are receding.

Light drizzle is continuing to linger but roads and streets are beginning to drain after the deluge which brought Dunedin's wettest day in a century.

Some roads and highways are still affected and NZTA is aiming to have State Highway 1 north of Dunedin re-opened by this afternoon.

Crews are working to remove 300 cubic metres of rock from a slip south of Waikouaiti and there are many smaller slips on the highway's shoulders.

Otago Civil Defence group controller Matt Alley says teams are also still working to provide support to communities, restore roading and support people who have had to evacuate.

He says it's too early to say how many homes and businesses have been affected, or the cost of damage.
