Live: 'I've got a right to say what I said' - Peters on defamation threat

The Foreign Minister was speaking at the New Zealand China Council while questions linger about the AUKUS security alliance .

Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Foreign Minister Winston Peters is speaking to the New Zealand China Council in Auckland .

It comes after lawyers for former Australian foreign minister Bob Carr wrote to Peters informing him they intend to launch legal action against him.

It also follows another speech earlier this week, where Peters said New Zealand was a long way from deciding on whether to join the AUKUS security alliance after criticism from the opposition.

Peters' speech this morning covered New Zealand's relationship with China. He also took questions from media following the speech, including on the legal drama.
