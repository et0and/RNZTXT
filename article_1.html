Two arrested, witness describes gun being pulled on street

Two people have been arrested after armed police cordoned off central Auckland streets and went into an apartment complex.

Police on Hobson Street in Auckland. Photo: RNZ/Lucy Xia

Officers have closed off sections of Hobson and Union streets and have since gone into the Goodview Apartment Hotel.

A group of four officers armed with guns and a riot shield entered the hotel.

Two people, a man and woman, were brought out by officers.

Police were called to the scene about 8am on Monday.

A witness told RNZ he saw two people arguing on the street. Soon after one of the pair "got up, put his hand in his pocket, pulled out a gun and loaded it and pointed it at him and said he's going to shoot him".

No injuries have been reported.

The public is being asked to avoid the area.

Photo: RNZ/Marika Khabazi

Auckland Transport said Hobson Street was closed between Cook and Union streets.

It is affecting bus routes 11W, 18, 105, 132, 195, 209, WX1, 11T.

Due to an emergency services incident at the intersection of Cook Street and Hobson Street, Auckland CBD, Hobson Street is closed between Cook Street and Union Street.
