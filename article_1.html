ACT MP Brooke van Velden gets public ticking off from commissioner

New government Minster and ACT MP Brooke van Velden wrongly quoted the Parliamentary Commissioner for the Environment.

ACT deputy leader Brooke van Velden at the formal swearing-in of the new coalition government at Government House. Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

New government Minster and ACT MP Brooke van Velden has got a public ticking off from the Parliamentary Commissioner for the Environment for making wrong and misleading comments about the ban on new oil and gas exploration.

The ban was brought in by the Labour led government in 2018 but the new coalition is going to repeal it.

The letter from Simon Upton to van Velden is published on the commissioner's website.

In in it Upton tells her she has wrongly quoted him as saying the ban was likely to increase global greenhouse emissions and that a similar comment was made in ACT's Energy policy.

The statements were incorrect and misleading and he has never said the ban would increase emissions, he said.

There was no firm basis for claiming that it will, he said.
