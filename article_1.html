Ravindra, Willliamson power Black Caps into ICC Champions Trophy final

Centuries by Rachin Ravindra and Kane Williamson and impressive bowling from captain Mitchell Santner guided New Zealand to a big win over South Africa.

New Zealand's Rachin Ravindra (L) celebrates with teammate Kane Williamson after scoring a century at the Champions Trophy, 2025. Photo: AFP

Centuries by Rachin Ravindra and Kane Williamson along with an impressive bowling spell from captain Mitchell Santner guided the Black Caps to a big win over South Africa in their Champions League semi-final in Lahore.

The Black Caps won by 50 runs.

After deciding to bat first New Zealand scored 362 for six with Ravindra making 108 and Williamson 102.

Daryl Mitchell and Glenn Phillips both scored 49.

The Proteas made a solid start before captain Mtchell Santner grabbed three wickets to slow their run chase.

South Africa finished on 312 for nine with David Miller scoring an unbeaten century.

Santner took three wickets for 43 runs from his ten overs. Glenn Phillips took two while fellow spinners Michael Bracewell and Ravindra took one each.

New Zealand will travel to Dubai to play India in Sunday's final.

New Zealand won this tournament in 2000 when it was known as the ICC Knock Out.
