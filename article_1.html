Watch: Luxon talks to media about Hawke's Bay cyclone recovery

Live - Prime Minister Christopher Luxon has been in Wairoa this morning to gauge progress of the town's recovery from Cyclone Gabrielle. Watch a media conference with him here.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon has been in Wairoa this morning to gauge progress of the town's recovery from Cyclone Gabrielle and is meeting with several Hawke's Bay mayors.

Watch a media conference with the PM here:

Luxon has already met with Wairoa Mayor Craig Little and toured damaged sites in the town, including Takitimu Marae where the floodwaters swept through leaving a thick layer of silt.

Wairoa was badly hit by Cyclone Gabrielle with scores displaced and a section of State Highway 2 between Wairoa and Napier washed out. In October, Little told RNZ between $60 million and $100m of work needed to be carried out in the town to prevent similar disasters.

Little said he told the prime minister flood protection was top priority because the town did not have any.

There were still 130 homes that were unliveable and half of them were uninsured, he said.

Little also told Luxon he wanted State Highway 2 fixed, because it was continually closed due to slips in poor weather.

Luxon is also expected to meet with other Hawke's Bay mayors today.
