Travellers face 100km detour after SH29 closed

People travelling between Hamilton and Tauranga may be forced to make an almost 100-kilometre detour after a major road was closed due to a crash.

File photo. Photo:

People travelling between Hamilton and Tauranga may be forced to make an almost 100-kilometre detour after a major road was closed due to a crash.

A collision between two vehicles on State Highway 29 in the Kaimai Ranges has shut down the main connecting road between the two cities.

The road has been closed since midday and the transport agency said it does not know when it will reopen.

The shortest detour is 96km via State Highways 28, 5 and 36.

Travellers posting on social media say traffic has ground to a halt.

Meanwhile, a person has died after a serious crash in Mount Maunganui, Bay of Plenty on Wednesday night.

Police said emergency services were notified of the crash at 10.17pm at the intersection of Aerodrome Road and Hewletts Road, Mount Maunganui.

A second person was taken to hospital with moderate injuries.

Police said inquiries were ongoing to determine the cause of the crash.

The Christmas-New Year holiday road toll now stands at eight.
