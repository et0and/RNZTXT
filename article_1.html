Man accused of damaging panel at Te Papa appears in court

A number of supporters have packed the public gallery of a Wellington courtroom at a hearing for a man charged in relation to a protest at Te Papa.

Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

A number of supporters have packed the public gallery of a Wellington courtroom at a hearing for a man charged in relation to a protest at Te Papa.

A display of an English version of the Treaty of Waitangi was damaged on Monday.

Te Wehi Ratana has pleaded not guilty to charges of intentional damage and obstructing police.

He appeared at Wellington District Court on Thursday afternoon seeking bail, which was granted by Judge Katie Elkin.

His conditions specify that he is to stay at a Wellington address until 8am Friday, when he and his family will drive back to Rotorua.

Judge Elkin said he was not to incite, support or participate in any unlawful protest activity, or travel south of Levin.
