Live: Forest lodge destroyed by out of control blaze

Convoys are being escorted across a reopened SH73 in Canterbury, as crews continue to try to contain a fire from the air and ground.

An out of control fire in Canterbury has doubled in size to nearly 980 hectares as crews battle to contain it.

One building, the Holloway Lodge - a former Forest Service hut near the peak of Bridge Hill - has been destroyed by the fire.

The blaze is still not contained, Incident Commander Brian Keown says.

SH73 was closed overnight, but reopened at midday with stop-go signs in place and motorists requiring an escort to pass through.
