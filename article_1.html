Corrections staff allegedly bribed with sex and money

Six people have been charged with corruption and bribery after a three-year probe by police into the jail.

Rimutaka Prison. (File image) Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

Corrections staff were allegedly bribed with sex and money to take contraband into Rimutaka Prison.

Six people have been charged with corruption and bribery after a three-year investigation by police into the Upper Hutt jail.

The six defendants appeared in Hutt Valley District Court on Tuesday.

A Porirua man appeared for allegedly accepting sex twice from a woman as a bribe to smuggle contraband into the prison.

He was granted interim name suppression and remanded until his next court appearance on the 9 January, 2024.

A 37-year-old woman has been charged with corruptly bribing the Porirua man. She failed to appear in court.

An inmate is allegedly party to offering that bribe to the Porirua man.

Another two men have been charged with accepting a cash bribe - one worth $150 and one worth $1200 - as an incentive to bring contraband into the prison.

Both men also cannot be named.
