Two in hospital after apartment explosion

Two people are in hospital following an explosion at a central Wellington apartment building.

Emergency services gather on Church street in Wellington after reports of an injury in an apartment explosion. Photo: RNZ/ Samuel Rillstone

Two people are in hospital following an explosion at a central Wellington apartment building.

Police, fire and ambulance were on the scene on Friday afternoon, and both Church Street and Boulcott Street were cordoned off to traffic.

Emergency services were called to the Church Street address at 1pm.

Wellington Free Ambulance said two people were taken to Wellington Hospital, one in a serious condition and one moderate.

Fire and Emergency attended the fire on Church Street in central Wellington. Photo: RNZ/ Samuel Rillstone

The building, which is leased by Kāinga Ora and contains up to 100 apartments, was evacuated.

Kāinga Ora regional director for Greater Wellington Vicki McLaren said they were "very concerned" to learn about reports of the explosion.

"Our immediate priority is checking on the safety and wellbeing of our customers, and we have staff onsite who are ready to offer support once we can access the building or reach the customers by phone."

FENZ assistant commander James Martin-Bond said they received a call from an alarm activation about 1pm.

"Upon arrival we had reports of a fire on level eight. When crews investigated, we found there had been an explosion in one of the apartments."

Police were also at the scene. Photo: RNZ/ Samuel Rillstone

He said eleven crews responded, the whole building was evacuated and they were now investigating the cause of the explosion.

The streets remained cordoned off by 3pm, but emergency services remained on-site.
