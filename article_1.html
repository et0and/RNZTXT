Watch live: Manurewa Marae election inquiry findings released

The inquiry was launched last year after allegations Te Pāti Māori misused data during the 2023 election campaign.

A delayed report into the alleged mis-use of personal information by Manurewa Marae is set to be released.

The review, being released by the Public Service Commission, was commissioned by Prime Minister Christopher Luxon after allegations Census and Covid-19 vaccination data collected at the marae was misused for Te Pāti Māori's election campaign.

Stats NZ and Police were also looking into the claims, with their reports yet to be released.

Te Pāti Māori has denied the allegations.

Te Pāti Māori called the allegations "baseless" and invited the police to investigate and prove their "innocence".

Public Service Commissioner Sir Brian Roche is set to release the findings of the inquiry at 2pm. We will be livestreaming the announcement at the top of this page.
