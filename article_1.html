ECan chair resigns after reports of speeding nearly 700 times in council car

Peter Scott's resignation takes effect from Friday, when councillor Craig Pauling will be acting chair.

Peter Scott has resigned as chair of the Canterbury Regional Council. Photo: RNZ / Philippa Tolley

Environment Canterbury chairperson Peter Scott has resigned.

His departure follows revelations in The Press newspaper that his council-provided car exceeded the speed limit more than 600 times, reaching speeds of 157km/h.

In July, Scott received a formal warning following an independent investigation into his own claims he was operating illegally on a South Canterbury farm.

He released a statement saying that while publicity about his behaviour had not been directly related to his work as chair, he recognised the damage it was doing to the organisation.

Scott's resignation takes effect from Friday, when councillor Craig Pauling will be acting chair.
