Homes without power after fierce storm hits Canterbury

Nearly 100 millimetres have fallen in Banks Peninsula over the past 12 hours.

A flooded street in Christchurch (file photo) Photo: Christchurch City Council

About 14 homes are still without power in Peraki Bay in Banks Peninsula, after torrential rains and gale-force winds lashed Canterbury overnight.

Heavy rain and strong wind watches are in place for parts of Canterbury north of the Rakaia River and the Kaikōura coast to Seddon until 11am.

MetService says the rainfall has caused surface flooding on some southern slopes of Banks Peninsula, but the rain started to ease about 3.30am on Wednesday.

Nearly 100 millimetres have fallen in Banks Peninsula over the past 12 hours.

MetService says the rainband is moving northwards towards Kaikōura and Marlborough.

Fire and Emergency said this morning they had received three reports of tree branches falling in Christchurch and Diamond Harbour. However, they were minor and would be referred to the council.
