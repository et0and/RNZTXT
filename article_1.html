Tension high as college put in lockdown, armed police patrol after threat

Some parents are angry the school was not closed until after students had been dropped off for the day.

Photo: RNZ / Calvin Samuel

An Auckland college was sent into lockdown as police probed a threat against schools in Auckland.

Students arrived at Waiuku College on Wednesday to a heavy police presence, and the school posted on Facebook shortly before 9am advising there was a lockdown under way.

After 11am the school posted on Facebook saying students and staff have returned to their classrooms.

The lockdown was prompted by an email threat, directed at two schools in the region.

The email was also sent to a number of organisations, including RNZ.

It says the school will close for the rest of the day "to allow staff and students to settle their wairua [sense of wellbeing] and prepare to return tomorrow".

The school's reception will remain shut.

The post asked parents not to go on school grounds and not to approach staff, who ii said have managed "incredibly well" this morning.

"We understand what some students have witnessed [armed police on site] can create upset and trauma. Our guidance team are available through teams, email and on-site tomorrow. The MOE [Ministry of Education] response team will also be on site," it said.

Some parents took to social media to complain about the lack of notice over the lockdown.

The front office at Waiuku College was closed on Wednesday. Photo: RNZ / Calvin Samuel

A police spokesperson says they made enquiries in the area concerned and were looking into the email.

They acknowledged the threat was unsettling and said reassurance patrols would be conducted.

The parent of two students at Waiuku College, Petrida Sutton, said tensions were high as parents speculated about the limited information they had received.

"We've heard there are Armed Offenders Squad out there, and police with guns patrolling around the school," she said.

"My son walked to school this morning, he would've got there around 8:30."

Some parents complained on social media that the school had entered lockdown after many children had already arrived, despite the email being sent in the early hours of this morning.

Sutton said her son had been in contact, and her daughter was home sick.

"I have [heard from him], they're not meant to have phones there but I have," she said.

An earlier warning on the website. The lockdown has since ended. Photo: Supplied

Her son had told her via a text the students had been out in a field and now they were back in the classroom.

"I'm reluctant to go to work until I know what's going on, I'm grateful that my daughter's unwell and not at school today."

An earlier update on the school's website about 10.30am said it was still in lockdown.

"We are under the direction of the NZ police. Please do not contact the school or attempt to visit at this stage."

In a statement, the Ministry of Education said the school had activated its emergency management procedures.

It said the immediate priority was the safety and wellbeing of students and staff.
