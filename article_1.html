Live: Canoe sprinters begin heats

The men's K4 500 are about to take to the water with Dame Lisa Carrington and the women's team close behind. Follow RNZ's blog for updates.

Max Brown at the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games on 5 August, 2021. Photo: Photosport / Steve McArthur

After gold for Finn Butcher in the kayak cross on day 10, there is more action in the water with the canoe sprints on Tuesday night.

The canoe sprints kicks off at 7.30pm with New Zealand's men's team taking part in the K4 500m heats. At 8pm, its the women's turn where Lisa Carrington, Aotearoa's most decorated Olympian, Alicia Hoskin, and Aimee Fisher and Lucy Matehaere in action.

Each of the quarterfinals follow later in the evening if the New Zealand paddlers qualify.

In other highlights coming up, first-time Olympian and middle-distance runner Maia Ramsden will be competing in round one of the 1500m at 8.05pm.

Climber Julian David, who won the men's and women's titles at the IFSC sport climbing oceania qualifier in Melbourne last November, is set for the seeding round at 11pm.
