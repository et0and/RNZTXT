Lower Hutt schools locked down, armed police in area

Local schools have been locked down as a precaution as police respond to an incident in Taitā.

Photo: RNZ / Patrice Allen

Police say they are responding to an incident in the Lower Hutt suburb of Taitā and say schools in the area have been locked down as a precaution.

Cordons are in place on High Street and residents in the area have been asked to stay inside or avoid the area until the situation has been resolved.

The Armed Offenders Squad is heading there, police said.

A resident says the police told them a person had a weapon, according to RNZ's reporter at the scene.

A helicopter could be heard flying overhead in Avalon.

A police traffic cordon was in place on High Street where the road intersects with Te Ara O Motutawa road.

Businesses near the cordon said police had not explained what was happening but officers were on the road holding guns.

One shop worker said traffic was being turned around and rerouted to avoid the scene.

Police said the situation was currently controlled and there was no threat to Naenae College which has gone into lockdown.

Schools which have confirmed they are in lockdown are Avalon Intermediate School, Naenae College and Naenae Intermediate.

Te Kura o Kimi Ora is also in lockdown and has contacted parents to let them know not to collect their children.
