Olympics Live: Three NZ women in pole vault final

Eliza McCartney, Olivia McTaggart and Imogen Ayris have all qualified for Thursday's medal event.

Olivia McTaggart during the qualifying round of the women's pole vault. Photo: BEN STANSALL / AFP

New Zealand's women pole vaulters have all qualified for Thursday's final at the Stade de France.

Earlier, Eliza McCartney cleared 4.55m on her second attempt to go through.

Olivia McTaggart and Imogen Ayris missed that height, but go through to the final by virtue of clearing 4.40m on their first attempt.

In the women's gymnastics, Italy's Alice D'amato was a surprise gold medallist in the beam.

Simone Biles - chasing her eighth title - fell off during her difficult routine and was relegated to fifth.

Meanwhile, Connor Bell missed out on a place in the final by the narrowest of margins.

Samoa's Alex Rose grabs the last spot with the same distance as Bell - 62.88m - but he achieved that mark with his first attempt, while Bell did it with his second.

Earlier, the mixed triathlon team - Dylan McCullough, Nicole van der Kaay, Ainsley Thorpe and Hayden Wilde - were out of contention from the start, when Hayden Wilde collided with a French athlete in the opening cycle leg.

In the early hours of Tuesday, Luuka Jones and Finn Butcher are in the kayak cross quarter-finals.
