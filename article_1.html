Evacuations due to large blaze fuelled by tree stumps, car wrecks

Some properties south of a large fire in Amberley are being evacuated, Fire and Emergency Canterbury says.

File photo. Photo: RNZ / Robin Martin

Some properties south of a large fire in Amberley are being evacuated, Fire and Emergency Canterbury says.

The properties are on Racecourse Road and they are threatened by a blaze in among vegetation which is being fuelled by tree stumps, tyres, grass and car wrecks.

FENZ has called in helicopters to help firefighters dealing with the fire which is affecting an area about 1km long and 500 metres wide.

The Hurunui District Council has asked those being evacuated to make their way to Hurunui Library on carters Rd in the town.

"There will be further updates about an evacuation location as soon as possible. Thank you for your cooperation and patience," it said in a post on social media.

The council has also appealed to residents in both Amberley and Loburn to limit their water use and not to turn on appliances such as washing machines.

"We have diverted extra water supply to allow the emergency crews to fight the fire."

FENZ has appealed to people to stay away from the scene of the fire so they do not hamper efforts to bring it under control.
