Damaging winds, hail, intense rain set to hit Gisborne

A severe thunderstorm warning has been issued for the east coast, with a warning heavy rain could bring flash floods.

Northland and the east coast may both be hit by thunderstorms. Photo: Unsplash / Daoudi Aissa

A severe thunderstorm warning has been issued for Gisborne, with a warning heavy rain, winds and hail could cause damage in the area.

MetService said the storm would be near Gisborne and Whangara on Tuesday from about 2pm.

"These thunderstorms are expected to be accompanied by very heavy rain, damaging wind gusts and hail."

The forecaster warned the conditions could cause flash floods around streams, gullies and urban areas.

The National Emergency Management Agency warned that people should take shelter away from windows as the storm approaches, get back to land and secure loose objects.

Drivers were warned to be aware of fallen trees and powerlines.

Separate thunderstorm watches remained in place for Northland, Bay of Plenty and Hawke's Bay.

Frequent rain and thunder looked likely in Northland on Tuesday, with localised downpours in the afternoon set to bring up to 40mm of rain an hour at times.
