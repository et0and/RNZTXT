Clog roads and crashes as New Year's traffic builds up

Commuters heading north from Auckland are in for a slow drive.

File photo: Traffic is building for New Year's. Photo: RNZ / Marika Khabazi

Commuters heading from Auckland to Northland via State Highway One are in for a slow drive.

Traffic was heavy before and through Warkworth, then again from Wayby through Wellsford up to Te Hana.

For holidaymakers going south, Auckland's Southern Motorway southbound was heavy from Manukau to Drury.

Meanwhile, there have been multiple crashes throughout the country.

Remutaka Hill has reopened in both directions after a three-vehicle crash that left one car on fire. Three people received minor to moderate injuries.

A three-way car crash on Remutaka Hill on 27 December, 2023. Photo: Supplied / Jeff Ng

A crash on Haywards Hill Road in Wellington has also closed the road to eastbound traffic.

The New Zealand Transport Agency said the crash was after the intersection with Mt Cecil Rd.

The northbound lane of SH1 south of Sanson was closed after a campervan crashed near Rongotea just after midday.

Police said no one has been injured.

SH1 near Rowe Road was closed to northbound traffic and while southbound lanes remain open, the Transport Agency said drivers should expect delays.
