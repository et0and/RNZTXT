Former All Black Julian Savea to play for Samoa?

Savea has a point to prove with his new Super Rugby Pacific side, with a return to international rugby on the horizon.

Julian Savea has left the Hurricanes for Moana Pasifika. Photo: PHOTOSPORT

Julian Savea is getting familiar with his new home at Moana Pasifika.

"I was pretty anxious at the start, but the boys have been welcoming," the former All Black winger said.

"The pleasing thing is that Tana [Umaga] has set the tone around our expectations and our standards."

And Savea said his personal goal is to "prove that I've still got it".Â 

"I'm not finished yet. I still want to be the best player in the comp."

Savea departed the Hurricanes last year after playing 153 games for the franchise - their most-capped player.

As a team, he'd like Moana to win games and place in the top eight.

"It's within our grasp. Last year the boys didn't manage to get a [win], but we are looking forward to that challenge."

The 33-year-old is also keen to take on a mentoring role, "bringing up the young boys in here".

The ex-Hurricane told the young athletes: "Once you're here, there is a reason why you're here - and the hardest part is staying here.

"I want to be approachable and make sure they can come to me about anything, whether it's rugby-related or off the field."

Now eligible to play for Manu Samoa, Savea said there were chats with head coach Seilala Mapusua prior to the Rugby World Cup.

"I was very close, but I got injured. I didn't want to go over injured and potentially take someone's spot."

The winger said he was sure there would be more discussions around Manu Samoa, but nothing has been confirmed so far.

The Luatuanuu, Siumu and Falesiu Uta athlete says being able to embrace his Samoan culture has been "different but it's nice".

"It's been eye-opening - especially when they cater to your needs, you become a lot happier."

Savea said that he was not a superstitious player when it comes to preparations before a game. However, he does like listening to slow jams like Mariah Carey's 'Always Be My Baby'.

"It gets me up sometimes."

Moana Pasifika open their season against Highlanders on 24 February.
