Explosions heard as large fire breaks out in south Auckland

Thick black smoke "filled the air" and "endless sirens" could be heard, witness says.

A large fire has broken out in Auckland's Takanini. Photo: Supplied / Ooh Lala Styling

A large fire has broken out in Auckland's Takanini.

Lala, who lives in nearby Parehurehure, said she could hear "very loud explosions" and see the flames getting larger and brighter on Sunday night.

"Thick smoke filled the air very quickly.

"It took less than 15 minute for the smoke to turn black and reach our area just above our roof.

"We could hear endless sirens and helicopters making their way to the scene."

Thick black smoke could be seen billowing across the area. Photo: Supplied / Ooh Lala Styling

The fire was believed to be on Tironui Road and was spotted about 11pm.

Lala said the fire had "definitely" got smaller by midnight, and looked as if it had been contained.

"There's currently lots of flashing red fire truck lights."

Fire and Emergency NZ has been contacted for comment.
