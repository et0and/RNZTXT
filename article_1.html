Live updates: National Anzac Day Service in Wellington cancelled

The 11am wreath laying service at Pukeahu National War Memorial Park has been cancelled due to high winds.

The Ministry of Culture and Heritage has pulled the plug on two services for Anzac Day in the capital over safety concerns for veterans and visitors due to strong winds.

The 11am wreath laying service at Pukeahu National War Memorial Park has been cancelled, as has the Ataturk Memorial wreath laying service in Strathmore.

MetService expects winds of up to 109km/h in central Wellington.

Ministry for Culture and Heritage chief executive Leauanae Laulu Mac Leauanae said: "It is very unfortunate that we have had to cancel today's service due to high winds."

A reduced service is going ahead for those people organised to lay wreaths at the Tomb of the Unknown Warrior.
