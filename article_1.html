Person arrested after Albert Park incident causes traffic chaos in Auckland

Police say a person made a "number of concerning comments" prompting an emergency response.

Streets have been cordoned off nearby Albert Park as police respond to an incident. Photo: RNZ / Jordan Dunn

A person has been taken into custody after an incident at Auckland's Albert Park that shut down roads and saw buildings evacuated.

Charges were being considered, police said.

Earlier they said officers were responding after a person made a number of concerning comments.

Albert Park, and buildings on Princess Street and Bowen lane were evacuated as a precaution, they said.

Nearby office buildings have been evacuated. Photo: RNZ / Jordan Dunn

Streets immediately surrounding the park were also cordoned off.

All cordons surrounding the park have now been lifted and there was no ongoing risk to the public, police said.

Traffic is backed up on Wellesley Street East and High St, with traffic also blocked down Wellesley St.

The Wellesley Street off-ramp left turn has also been closed, NZTA said.

A number of bus routes have been put detours. They are: 933, 931, Outer Link, 75, 101, 939.

Dozens of people wait it out on High Street as police respond to the nearby incident. Photo: RNZ / Jordan Dunn

Police will be visible in the area while the incident is resolved.
