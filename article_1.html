Live: Government must ensure Bluebridge has competitor

One of the KiwiRail shareholding ministers says there are good reasons for the InterIslander ferry service to stay in government hands.

Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

One of the KiwiRail shareholding ministers says there are good reasons for the InterIslander ferry service to stay in government hands.

The coalition government last year canned a replacement project for the ageing fleet, but it has also lambasted the way KiwiRail, which owns InterIslander, has maintained the vessels.

After the Aratere grounding on Friday, Picton locals have been asking whether it's time for another operator to take over.

Nicola Willis told First Up the government must ensure the privately run Bluebridge service has a competitor.

The government also must ensure there is enough capacity to ship freight and passengers between the North and South Islands, she said.

The minister has committed to buying replacement ferries, but would not give a date.
