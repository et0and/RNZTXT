All Blacks 31, Wallabies 28 in first Bledisoe Cup test

The All Blacks headed into today's opening Bledisloe Cup encounter with a mission to get their season back on track.

Jordie Barrett in possession. Photo: David Neilson / Photosport

The All Blacks have survived a late scare from the Wallabies, to win 31-28 in Sydney and secure the Bledisloe Cup for the 21st year on the trot.

While the first half was vintage All Blacks, the second summed up the struggles of the 2024 side.

New Zealand ran in three tries in the opening 15 minutes, and at halftime led the Wallabies 28-14, but managed just three points in the second half as Australia stormed back to trail by just three with 30 seconds remaining.
