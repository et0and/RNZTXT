Auckland Muslim schools in lockdown after threat

Al-Madinah School and Zayed College schools have shut after threat.

Photo: Supplied / Google Maps

Al-Madinah School and Zayed College, both on Westney Road in Mangere, are in lockdown after a suspicious call.

In a post on social media, Al-Madinah School asked parents not to send their children there until further notice.

The state integrated area school has primary and secondary age pupils.

Zayed College said on its website that it was closed Monday as a matter of caution following suspicious call to Al-Madinah.

Zayed College for Girls. Photo: Supplied / Google Maps

"If your daughter or family member is currently at school, we are in full lockdown and will communicate with you as soon as we can," commissioner Linley Myers said.

Zayed College said it was the first Islamic College for girls in New Zealand, it was a state integrated special character Islamic secondary school for girls Year 7 - 13.
