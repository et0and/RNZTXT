Long delays on Coromandel road after fatal crash

A major road on the Coromandel Peninsula has been closed after a fatal crash, and travellers are being told to take other routes.

The crash was between Te Puru and Valder Place, Waiomu, on the west side of the Coromandel Peninsula, police said. Photo: Supplied/ NZTA

A major road on the Coromandel Peninsula has been closed after a fatal crash, and travellers are being told to take other routes.

There is a 90-minute delay for travel on State Highway 25A in the Coromandel after a fatal accident earlier today.

Emergency services responded to a crash on State Highway 25 between Thames and Coromandel town, reported just before 9am on Friday.

One person was dead after the two-car crash, police said.

St John said it treated four patients with minor injuries at the scene.

Police said at 3pm that traffic was building up in the area and the road remains closed.

"Motorists are asked to avoid travelling in the next few hours on SH25a due to the current 90 minute delay on this route," police said.

The delay is between Kopu and Tairua and motorists are asked to postpone their travel if possible.

The narrow coastal road winds up the west side of the Coromandel Peninsula and is heavily used during the peak summer holiday period.
