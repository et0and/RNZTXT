Economy shrinks 0.3 percent for quarter

Weak manufacturing and exports drove the decline, while household spending was also weaker.

Seasonally-adjusted gross domestic product fell 0.3 percent in the three months ended September, Stats NZ said. Photo: 123RF

The economy shrank in the third quarter as weakening household spending and manufacturing hit activity.

Stats NZ said seasonally-adjusted gross domestic product fell 0.3 percent in the three months ended September, while growth was revised by nearly half to 0.5 percent growth in the previous quarter.

Weak manufacturing and exports drove the decline, while household spending was also weaker.

The economy shrank by 0.6 percent compared to the same quarter a year ago.
