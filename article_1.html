Live: Kiwi paddlers target more Olympic medals

After gold for Finn Butcher in the kayak cross earlier today, more action this evening on the water includes Lisa Carrington in the canoe sprints. Follow RNZ's blog for updates.

Photo: PhotoSport / Steve McArthur

After gold for Finn Butcher in the kayak cross on day 10, there is more action in the water with the canoe sprints on Tuesday night.

The canoe sprints get underway at 7.30pm but New Zealand's first paddlers are not on until Max Brown and Grant Clancy in the men's C2 500m heats at 8.30pm, followed by Kurtis Imrie and Hamish Legarth in the men's K2 500m heat at 9.30pm.

At 10.10pm, the women's K2 500m heats will see Lisa Carrington, Aotearoa's most decorated Olympian, Alicia Hoskin, and Aimee Fisher and Lucy Matehaere in action.

Each of the quarterfinals follow later in the evening if the New Zealand paddlers qualify.

In other highlights coming up, first-time Olympian and middle-distance runner Maia Ramsden will be competing in round one of the 1500m at 8.05pm.

New Zealand's first woman to wrestle at the Olympic games, Tayla Ford, will be in action in the women's 68kg freestyle repechage at 9pm.

Climber Julian David, who won the men's and women's titles at the IFSC sport climbing oceania qualifier in Melbourne last November, is set for the seeding round at 11pm.
