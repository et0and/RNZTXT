Every month waiting for new drugs means more deaths - Bowel Cancer chairperson

Live: The government says new cancer treatments will be rolled out from October, but some want faster access.

Health Minister Dr Shane Reti. Photo: RNZ/Nick Monro

Some of the newly funded cancer drugs should be available in the next three months.

But it could be more than a year before all the newly-funded treatments are on the market.

On Monday, the government confirmed $604 million over four years in extra Pharmac funding to cover up to 54 new medicines, including 26 cancer drugs.

Cancer patients and advocates have told RNZ they want access as soon as possible.
