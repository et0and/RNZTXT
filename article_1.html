Tension high as college put in lockdown, armed police patrol schools after threat

An email was sent to a number of organisations, including RNZ.

Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

Waiuku College says it has gone into lockdown, as police investigate an email threat made to two Auckland schools.

The school is urging people not to contact or visit the premises.

The email was sent to a number of organisations, including RNZ.

Photo: Supplied

A police spokesperson says they made enquiries in the area concerned and are looking into the email.

They acknowledged the threat was unsettling and said reassurance patrols would be conducted.

The parent of two students at Waiuku College, Petrida Sutton, said tensions were high as parents speculated about the limited information they had received.

"We've heard there are Armed Offenders Squad out there, and police with guns patrolling around the school," she said.

"My son walked to school this morning, he would've got there around 8:30."

Some parents complained on social media that the school had entered lockdown after many children had already arrived, despite the email being sent in the early hours of this morning.

Sutton said her son had been in contact, and her daughter was home sick.

"I have [heard from him], they're not meant to have phones there but I have," she said.

Her son had told her via a text the students had been out in a field and now they were back in the classroom.

"I'm reluctant to go to work until I know what's going on, I'm grateful that my daughter's unwell and not at school today."

In a statement, the Ministry of Education said the school has activated its emergency management procedures.

It said the school leadership team will continue to provide updates and the immediate priority was the safety and wellbeing of students and staff.
