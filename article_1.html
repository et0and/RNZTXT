Roads closed, smoke billows as large fire erupts at car workshop

Residents are being warned to stay inside if possible as smoke drifts over three Christchurch suburbs.

Fire broke out at called to Auto Inspection Services on Colombo St on Thursday morning. Photo: RNZ / Anna Sargent

A large fire at a car workshop in Christchurch's Sydenham, has caused smoke to billow across the city.

Emergency services were called to Auto Inspection Services on Colombo St shortly before 4.30am on Thursday, a Fire and Emergency NZ spokesperson said.

The large fire was still burning, the spokesperson said and part of Colombo and Durham St, around the building were closed and would remain so for some time.

Eleven fire engines were at the scene, the spokesperson said. A police car and ambulance were also at the site, an RNZ reporter said.

The smoke drift was affecting the Sydenham, Addington and Spreydon area, Fire and Emergency said.

Photo: Screengrab / RNZ

It issued an emergency alert warning people in to stay indoors if possible, keep doors and windows closed and be mindful about using air condititioning units.

"If you have any health concerns please call Healthline or 111 in an emergency".

At this stage it was too early to say what had caused the fire, the spokesperson said.

It was believed it would take some time to extinguish the fire due to the size of the building.

No injuries have been reported.
