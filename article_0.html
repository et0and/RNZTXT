Live: Lisa Carrington and Aimee Fisher cruise through to semifinals

Just three times in Olympic history have two New Zealanders stood on different levels of the podium for the same event. Dame Lisa and Fisher could make it happen again.

Lisa Carrington, left and Aimee Fisher embrace after the Women's K1 500m Canoe Word Championship Qualification Race 3 at Lake Karapiro in Waikato in April 2022. Photo: Photosport

World-class athletes Aimee Fisher and Dame Lisa Carrington won their kayak sprints heats and will head into the semifinals at the Paris Olympics.

Just three times in Olympic history have two New Zealanders stood on different levels of the podium for the same event. Dame Lisa and Fisher could make it happen again.

The pole-vaulting trio of Eliza McCartney, Olivia McTaggart and Imogen Ayris will chase Olympic glory on Wednesday night, with all three in the Paris Olympic Games final.

The women's team pursuit are also on track for gold at the velodrome.

Ally Wollaston, Bryony Botha, Emily Shearman and Nicole Shields broke the national record and were just 0.4s off the world record in being top qualifiers and advancing to a semi-final against Italy.
