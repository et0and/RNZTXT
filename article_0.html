Live: Who will take home the Silver Scroll Award?

Stars are arriving for Aotearoa's most prestigious songwriting award ceremony, with the top five finalists in for a chance of winning for the first time. Video

The top five finalists for the APRA Silver Scroll Award have a chance of winning the prize for the first time.

They are Stan Walker (for 'I Am'), SKILAA (for 'Jenny Greenteeth'), Anna Coddington (for Kātuarehe'), Georgia Lines (for 'The Letter'), and Mermaidens (for 'I Like to Be Alone').

Walker is also a top three contender for the APRA Maioha Award, which recognises contemporary Māori music and songwriters telling Te Ao Māori stories. His song 'Māori Ki Te Ao' is a celebration of his Tūhoe identity.

The other finalists for this award are TAWAZ for 'He Aho' and Jordyn with a Why for 'He Rei Niho'.

The winners are determined by the votes of New Zealand APRA members (who are also songwriters, producers and performers).

Ahead of the awards ceremony in Pōneke Wellington, the five finalists told Music 101 the stories behind their nominated songs.
