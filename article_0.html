One dead after Tesla Cybertruck explodes outside Trump Hotel in Las Vegas

There was one dead person inside the Cybertruck, a Las Vegas sheriff says.

By Chelsea Bailey for CNN

A Tesla Cybertruck displayed at a Tesla dealership on 20 December 2024 in Corte Madera, California. Photo: AFP / JUSTIN SULLIVAN

Las Vegas authorities are investigating a Tesla Cybertruck explosion and fire at the entrance of the Trump International Hotel.

A vehicle fire was reported at the hotel just after 8.40am Wednesday local time (5.40am Thursday NZT) and crews arrived to find a 2024 Cybertruck engulfed in flames, Las Vegas sheriff Kevin McMahill said in a news conference.

"There is one deceased individual inside the Cybertruck and I don't know whether it is a male or a female at this time," McMahill said.

Seven other victims sustained what McMahill described as "minor" injuries as a result of the explosion.

Jeremy Schwartz, acting special agent in charge of the FBI in Las Vegas, also confirmed during the news conference that his team is involved in the investigation.

CNN has reached out to Tesla for comment on the incident.

Eric Trump, executive vice president of the Trump organisation, posted on X that the "safety and well-being of our guests and staff remain our top priority."

"We extend our heartfelt gratitude to the Las Vegas Fire Department and local law enforcement for their swift response and professionalism," Trump wrote.

Tesla chief executive Elon Musk said the whole Tesla senior team was investigating this on X.

"We've never seen anything like this," he wrote.
