Live: Evacuations under way as heavy rain lashes Tai Rāwhiti, Hawkes Bay

People in Te Karaka are being asked to relocate as the Waipaoa River rises, and police are evacuating residents in Haumoana, Hawke's Bay.

Photo: Metservice/screenshot

Evacuations are under way in parts of Tai Rāwhiti, with police going door to door, as heavy rains and wind continue to lash the district.

Extra police have been sent to help move the 700 people who live at Te Karaka, 30km from Gisborne, after the Waipaoa River topped 7.68 metres. It will be inaccessible once the river reaches 8 metres.

In Hawke's Bay, police are knocking on doors and evacuating residents in Haumoana due to costal inundation.

Civil Defence has declared a state of emergency in the Heretaunga Ward in Hastings District, as the region is also battered with heavy rain.
