Live updates: Commuters warned to expect disruption from National Māori Action Day protests.

The planned demonstrations are in response to Te Pāti Māori's call for action against the new government's policies on co-governance and the Treaty.

Police say they will be doing what they can this morning to stop protests around the North Island blocking roads and disrupting people getting to work.

The demonstrations are in response to Te Pāti Māori's call for action against the new government's policies on co-governance and the Treaty.

Follow the latest with RNZ's live updates:

The protests are in 12 towns and cities with people gathering at 7am near on-and-off ramps on Auckland's three motorways. They are also planning to disrupt traffic going through Wellington's Terrace tunnel.

Other cities include Tauranga, Rotorua, Palmerston North and Whangārei.

Police say in some places they will stop protesters putting themselves and drivers in harm's way but expect there will be disruption.

Auckland Mayor Wayne Brown is urging the demonstrators to be respectful, safe, and to consider the needs of everybody.
