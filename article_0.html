Watch live: James Shaw delivers valedictory speech

The former Greens co-leader is set to make his farewell to politics after 10 years.

Former Greens co-leader James Shaw is set to make his farewell speech after 10 years in Parliament.

Shaw stepped down as co-leader in January, but remained at Parliament as an MP to try and shepherd a member's bill through the House.

The bill - which would have changed the Bill of Rights to include the right to a sustainable environment - did not pass its first reading.

Shaw was climate change minister in the Labour led-governments from 2017 to 2023.

In an exit interview with RNZ this week, Shaw summarised his time in politics: "It's been the greatest privilege of my life, it really has, and it was the hardest thing I've ever done, and probably will ever do, and by the end of it I was not in great shape."
