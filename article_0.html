Live: Women's kayak sprint duo paddling for gold

Dame Lisa Carrington and Alicia Hoskin are favourites in the race of the night: the women's K2 final. Follow RNZ's live updates.

New Zealand's Lisa Carrington (L) and New Zealand's Alicia Hoskin compete in the women's kayak double 500m semifinal of the canoe sprint competition at Vaires-sur-Marne Nautical Stadium in Vaires-sur-Marne during the Paris 2024 Olympic Games. Photo: BERTRAND GUAY / AFP

Fresh from claiming gold in the women's kayak fours on Thursday, Dame Lisa Carrington and Alicia Hoskin are back on the water on Friday night for the women's 500m kayak doubles.

They won their semi-final, crossing the line ahead of Germany and the Netherlands, and race in the final at 11.10pm for New Zealand's first chance of a medal on Friday night.

The men's K2 team - Kurtis Imrie and Hamish Legarth - are out of medal contention after finishing seventh in their semifinal.

Meanwhile, Lydia Ko has returned to Le Golf National for the third day of the women's individual golf tournament, after surging up the leaderboard and into medal contention on Thursday.

After 4am, cyclists Bryony Botha and Emily Shearman are back in action in the velodrome in the women's madison final.

They have already won silver in Paris for the women's team pursuit and will be looking to add to their haul.
