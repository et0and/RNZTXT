Watch: PM speaks from CHOGM in Samoa

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon, in Samoa for this year's Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting, is set to speak to the media.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon, currently in Samoa for this year's Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting (CHOGM), has spoken to media.

Luxon left for the Pacific state on Thursday morning, where he will talk to other leaders at Apia and Mulifanua on topics such as the climate, the Middle East and small nations on the world stage.

This is the 27th CHOGM, and the first to be held in a Pacific state outside of New Zealand.

Christopher and Amanda Luxon greeted by NZ High Commissioner Si'alei van Toor and Second Secretary Jonathan Lee on arrival to Apia for CHOGM. Photo: Russell Palmer / RNZ

"CHOGM is a fantastic opportunity to connect with other Commonwealth leaders," Luxon said earlier this week.

"In these uncertain geopolitical times, it is important to meet face-to-face to talk about how we can be more resilient in the face of global challenges."

King Charles III and Queen Camilla also arrived earlier on Thursday. Charles is expected to speak on Friday.

Christopher and Amanda Luxon greeted by NZ High Commissioner Si'alei van Toor and Second Secretary Jonathan Lee on arrival to Apia for CHOGM. Photo: Russell Palmer / RNZ

The event is being held under the cloud of the sinking of the New Zealand Defence Force vessel the Manawanui.
