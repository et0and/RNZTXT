Watch live: Prime Minister Christopher Luxon speaks from Queenstown

Earlier today it was revealed Luxon will be claiming $52,000 to cover his accommodation expenses for an apartment he owns mortgage-free.

Earlier today it was revealed Prime Minister Christopher Luxon is collecting a $52,000 accommodation supplement to live in his own mortgage-free home in Wellington.

A $31,000 supplement is available for all MPs living away from home when in Wellington, but the Prime Minister's is a higher subsidy that tops up his $471,000 salary.

The Prime Minister campaigned on wasteful public spending and a need for cost cutting across the public service and under the coalition government ministries have been asked to find at least 6.5 percent of savings.

Prime Ministers have Premier House available to live in but Luxon was choosing not to due to long-standing maintenance issues.
