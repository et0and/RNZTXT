Watch live: Politicians welcomed to Rātana Pā

MPs are arriving at Rātana Pā for speeches and celebrations commemorating the church's founder, and the unofficial start of the political year.

Politicians are arriving at Rātana Pā in Whanganui for the annual celebrations commemorating the church's founder.

Typically considered the first main event on the year's political calendar, the Rātana celebrations this year carry the mauri of the Hui aa Motu called by the Kiingitanga.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon has said he is expecting to be challenged at the event.

Opposition representatives are expected to be welcomed with a pōwhiri in the morning, with another pōwhiri for government politicians in the early afternoon.

All parties in Parliament other than ACT are attending.
