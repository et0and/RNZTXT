Former All Black Norm Hewitt dies at age 55

Hewitt was also a stalwart of Hawke's Bay, Hurricanes and Māori All Blacks rugby, and won Dancing with the Stars.

All Blacks hooker Norm Hewitt walks from the field after the All Blacks v England match on 6 December 1997. Photo: Photosport / Andrew Cornaga

Former All Blacks hooker and Dancing with the Stars winner Norm Hewitt has died, aged 55.

Hewitt, who was understood to have motor neurone disease, died overnight.

Hewitt won the first season of Dancing with the Stars in 2005 and played for the All Blacks from 1993 to 1998 in 14 matches, nine of which were tests.

He was a stalwart of Hawke's Bay, Hurricanes and Māori rugby.

He played initially during the Sean Fitzpatrick era in the All Blacks, which limited his number of appearances.

A dynamic, combative player on the field, he had problems off it with alcohol which resulted in a few high-profile incidents.

But he later became a vocal advocate over the following decades for changing drinking habits.
