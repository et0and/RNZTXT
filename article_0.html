Barry Young, accused of stealing Te Whatu Ora Covid-19 data, denied bail

The man accused of stealing Te Whatu Ora Covid-19 data and using it to spread disinformation will stay behind bars after he was denied bail today.

Barry Young. Photo: RNZ / Ruth Hill

The man accused of stealing Te Whatu Ora Covid-19 data and using it to spread disinformation will stay behind bars after he was denied.

Barry Young, 56, appeared in the Wellington District Court, charged with accessing a computer system for dishonest purposes.

Te Whatu Ora says an employee - who has no clinical background - downloaded data, which was published on an overseas website and did an interview alleging a cover-up of vaccine-related deaths.

Young's case was pushed back to Monday afternoon after police announced they opposed bail, claiming a possible risk of interference to evidence.

Police prosecutor Lydia McIvor told the court that police were in the process of downloading a large amount of information from cloud storage but that process would not be completed until tomorrow.

She said there was a risk that if released on bail, Young could have an opportunity to interfere with that process.

HIs lawyer, Matthew Hague, argued there was no evidence he would interfere with the process, but the court could manage that risk with bail conditions, such as a 48-hour ban on using the internet.

However, Judge Andrew Nicholls said he was satisfied there was a risk that could not be managed, and Young would be released automatically in the afternoon.
