Live: Cyclists hunting more medals on last day of Olympics

Ellesse Andrews, Ally Wollaston and Sam Dakin are back in the velodrome for one last tilt at gold. Follow RNZ's live updates.

Ally Wollaston is riding for a medal in the women' omnium. Photo: SWpix.com

The last day of the 2024 Summer Olympics is here, with the final competitions running through to about midnight, before the closing ceremony kicks off at 7am Monday NZT.

Long-distance runner Camille French contests the women's marathon from 6pm, before cyclists Ally Wollaston, Ellesse Andrews and Sam Dakin take to the velodrome.

Both Wollaston and Andrews have already bagged medals at these Games - Wollaston holds a silver for the women's team cycling pursuit, while Andrews has silver and gold from the team sprints and the keirin, respectively - and are looking to clinch more.

Sam Dakin is in the keirin quarterfinals at 9.29pm.
