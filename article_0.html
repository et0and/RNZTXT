Live weather updates: More torrential rain to come for West Coast

There are eight orange heavy rain warnings in the South Island and one in Taranaki.

The Waiho River sitting above 8m on 10 April as rain hit the West Coast. Photo: RNZ/Nathan Mckinnon

The West Coast is today expected to get the worst of the torrential rain that has been drenching parts of the region since Tuesday morning.

MetService said up to 450mm of rain hit Westland on Wednesday and another 350mm is forecast by 9pm on Thursday.

There was still a chance the current orange warning could be upgraded to red, it said.

Despite a lull in the rain late on Wednesday, West Coast Civil Defence is urging people not to become complacent, and to take weather warnings seriously.

SH6 from Franz Josef to Makarora - the only direct route between the West Coast and Otago - was closed overnight.

The Waiho River at SH6 is back up to 8m breaching an alert level.

There are now eight orange heavy rain warnings in the South Island, and one in Taranaki which ends on Friday morning.
