Olympics live: 'The trifecta is incredible' - NZ jumps up the medal table

Kiwis in Paris are celebrating a "pretty spectacular" hour for rowing, after three back-to-back medals.

From left: Ollie Maclean, Tom Murray, Matt Macdonald and Logan Ullrich from New Zealand. Photo: Iain McGregor / www.photosport.nz

It was "pretty spectacular" to be part of such a magical hour for rowing, Ollie Maclean says.

Maclean was a member of the men's coxless four team that took silver at the Vaires-sur-Marne course.

Women's double sculls team Brooke Francis and Lucy Spoors won gold in their event, while the women's four took bronze.

"Having the two mums lead that as well with the gold medal that was a huge confidence booster for us. To get the trifecta today is just incredible."

It was daunting and added pressure to be the last race of the day, however, pressure helps him perform better, Maclean believes.

Meanwhile, the men's and women's medal races in the skiff class were postponed due to light, shifty conditions in Marseille.

New Zealand duo Isaac McHardie and Will McKenzie are in bronze position heading into the double-points medal race. But with 12 points separating second to seventh, several crews are in with a shot of snatching a medal.
