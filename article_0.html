Olympics live: NZ jumps up medal table after rowing triple

A big day on the water in Paris has more than doubled New Zealand's Olympic medal tally, adding a gold, a silver and a bronze.

A successful day on the water in Paris has more than doubled New Zealand's Olympic medal tally, adding a gold, a silver and a bronze.

New Zealand sit 12th on the medal table with two golds, two silvers and a bronze.

China top the table with 11 gold, followed by the United States, then hosts France and Australia are fourth.

Last night women's double sculls team Brooke Francis and Lucy Spoors won gold in their event, while the women's four took bronze.

It was "pretty spectacular" to be part of such a magical hour for rowing, Ollie Maclean says.

Maclean was a member of the men's coxless four team that took silver at the Vaires-sur-Marne course.
