Live: Fairweather emotional after placing fourth in 'race of the century'

Erika Fairweather has narrowly missed out on winning New Zealand's first medal. Stay up to date on the latest with RNZ's live blog.

Photo: AFP/JONATHAN NACKSTRAND

The first official day of the 2024 Olympic Games has begun in Paris, following a colourful opening ceremony on the banks of the River Seine.

Erika Fairweather has narrowly missed out on winning New Zealand's first medal at the Paris Olympics after finishing fourth in the women's 400-metre freestyle final.

Fairweather was aiming to become the first New Zealand woman in 72 years to win an Olympic medal in the pool.

In a high-profile final, she was beaten home by Australian Ariarne Titmus, who won gold from Canadian Summer McIntosh, with American great Katie Ledecky third.

The schedule for the first day is jam-packed, with New Zealand athletes competing in sports as varied as equestrian dressage, canoe slalom, surfing, rugby sevens and freestyle swimming.
