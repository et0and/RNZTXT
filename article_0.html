'I welcome his intention to bring in an observer' - Wellington mayor

Tory Whanau says her council will work collaboratively with the government, after its decision to appoint a Crown Observer.

Wellington mayor Tory Whanau says her council will work collaboratively with the government, after its decision to appoint a Crown Observer.

Local government minister Simeon Brown announced his plans this afternoon, citing concern around the impact of council decisions on rates.

Whanau says it's been a tough couple of years, but the council is committed to delivering the best long term plan it can.

She says there has been a lot of media attention and political theatre around the decision, but the council needs assistance, not punching down.

The council now has 10 days to respond to the minister, who says he will take its view into account before he appoints an observer.
