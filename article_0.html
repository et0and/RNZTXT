Watch live: Luxon attends 'special' Matariki ceremony

The national observance is being hosted by Ngāi Tahu at the Treble Cone skifield near Wānaka.

The national observance of Matariki is being held on Friday morning at Treble Cone skifield near Wānaka.

About 200 people were gathering above the clouds, including Prime Minister Christopher Luxon and other dignitaries.

Luxon said it was a great honour to attend the "wonderful" ceremony .

"That was very, very special," he told RNZ's Matariki show.

The holiday was an important chance to stop, and to reflect on the people lost over the year, he said.

"A moment to take a beat to think about the year ahead."

The theme for this year's celebrations was Matariki Heri Kai, the Feast of Matariki. It is a call for people to think not only about their kai, but the environment it came from.

There are also other commemorations happening around Aotearoa.
