Search for person missing from boat in Auckland's Manukau Harbour finished for night

Two people were on board, Coastguard says. One swam to shore and raised the alarm, while the second is missing.

Coastguard volunteers on three boats from Titirangi and Papakura were called to assist shortly after 6pm on Tuesday (file image). Photo: Coastguard / Supplied

A search is underway for a person missing from a boat in Auckland's Manukau Harbour.

Coastguard said there were two people on a boat that got into difficulty near Puketutu Island on Tuesday.

One person was able to swim back to shore and alerted police, but the other was still missing.

Police confirmed they responded to a distress call just before 6pm.

"A search was initiated for two people believed to be in the water. One person has been located safe and well and requires no medical attention."

A RNZ employee said they could see two helicopters circling the area shortly after 7.30pm on Tuesday. Photo: Screenshot / adsbexchange.com

Coastguard volunteers on three boats from Titirangi and Papakura were called to the harbour shortly after 6pm on Tuesday. Auckland Airport's hovercraft is also assisting in the search, as is the Westpac Rescue Helicopter.

A RNZ employee said they could see two helicopters circling the area shortly after 7.30pm on Tuesday.
