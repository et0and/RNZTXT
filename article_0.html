Nine Dunedin homes red-stickered amid flooding

The South Island continues to recover from a devastating two-day series of record rain.

Tess Potae of Milton opened her property to flood victims. Photo: RNZ / Nathan Mckinnon

Dunedin and the Clutha District remain under a state of emergency, after the Otago region was pummelled by heavy rain Thursday and Friday, causing flooding and land slips.

A red heavy rain alert for North Otago, Dunedin and coastal Clutha was lifted Friday night and there are now no warnings or watches in place in those regions.

Photo: RNZ/Nathan Mckinnon

Nine homes have been red-stickered in Dunedin, it was announced Saturday afternoon.

Dunedin Civil Defence and Emergency Management confirmed the red-sticker notices have been issued but the location of the houses couldn't be confirmed at present.

A red sticker means the house is not safe to occupy at present.

Rapid assessments of slip-affected properties are also underway in Dunedin, to check whether they are safe for residents to return to.

The Dunedin City Council urged residents with flooded properties to contact their insurance advisor.

Community spirit is being felt in the Otago town of Milton, with one resident opening the doors of her community space to house evacuated residents.

Tess Potae has lived in the area for 21 years and said she thought this week's deluge was not as bad as previous storms.

She opened the property she owns around the corner from her home, to help flood-ravaged residents.

"We call it a whare, and we had the facilities for the doors to be open for anyone that was stranded.

"We probably housed 20-odd people."

Potae said she is relieved the sky is now clearing.

A Macandrew Bay resident said he was impressed by how prepared and responsive the council and emergency teams have been to the flooding.

Footpaths on Portobello Road are still covered in a thick layer of mud.

The local man, who did not wish to be named, told RNZ he was pleased by how quickly clean-up began.

"I've been really impressed by the recovery, eh?

"It seems like the council's been really prepared. You know, as soon as daybreak broke today there was people out and a big convoy of trucks all ready to go. It's been really impressive."

Road access to the Dunedin settlement is still only open to residents.

Kyeburn. Photo: Supplied / Emergency Management Otago

As the region moves into recovery mode, State Highway 1 north and south of Dunedin has been reopened.

But the Port Chalmers highway, State Highway 88, will remain closed over the weekend except for residents and emergency services.

Soldiers supervising a cordon in Hargest Crescent, St Kilda, on Saturday morning, 5 October, 2024. Photo: Otago Daily Times/ Stephen Jaquiery

The Dunedin City Council said the the welfare centre at Forsyth Barr stadium is likely to close tonight when the last three families there have temporary accommodation.

Civil Defence payments are available through the Ministry of Social Development for people impacted by flooding in Dunedin, Otago and coastal Clutha.

The payments can help with emergency food, clothing and bedding. Emergency Management Otago said it can also covers costs if you've had to leave your home; a payment if you have evacuees staying with you in places such as a private home, marae or community centres.

If you are a farmer or grower, or a worker or live on a farming business, Emergency Management Otago said support is available through your local Rural Support Trust. The number is 0800 787 254.

Please contact your milk supplier if you have any concerns about milk collection, it added.

There's thick mud and some debris on the main street in the Dunedin suburb of Macandrew Bay on the Otago Peninsula. Photo: RNZ / Tess Brunton

People are still encouraged to conserve water, with an urgent conserve water notice is in place for Clutha.

A precautionary boil water notice is also in effect for parts of Dunedin's West Harbour.

Residents are being warned of the risk of gastro due to floodwater and silt being contaminated with sewage.

About 110 households are also still without power in Toko Mouth and Glenledi.

PowerNet said it could not yet confirm when power will be restored due to road closures and unsafe conditions.
