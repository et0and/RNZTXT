King Tuheitia's youngest child and only daughter has been named as the eighth M훮ori monarch in a ceremony at Tuurangawaewae marae.

Its chair, Che Wilson, said Thursday's ceremony dated back eight generations.

"We follow the tikanga of our ancestors who created the Kiingitanga to unify and uplift our people and we have chosen Nga wai hono i te po as our new Monarch."

Thousands of mourners are in Ngaaruawaahia to attend the funeral of the M훮ori King Tuheitia, and the appointment of his successor.

Head of the Kiingitanga movement, Kiingi Tuheitia Pootatau Te Wherowhero VII died on Friday, aged 69, after heart surgery.

The formal ceremony to announce the king's successor began 10am. It will be followed by the funeral service for Kiingi Tuheitia.
