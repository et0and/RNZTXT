Live: Road user charges for plug-ins slashed

Owners of plug-in hybrid cars will now pay significantly less in charges after a change of heart by the government.

Photo: RNZ / Angus Dreaver

Owners of plug-in hybrids, like the popular Outlander plug-in, will pay less in road user charges after a change of heart by the Government.

Transport Minister Simeon Brown announced owners of plug-in hybrids will pay 38 dollars per thousand kilometres driven, under new taxes to pay for road upkeep.

The Minister originally proposed a rate of 53 dollars, but a select committee recommended dropping the fee.

Owners of plug-in hybrids had argued they would end up paying more than others because they used both electric batteries and petrol, which especially affected older vehicles with less battery power.
