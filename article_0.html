MP Efeso Collins collapses during charity run

A sheet was being held up to obscure an area where paramedics were working, with the event halted.

The scene at Commercial Bay. Photo: RNZ / Maia Ingoe

Green Party MP Efeso Collins has collapsed while taking part in a charity run in central Auckland.

The party confirmed the incident had happened but were still working to find out how he was doing.

Fire and Emergency New Zealand personnel have joined St John paramedics and police to surround the closed-off area.

Green Party MP Efeso Collins. Photo: Supplied / Efeso Collins

St John could not confirm Collins' condition.

A sheet was being held up to obscure an area where paramedics were working.

People at the scene were visibly upset and comforting each other.

The ChildFund Water Run was taking place in Te Komititanga Square, outside Commercial Bay at 8am on Wednesday.

The charity event was to raise awareness and funds to bring safe, clean drinking water to children in the Pacific.

The relay event has been halted.

As well as Collins, presenter Hilary Barry, community leader Dave Letele, radio host Mariner Fagaiava, actor Shavaughn Ruakere and ChildFund chief executive Josie Pagani took part in the run.
