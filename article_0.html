'That is fascism' - Peters on protesters at NZ First speech

New Zealand First leader Winston Peters has been repeatedly heckled while delivering a State of the Nation speech in Christchurch.

New Zealand First leader Winston Peters is delivering a State of the Nation speech in Christchurch.

Peters is scheduled to speak at a public meeting at James Hay Theatre from 2pm, and to answer questions from 3:15pm.

In remarks released to media, Peters called to 'Make New Zealand First again' and proposed a "war on woke".

"One of the most concerning and insidious cancers in our society today is the underlying creep of woke social engineering," Peters was to say.

"This seeping of the leftist group-think and condoned by too many on the right, has been mostly hidden from society in the way it has implanted itself in New Zealand."

At the speech's conclusion, Peters' remarks end with, "We together are going to take back control, take back our country, and Make New Zealand First Again."

It comes on the heels of the Minister for Foreign Affairs' arrival back from Washington DC, where he met with US Secretary of State Marco Rubio.

Peters has been tight-lipped about the specifics of the meetings he attended, insisting he will brief his Cabinet colleagues before speaking publicly on the issues.
