Former surgeon staged wife's death 'like something out of a crime novel' - prosecution

Philip John Polkinghorne's wife, Pauline Hanna, was found dead in the Remuera home she shared with her husband.

Philip Polkinghorne in court. Photo: RNZ/Nick Monro

The Crown alleges a retired eye surgeon murdered his wife then staged the scene to make it look like her death was a suicide.

The High Court jury trial of Philip John Polkinghorne is underway in Auckland after he pleaded not guilty to murder.

In her opening address, Crown prosecutor Alysha McClintock told the court Dr Poklinghorne had strangled his wife shortly before calling 1-1-1 to say she had killed herself.

"He did so, perhaps while angry and high or low, or just plain strung out on methamphetamine, staged the scene to make it look like his wife's death was a suicide.

"It will sound shocking, like something out of a crime novel."

She told the court Dr Polkinghorne was living a double life and his marriage had deteriorated.

The trial is set down for six weeks.

Polkinghorne arrived in court dressed in a suit and surrounded by his legal team, including Ron Mansfield KC.

Before the trial began, Polkinghorne pleaded guilty to two charges that had been suppressed until today - possession of the class A drug methamphetamine and possession of a pipe, at the time his wife died.

Philip Polkinghorne (centre) arrives at the High Court in Auckland with his lawyer Ron Mansfield. Photo: NZME/Michael Craig

He now faces one charge of murder.

Pauline Hanna, who was 63, was found dead in the Remuera home she shared with her husband in April 2021.

Unexplained for more than a year, her death in the upmarket Auckland suburb captured the nation's interest.

Police charged Polkinghorne, who is now 71, with murder 16 months after his wife's death.

The trial is set down for six weeks.
