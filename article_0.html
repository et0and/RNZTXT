Live: No tsunami threat after 6.7 quake off lower South Island

The National Emergency Management Agency said people do not need to evacuate their homes.

Photo:

A 6.7 magnitude earthquake has struck off the lower South Island. It was initially recorded as a 7.0 but was downgraded by the National Emergency Management Agency (NEMA).

NEMA said there was no tsunami threat to land and people did not need to evacuate.
