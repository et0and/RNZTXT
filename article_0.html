Olympics live: Hamish Kerr wins historic gold in high jump

Hamish Kerr won the title in a dramatic jump-off, just hours after Dame Lisa Carrington and Lydia Ko won gold overnight. Follow RNZ's live updates.

Hamish Kerr celebrates his gold medal in the men's high jump after a dramatic jump-off. Photo: BEN STANSALL / AFP

Hamish Kerr has won the high jump gold medal in a dramatic jump-off.

Earlier, he cleared 2.36m - a new national record.

When both Kerr and American jumper Shelby McEwen missed 2.38m, it looked like the Kiwi had won gold.

Instead, the duo were forced into a jump-off, with whoever clearing first taking the title.

Kerr eventually jumped clear at 2.34m, before doing a victory lap of the track at the Stade de France.

Earlier, Lydia Ko claimed a gold medal - New Zealand's second of the day - with a win in the women's golf tournament just before 4am.

Her win came just hours after Dame Lisa Carrington triumphed in the great Paris Olympics showdown on the water.

Dame Lisa won the K1 500m at the Tokyo Olympics, however, Aimee Fisher had beaten her at the distance twice this year, including at the last regatta before the Olympics, in Poland in late May.

But Fisher was not strong enough in the final, and Dame Lisa swept to victory to claim her eighth Olympic gold medal, with Fisher coming in fourth.

Kerr is hoping to become the first New Zealander to claim a high jump medal in his final at 5.10am Sunday.
