Live updates: Ferry refloat attempt begins

Two tugboats, the high tide and the Aratere's own propulsion will all be used to try and get it off the rocks. Follow our live updates here.

An attempt to refloat the stranded Interislander ferry Aratere is expected around 9pm and is "likely to take several hours".

The ship ran aground near Picton on Friday night after suffering a reported steering failure on a freight-only sailing.

No one was injured and there was no environmental damage, KiwiRail said.

Marlborough District Council said the refloat attempt would commence with the high tide around 9pm Saturday.

Two tug boats and the ferry's own propulsion would be used to try and get it off the rocks.

The refloat attempt was "likely to take several hours", the council said.
