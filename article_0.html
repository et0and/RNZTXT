Watch live: Trump guilty on all 34 charges, calls decision 'rigged'

Moments after being found guilty, the former US president called the verdicts a "disgrace" and said he was an "innocent man". Video

Former US President Donald Trump was found guilty all 34 counts in his hush money trial.

The jury's verdict on each count was read aloud in a New York courtroom on Friday (NZT).

Moments after being found guilty, Trump called the verdicts a "disgrace".

Speaking to reporters outside court Trump said it was a "rigged" trial and he vowed to keep fighting.

The New York jury in Trump's hush money trial has found him guilty of all of the charges against him.

This morning the judge announced the jury had reached a verdict, but had requested extra time to complete forms related to their decisions on the 34 felony counts the former president faces.

Trump was sitting in the courtroom with his arms folded across his chest as he awaited the reading of the verdict.

It was the second day of deliberations Thursday after the panel began the weighty task a day before. The jury deliberated for about 9.5 hours total: about 4.5 hours Wednesday and five hours Thursday.

At the heart of the charges are reimbursements paid to Trump's former lawyer Michael Cohen for a US$130,000 hush money payment to porn actor Stormy Daniels in exchange for not going public with her claim about a 2006 sexual encounter with Trump.

Prosecutors said the reimbursements were falsely logged as "legal expenses" to hide the true nature of the transactions.

The charges Trump faced were punishable by up to four years in prison. He has denied all wrongdoing and pleaded not guilty.

The case is the first of Trump's four indictments to reach trial and is the first-ever criminal case against a former US president.
