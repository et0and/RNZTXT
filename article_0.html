Live: Dozens still without power after floods

The Transport Agency still has numerous road warnings around Otago and Southland. Follow RNZ's blog for the latest weather updates.

Emergency response teams in Otago say floodwaters in the city are receding, while crews are cleaning up and restoring power.

PowerNet said it still does not know when power can be reconnected in Toko Mouth and Glenledi.

About 110 households have been without power since the storm which caused flooding around Otago brought down lines in the coastal Clutha settlement.

PowerNet said a worker got through to the community this morning to assess the situation and found a power pole damaged and lines down. The lines company said a bucket truck and crane is needed to repair the lines and cannot get through at present due to road closures and unsafe conditions.

A spokesperson told RNZ it is unclear when power will be able to be restored.

Some roads and highways are still affected and NZTA is aiming to have State Highway 1 north of Dunedin re-opened by this afternoon.

Crews are working to remove 300 cubic metres of rock from a slip south of Waikouaiti and there are many smaller slips on the highway's shoulders.
