Threat of power cuts will become more common - industry group

Big industry has been scaling back production to help ease the load on the electricity network as Transpower tries to avoid power cuts this morning.

Transpower is trying to avoid electricity cuts this morning. Photo: RNZ / Rebekah Parsons-King

A group representing big industry is warning that appeals to conserve electricity will become more common.

The country is waking to a bitterly cold morning with temperatures dropping as low as -6C in some areas overnight.

Electricity grid operator Transpower on Thursday warned the low temperatures could see power cuts occur if electricity demands spiked too high, particularly between the hours of 7am and 9am.

Businesses were asked to help and the Major Electricity Users' Group said industry starting cutting back production on Thursday, which had provided some relief.

The group's chair John Harbord said they had moved quickly to help shore up supply.

"So already we're no longer looking at a shortage of 22 megawatts, we're looking at a surplus of supply of 33 megawatts."

In addition to that, there were 243 megawatts of electricity "sitting in reserve", he said, though he warned that did not mean people could be complacent.

Harbord said appeals for people to conserve power were going to become more common.

"As we become even more reliant on intermittent renewable electricity generation like wind and solar... these types of scenarios become more likely."

Harbord said a balance needed to be struck between renewable energy sources and thermal generation.

It is the third time this month Transpower has issued a notice about low power supply but the first time it has put out a warning for the public to cut back on power usage.
