Live updates: Commuter disruptions as protesters march on Parliament

More than 600 people have gathered as Te Pāti Māori co-leader Rawiri Waititi told the crowd it should march on Parliament.

Protesters in Wellington have reached the streets around Parliament.

Protesters gathered on the Aurora Terrace overbridge to signal to motorway traffic their protest, but 15 minutes ago began to march on Parliament.

Te Pāti Māori co-leader Rawiri Waititi announced to the crowd it should march on Parliament.

An RNZ reporter at the scene, Tessa Guest, says the crowd has swelled to more than 600 people. They are singing waiata, whooping and waving signs as they reach Parliament.

Police have blocked off some side streets.

The demonstrations are in response to Te Pāti Māori's call for action against the new government's policies on co-governance and the Treaty.
