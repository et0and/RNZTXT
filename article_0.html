Gunshot heard, woman taken away in ambulance, resident says

A woman has been taken away by an ambulance "screaming her head off" after a serious incident today, a Wainuiomata resident says.

Police at the scene south of Wainuiomata, near Wellington. Photo: RNZ / Nick James

A woman has been taken away by an ambulance "screaming her head off" after a serious incident today, a Wainuiomata resident says.

Police have said they are responding to a serious incident south of the suburb in the Wellington region this afternoon.

Wainuiomata resident Elizabeth Lister told RNZ a woman ran out of a car towards her neighbours' house and believed the woman was being threatened at knife point by a man.

"I heard this loud car coming down the road... and I thought that's just a stupid guy and before he hardly even stopped outside Margaret and Bunter's driveway the girl opened the door and she ran up the driveway.

"It looked like he threw a rock or something at the windscreen of the car, then it wasn't long ago after that the police arrived

"We could hear the police yelling drop the knife, drop the knife."

Moments later she heard a gun shot.

The woman was then taken away by an ambulance "screaming her head off".

The armed offenders squad were still on Coast Rd, Lister said. She said there were about 12 to 15 officers at the scene.

Earlier, two armed officers visited Lister's home and asked if they could get in the bushes to get a better view.

A police spokesperson confirmed they had closed off Coast Road after being called to the area just ahead of midday.

They say a cordon has been put in place south of the Wainuiomata Golf Club.
