Watch live: Wellington mayor on Crown Observer being appointed for council

An urgent council meeting has ended, after the government called the situation in the capital "a shambles".

An urgent meeting of Wellington City Councillors has concluded.

It was called after Minister of Local Government Simeon Brown announced he planned to appoint a Crown Observer to Wellington City Council, to help with the long-term plan process and report back to him.

It comes after news the council would be required to rewrite its 2024-34 Long Term Plan following a back-track on the sale of shares in the city's airport.

Ministers had been threatening to intervene at the council, calling it a "shambles".

Councillors Diane Calvert and Ray Chung told RNZ it was a fairly positive meeting, where they discussed the terms of reference in Brown's statement.

Wellington mayor Tory Whanau is expected to speak to media at 4pm.
