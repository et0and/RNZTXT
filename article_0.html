Darleen Tana formally refuses to resign

The former Green MP has responded to the party and says they have no intention of quitting.

Darleen Tana says they were fairly elected as a list MP and has no intention of resigning. Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

Former Green MP Darleen Tana has responded to the co-leaders request they resign, saying they have no intention to do so.

The Green Party co-leaders formally wrote to Tana last month, to indicate their refusal to resign distorted Parliament's proportionality.

In response, Tana said they were fairly elected as a list MP at the last election, and they had done nothing that would require the Speaker to expel them from the House.

Party delegates will vote next month on whether to apply the party-hopping legislation, which the Greens have historically been opposed to.
