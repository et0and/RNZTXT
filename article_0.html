Live weather updates: Roads still closed as states of emergency remain in place

Dunedin is waking up to a massive cleanup today after its wettest day in more than a century. Follow RNZ's blog for the latest weather updates

Flooding on Botha St, Saint Kilda in Dunedin. Photo: Nathan Mckinnon/RNZ

Local roads remain closed across Otago this morning, after two days of heavy rain and flooding.

The Dunedin City Council says clearing slips and reopening roads will be a key focus as the cleanup starts today.

The fire service has released drone footage showing a huge slip near Ravensbourne, which has knocked out the water supply to the West Harbour suburbs.

As of late Friday night, water tanks could not be delivered there because of road blockages, although the city council says residents have reserves.

Both the Dunedin City and the Clutha District councils are asking people not to go out unnecessarily, and states of emergency remain in place.

The army is supporting cordons around flooded parts of South Dunedin, and the council says if people have no reason to be in a particular area, they need to stay away.
