Watch live: Police provide update on Dunedin fatal stabbing

Police are providing an update after a high school student was stabbed at a bus hub in the city on Thursday.

Police are providing an update after a high school student was stabbed at a bus hub in the city on Thursday. Otago Coastal Area Commander Inspector Marty Gray is expected to speak to media about 2.45pm.

The 16-year-old Trinity Catholic College student was attacked on Great King Street shortly after 3pm on Thursday.

He was rushed to Dunedin Hospital where he later died from his injuries.

A 13-year-old boy appeared before Judge Michael Turner at the Youth Court in Dunedin, who transferred the matter to the Dunedin High Court for a first appearance on 11 June.

He cannot be identified due to his age.

Police found a knife at the scene and were working to figure out what led to the stabbing.

A post-mortem was expected to be carried out on Friday.

Detective Senior Sergeant Kallum Croudis said police wanted to hear from anyone who had photos or footage of what happened.
