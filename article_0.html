Live: 'Report exposes a national disgrace'

The country's disgrace continues "to this day", chair of the Abuse in Care Royal Commission of Inquiry Judge Coral Shaw says.

The chairperson for the Abuse in Care Royal Commission of Inquiry says the final report lays bare significant failings by state and faith-based care, to the most vulnerable.

The inquiry's 3000 page final report has been tabled in Parliament this afternoon.

It found at least 200,000 people have been abused in state and faith-based care since 1950.

Judge Coral Shaw says the abuse was widespread and systemic.

"Our report exposes a national disgrace, one that lasted over 50 years and continues to this day."

The report makes 138 recommendations to overhaul Aotearoa's care system.

Many of the survivors spoken to by RNZ say they are glad the report is finally being released, but some remain sceptical over whether the government will honour it.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon is expected to make a formal apology to survivors in November.
