Magnitude 7.6 earthquake strikes Japan, tsunami warning issued

An earthquake with a preliminary magnitude of 7.6 hit north central Japan on Monday, followed by a warning of another earthquake to come, public broadcaster NHK said.

Emergency services are responding after a large earthquake in central Japan (file photo). Photo: AFP

An earthquake with a preliminary magnitude of 7.6 hit north central Japan on Monday, public broadcaster NHK said.

It was followed by a warning of another earthquake to come in Ishikawa, NHK said soon afterwards.

The Japan Meteorological Agency issued a tsunami warning along western coastal regions of Ishikawa, Niigata and Toyama prefectures.

Tsunami of up to five metres high are believed to be reaching Noto in Ishikawa Prefecture, according to the weather agency.

Waves more than 1m high hit the coast of Wajima City in Ishikawa Prefecture, NHK reported.

Hokuriku Electric Power said it was checking for any irregularities at its nuclear power plants, NHK reported. And Kansai Electric has said there are no abnormalities detected at nuclear plants in the quake area.

The South Korea Meterological Agency said the sea level on its eastern province of Gangwon could rise after the earthquake.
