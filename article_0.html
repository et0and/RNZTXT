Watch live: Civil Defence gives update on Otago flooding

Otago's state highway network is beginning to reopen with the worst of the wild weather now passed. Follow RNZ's blog for the latest weather updates.

Local roads remain closed across Otago this morning, after two days of heavy rain and flooding.

The Dunedin City Council says clearing slips and reopening roads will be a key focus as the cleanup starts today.

MetService is assuring people in North Otago, Dunedin and coastal Clutha, that any rainfall today will not be significant.

Meanwhile, Clutha District Council has had reports of human waste around treatment plants that have overflowed.

It wants people to treat all floodwaters as contaminated and avoid contact with it.

The waste was found at seven plants, including Tapanui, Balclutha and Kaka Point.

Both the Dunedin City and the Clutha District councils are asking people not to go out unnecessarily, and states of emergency remain in place.
