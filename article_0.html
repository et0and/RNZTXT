Olympics live: 'I gave it everything' - silver medallist Hayden Wilde

It was so close yet so far for Hayden Wilde, who has claimed silver in a dramatic triathlon in Paris.

Hayden Wilde with his friend and rival Alex Yee from Great Britain after the race. Photo: Iain McGregor / www.photosport.nz

It was so close yet so far for Kiwi Hayden Wilde, who has claimed silver in a dramatic triathlon in Paris.

Wilde looked to be cruising to gold, but was pipped on the home stretch by his great rival from Great Britain, Alex Yee.

Yee came out of nowhere to overtake Wilde, who settled for silver and New Zealand's second medal of the games.

Speaking to Sky Sport, he said it was a nervy finish seeing Yee close in.

"I looked back and there was a gap and he looked composed and I was trying to get composed as well," Wilde says. "It felt good but it was hot, just with obviously the change of time so that might have been a bit of an effect.

"I gave it everything and I can't leave this race with a what if. I'm just stoked for Alex as well, just to finally have an even race, no penalties, nothing, just purely a good foot race, it was exciting."

In sailing in the men's skiff (49er) the crew of Isaac McHardie and William McKenzie sit in first place overall after 11 races.

The women's skiff (49er) of Jo Aleh and Molly Meech are seventh after 12 races, having now qualified for the medal race.
