Warning as fire burns through 10 hectares in Porirua

Smoke is drifting over a large part of the city as fire crews and helicopters battle a blaze at Titahi Bay.

Fire and Emergency responded to the fire just after 11pm on Thursday. Photo: RNZ / Richard Tindiller

Eight fire crews and helicopters with monsoon buckets are working to bring a vegetation fire in Titahi Bay under control.

Fire and Emergency received a call about the fire at about 11pm on Thursday and the first crews were onsite 10 minutes later.

By Friday morning the fire had burnt through 10 hectares of vegetation in Whitireia Park, Fire and Emergency said in a statement.

If you've witnessed the fire, and would like to share photos or your story with us, please email it to: iwitness@radionz.co.nz

Fire and Emergency said additional rural firefighters were being brought in to support and relieve the urban and rural crews who had been working overnight.

Smoke is affecting a large part of Porirua and the Public Health Service said those who are affected should go inside and close the windows and doors and turn off air conditioning units.

Smoke could irritate the eyes, nose, throat and airways and smokers, the elderly, children and those with asthma, heart or lung disease were most at risk, it said.

A spokesperson said no homes have been told to evacuate but the public should stay away from the area.
