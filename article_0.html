Live: More than 10,000 turn out for national Hui-ā-Iwi at Tūrangawaewae Marae

More than 10,000 people have turned out to a national Hui-ā-Iwi being held in Ngāruawāhia convened by Kiingi Tuuheitia, including representatives from iwi, political parties and public services.

More than 10,000 people have turned out to a national Hui-ā-Iwi being held in Ngāruawāhia convened by Kiingi Tuuheitia, including representatives from iwi, political parties and public services.

More than 100 members of Waikato Tainui welcomed a mass contingent of iwi, community, public service, and political representatives on to Tūrangawaewae Marae on Saturday morning.

Kiingi Tuuheitia extended the invite in December last year after iwi leaders highlighted the need for a unified response to

coalition government policy impacting Māori and Te Tiriti o Waitangi.

Many of the visitors have had to move down to the bank of the Waikato River in order to accommodate the number of people.
