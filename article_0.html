Watch live: Child given meth 'lolly' found in donate food parcel, City Missioner says

Up to 400 people who were given food parcels at the Auckland City Mission are being contacted after potentially lethal amounts of methamphetamine were found in a lolly.

Auckland City Mission is urgently contacting people who may have been given food parcels with lollies containing potentially lethal amounts of methamphetamine.

The Rinda-brand pineapple lollies were unknowingly distributed in food parcels.

Drug Foundation testing found a sample was 100 percent methamphetamine. Further testing was expected to happen on Wednesday.

The Foundation was trying to figure out if there are more contaminated lollies out there. The lolly was one in a sealed bag of about 20.

Missioner Helen Robinson and a representative from the NZ Drug Foundation will speak to media at 11am in Auckland.
