Multiple injuries reported at derelict Wellington hotel

Police cars, ambulances and fire vehicles have rushed to the Amora Hotel amid reports there have been 'structural issues'.

Emergency services at the Amora Hotel in Wellington, June 2024. Photo: Nick James / RNZ

Emergency services are at a derelict central Wellington hotel, with reports people have been injured.

A number of police cars, ambulances and fire vehicles are outside the Amora Hotel on Wakefield Street.

The New Zealand Herald reports "multiple people" have been injured due to "structural issues".

Fire and Emergency said it was there to assist the ambulance service.

The 192-room hotel shut in 2017 for extensive refurbishment after the 2016 Kaikoura earthquake.

It has been boarded up since.

Wellington Free Ambulance says it is an "active scene" and will get more information shortly.
