As it happened: Biker Sam Gaze misses out on medal on Day 3

The defending world champion couldn't keep up with the leaders' pace in scorching conditions during the cross country mountain biking.

Sam Gaze of New Zealand during a training session. Photo: SW Pix / www.photosport.nz

New Zealand is still hunting for Olympic medals after Sam Gaze couldn't keep up with the pace in the men's short-course cross country mountain biking race.

Gaze - who is the defending world champion - placed sixth in the race. Conditions on the course were so hot, race marshalls had to hold umbrellas over the competitors' heads before the start.

Earlier, the equestrian eventing team finished outside medal contention, placing eighth overall as the competition concluded with show jumping at the Chateau de Versailles.

At the same time, Tim Price and Clarke Johnstone qualified eighth and 16th for the individual jumping event; the final for that starts around 1am Tuesday.

The Black Ferns take on Fiji in their group match at the Stade de France at 2.30am. They are already in the quarter final, which starts at 7am.
