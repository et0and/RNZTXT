Watch live: Dawn ceremony marks beginning of Waitangi Day

The ceremony is taking place at Te Whare Rūnanga Marae on the upper Treaty grounds.

A dawn ceremony will mark the beginning of Waitangi Day commemorations at Te Whare Rūnanga Marae on the upper Treaty grounds this morning.

Today's celebration came after a tense pōwhiri at the marae yesterday which saw ACT Leader David Seymour's microphone cut off twice, and his speech drowned out by waiata.

A flag raising ceremony will take place followed by a public breakfast at 7am.

At 9am the traditional waka parade will take place just infront of Te Tii Marae before eventually docking near the lower treaty grounds.

There will also be a number of performance from local groups, including kapa haka and reggae, as well as market stalls throughout the grounds.
