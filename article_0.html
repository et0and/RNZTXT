'The town is pumping': Business booming in Tairua

This year's highway closure meant the coastal town was often bypassed by peninsula travellers, but now things are looking up for local retailers.

Tairua, on the Coromandel Peninsula. Photo: Leonard Powell

A week on from the Coromandel Peninsula's State Highway 25A reopening, business is booming in Tairua.

After slips washed away parts of SH25A earlier this year, many travellers bypassed the coastal town as they took different routes to other peninsula hotspots like Whitianga and Whangamata.

But now that the key road is open again, people driving through are stopping at the local businesses.

Tairua Business Association co-chair Peter Hunt said people were initially not sure when the road would open.

"However, it got busier and busier as news spread that it's open and everyone could plan their journeys into Tairua.

"Because we didn't have a definitive date of it opening, obviously some people were just leaving it until they knew for sure."

Hunt said he felt encouraged to see the eateries packed with people and the town busier than normal.

Maree Smith owns clothing store Get It On and said she has had to bring in extra staff this week.

"The town is pumping. It's phenomenal and it's life changing. All the eateries are full and there's no parking anywhere (and we're not complaining about that!)."

"So, it's obvious the change that it's made (the road opening). It's great."
