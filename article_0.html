Marokopa mystery: Mum of missing Phillips kids breaks silence

It is the first time in two years their mother has spoken publicly, and she's asking for people to help.

The mother of the missing Phillips children has appealed for people's help in returning them to her.

Today marks one week since police offered an $80,000 reward for information that would help discover the whereabouts. It is also one of the children's 11th birthday.

Ember, Maverick and Jayda Phillips have been missing along with their father for two and a half years.

Police believe they are in the Marokopa area in the King Country.

Today their mum, Cat, said she was begging for people's help in finding them.

Photo: Screenshot

In a video provided by police she said: "They are just innocent children. They do not deserve to be treated this way.

"They do not deserve the life that is being provided to them right now."

Referring to her daughter's birthday, Cat said: "She will be a young woman now and she needs her mother."

Her other daughter, Ember, was asthmatic and needed medical care "that cannot be provided from the land", Cat said.

"Many of you say that the children are fine, that they're being well looked after.

"How do you know? Have you seen them? Or is it just bush talk?"

She then took aim at her estraged husband, Tom, accusing him of child neglect, endangerment, abandonment and abuse.

"What Thomas is doing is not okay. It is not okay to divide and conquer.

"It is not okay to isolate and endanger.

"None of this is okay. My babies deserve better."

She concluded by asking anyone with information to ring the police.
