Watch live: Casey Costello fronts after tobacco tax changes

The Associate Health Minister is set to speak to journalists after quietly halving tax on heated tobacco products.

Associate Health Minister Casey Costello is expected to speak to journalists after quietly halving tax on heated tobacco products (HTPs).

The move took effect this month, with Costello saying she wanted to see whether HTPs would be a useful cessation device similar to vaping - despite advisors warning there was no evidence to support their use for quitting smoking.

She refused to be interviewed by RNZ, but is speaking to reporters in Christchurch about the launch of community beat teams.

Police Assistant Commissioner Mike Johnson, Superintendent Lane Todd, Christchurch mayor Phil Mauger, and Business Canterbury chief executive Leeann Watson will also be at the launch event.
