Live: Evacuations under way as heavy rain lashes Tai Rāwhiti, Hawke's Bay

People in Te Karaka are being asked to relocate as the Waipaoa River rises, and police are evacuating residents in Haumoana, Hawke's Bay.

Dozens of families were sheltering at the the Haumoana School Hall evacation centre on Wednesday morning. Photo: RNZ / Anusha Bradley

Evacuations are under way in parts of Tai Rāwhiti, with police going door to door, as heavy rains and wind continue to lash the district.

An iwi-led response team has encouraged some Te Karaka residents to evacuate due to high levels at the Waipaoa River.

Pimia Wehi says about 70 people have evacuated to Te Karaka Area school, and another 80 residents have told them they have evacuated to marae.

She says the river level is currently at 8.2 metres, and when it reaches 9m, sirens will sound which will signal the whole community to evacuate to the school.

In Hawke's Bay, police are knocking on doors and evacuating residents in Haumoana due to costal inundation.

Civil Defence has declared a state of emergency in the Heretaunga Ward in Hastings District, as the region is also battered with heavy rain.
