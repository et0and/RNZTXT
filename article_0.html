Inflation slows to lowest rate in more than two years

The CPI rose 0.5 percent in the December quarter, as rises in the cost of rents, rates and insurance offset cheaper food and fuel.

Photo: 123RF

Inflation has slowed to its lowest rate in more than two years.

The consumer price index rose 0.5 percent in the three months ended December as rises in the cost of running a home such as rents, rates and insurance offset cheaper food and fuel.

The annual rate fell to its lowest level since June 2021 at 4.7 percent from 5.6 percent.

The numbers were largely in line with expectations.

Economists were picking that the new inflation numbers would show price growth was slowing..
