Peters sacks Goff over Trump comments

The foreign affairs minister says Phil Goff's position as UK High Commissioner is untenable after public comments about the US President.

Phil Goff. Photo:

The foreign affairs minister says Phil Goff's position as High Commissioner to the United Kingdom is untenable, following public comments about US President Donald Trump.

According to The Post, Mr Goff was speaking at a Chatham House event, and said "President Trump has restored the bust of Churchill to the Oval Office. But do you think he really understands history?"

A spokesperson for Winston Peters says Mr Goff's comments are deeply disappointing and do not represent the views of the New Zealand government.

The Secretary of Foreign Affairs and Trade will now work with Mr Goff on an 'upcoming leadership transition' at the High Commission - effectively confirming his sacking from the position.

Last year Goff offended the Māori King at at an event for the New Zealand delegation in London. He forgot to perform a karakia to open the event at the High Commission, and then went on to say in front of Kīngi Tuheitia that no one in the room had experienced a coronation before.
