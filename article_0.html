Girl among five killed as storms lash Australia

A nine-year-old has been killed, and several people are still missing after powerful winds and flash floods on the east coast.

Downed trees seen in Oxenford in Gold Coast, Queensland, Australia, after severe storms. Photo: Supplied / Energex

Five people are dead, including a nine-year-old girl who got lost inside a storm drain in Brisbane's south, as severe storms lash much of Australia's east coast.

Queensland police said the girl was found dead following an extensive search on Tuesday night.

A passenger onboard a capsized boat off Brisbane's coast also died, as police divers search for two other missing passengers.

Meanwhile, a woman has died at a regional Victorian campground after it was hit by flash flooding.

It comes as a man in Victoria's east and a woman on the Gold Coast both died after being struck by falling trees in the past 24 hours.

A nine-year-old girl is among those dead after severe storms ripped through parts of south-east Queensland on Monday and Tuesday, leaving more than 110,000 people without power.

The girl was reported missing on Tuesday afternoon, believed to be lost inside a flooded drain in the south Brisbane suburb of Rochdale.

Queensland police later released a statement saying she had been found and added her family were requesting privacy.

Police also located a body after a 12-metre boat capsized between Manly and Green Island, off Brisbane's coast.

Following a search for three missing passengers, police found one person dead in the water about 6:15pm.

The two other people are still missing.

A buckled crane in Burleigh Heads after severe storms lashed the Gold Coast, Queensland, Australia. Photo: Supplied / Energex

Police said the search was suspended just before midnight and would continue Wednesday morning.

The boat was believed to be carrying 11 passengers, and eight others were taken to hospital in a stable condition.

Queensland Ambulance Services (QAS) said the eight rescued men, aged in their 50s and 60s, were in the water for around an hour before help arrived.

Manly State School, a primary school in Brisbane's Bayside, sustained some major damage, with the roof of the school hall blown off.

A 59-year-old woman died on Monday night in the suburb of Helensvale, on the Gold Coast.

Police say the woman was struck by a tree about 9.30pm as the state's south-east was hit with intense rain, large hail and fierce winds.

Emergency services also remain searching for two people missing in floodwaters north of Queensland's Sunshine Coast.

It is understood three people were in a large drain that washes out into the Mary River in Gympie when the area was inundated with water.

Downed power lines and trees seen on the Gold Coast after severe storms. Photo: Supplied / Energex

The QAS confirmed one patient in their 40s was being treated for head injuries.

Police and swift water rescue are searching for two women, both in their 40s, who remain unaccounted for.

State-owned power utility Energex confirmed 98,367 people were still without power late Tuesday night, with nearly 1100 powerlines brought down in the past 24 hours.

By Tuesday evening the SES had responded to more than 1,000 calls for help around the Gold Coast, Scenic Rim and Logan, according to Queensland Fire and Emergency (QFES).

QFES crews have been called to more than 600 incidents in those areas.

In Brisbane, police said multiple roads in South Brisbane are being impacted by flash flooding, as Brisbane City recorded 85mm of rainfall in two hours.

Brisbane City Council has also issued warnings over rising water levels in the Norman River in East Brisbane and the Stable Swamp Creek, near Rocklea.

Brisbane Airport said multiple flights have been delayed or will be potentially cancelled due to the severe weather.

Damage seen to houses after severe storms ripped through parts of south-east Queensland on 25 and 26 December, 2023. Photo: Supplied / Queensland Fire and Emergency Services

A woman has died after a campground in regional Victoria was hit by flash flooding.

Emergency workers were called to the Buchan campground in east Gippsland, where the woman was found dead at about 6.45pm (local time) on Tuesday.

"Police were told a number of vehicles in the campground area were underwater and a number of people were safely retrieved from a nearby bridge," Victorian police said in a statement.

Police said a woman and her dog are lucky to be alive after they were rescued from the Werribee River, in Melbourne's south-west on Tuesday.

The 29-year-old woman was swept away by fast-flowing water about 1.30pm after she tried to retrieve her dog from the river.

A passer-by raised the alarm after seeing the woman holding on to a tree.

The Search and Rescue Squad and Water Police rescued the woman and her dog.

Victoria's State Emergency Service said it responded to more than 1000 calls for help over a 72-hour period with the state's west being hit the hardest.

Among those affected was the regional Victorian town of Wedderburn where businesses and homes were inundated with water.

The central Victorian town of Elmore has also had warnings issued after reports of a faulty storage tank at the local water treatment plant.

Residents are warned not to use their local tap water for drinking, food preparation or preparing baby formula.

Residents are also being told not to use tap water to wash themselves, to clean dishes and clothing, and not to give it to pets.

The storage tank has reportedly been drained and the local water authority is organising bottled water delivery for the town's 800 residents.

Local water authority Coliban Water said it could be several days before the water is restored and safe to drink.

The cause of the fault is currently being investigated.

Several alerts remain in place across the state with residents urged to check the Vic Emergency website throughout the day for updates.

In New South Wales, there is currently a severe thunderstorm warning, with damaging winds and large hail stones predicted for Sydney, Greater Newcastle and Central Coast areas.

NSW State Emergency Service (SES) said it had responded to 473 calls for help since Christmas Day, including six flood rescues in the state's south-east.

Volunteers responded to 492 incidents on Christmas Eve, mostly in Sydney.

Most of the calls were from south-eastern parts of the state, where flash flooding impacted parts of the Illawarra, Goulburn and Eurobodalla.

SES spokesperson Emily Barton said some parts of the south coast experienced intense rainfall of up to 156 millimetres.

Meanwhile, Grenfell in the state's central west was impacted by hail storms, with the SES receiving 41 calls for assistance.

More than 1000 volunteers were deployed to assist over the Christmas period.

NSW SES Assistant Commissioner Sean Kearns said further storms are forecast for Boxing Day, shifting towards the east.

"We've been prepared and we are ready for further rainfall today," he said.

A watch and act alert for flooding has been issued for Hedley, Welshpool, Toora and Port Franklin as well.

Meanwhile in Adelaide, the state recorded its coldest Christmas day in 17 years with a maximum of 19.7 C.

An upper trough moving across the state's south-east around Christmas Eve has also brought some unseasonable hail to the Murray Mallee region.

38mm of rain fell in Lameroo and 2mm in Pinnaroo while on Christmas Day and overnight, Macclesfield in the Adelaide Hills had 24mm of rain - the highest in the state.
