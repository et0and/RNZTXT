Two in hospital after apartment explosion

Emergency services are at the scene of the fire in central Wellington.

Emergency services gather on Church street in Wellington after reports of an injury in an apartment explosion. Photo: RNZ/ Samuel Rillstone

Two people are in hospital following an explosion in a central Wellington apartment building on Friday afternoon.

Roadblocks are in place on adjoining Boulcott Street. People are asked to avoid the area.

Assistant commander James Martin-Bond said initial reports were of a fire on level eight.

"When crews investigated they found there'd been an explosion in one of the apartments, and so we are now investigating the cause of that explosion."

Fire crews were alerted about 1pm and there are 11 fire crews in attendance.

Fire and emergency are attending the fire on Church Street in central Wellington. Photo: RNZ/ Samuel Rillstone

Wellington Free Ambulance said three of its ambulances and two cars responded as well.

They have transported one patient in a serious condition and another in a moderate condition to Wellington Hospital.

A policeman at the cordon told an RNZ reporter there had been an explosion at an apartment and all residents have been evacuated from the building.
