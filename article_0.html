Watch live: Luxon fronts media in Tokyo

The prime minister is taking a break from a busy schedule of visits to businesses and other activities to speak to journalists.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon is taking a break from a busy schedule of visits to businesses and other activities to speak to journalists, during his visit to Japan.

Luxon started his trade mission programme with a full business delegation in tow this morning.

The group caught up with him in Tokyo on Monday night, after the Defence Force plane they were travelling on earlier broke down in Papua New Guinea.

Luxon is in Japan to promote New Zealand business and international investment and to meet with his Japanese counterpart Fumio Kishida.

He has several business engagements today, including being present when Rocket Lab sign a large rocket launch agreement with Japanese earth observation company Synspective.

He will also visit a navy base later today.

The briefing is due to start around 3.30pm. (NZT)
