Luxon refuses to speak about Treaty Principles Bill

The Prime Minister says ACT leader David Seymour will have more to say on the Bill soon.

Photo: RNZ / Samuel Rillstone

The Prime Minister is refusing to talk about the Treaty Principles Bill, saying ACT leader David Seymour will have more to say on the bill soon.

Christopher Luxon was asked a series of questions at his weekly post-Cabinet conference about updates or further detail about the Treaty Principles Bill, given Cabinet considered it today.

He said he would not be drawn on Cabinet discussions - but his coalition colleague Seymour will have more to say "shortly".

It comes after a draft version of the government's controversial Treaty Principles Bill was considered by Cabinet on Monday.

The final version will be considered again by Cabinet before it is introduced to Parliament.
