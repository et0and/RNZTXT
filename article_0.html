Engineer forged signatures on almost 1000 house designs

Jonathan Beau Hall forged his supervisors' signatures - and a judge estimates the cost of his fraud to be in the millions.

Photo: Supplied/ Dan Bailey

An engineer who forged the signatures of two higher-qualified colleagues in a wide-scale fraud has been sent to jail for four and a half years.

Jonathan Beau Hall was sentenced today in the Rotorua District Court on 112 counts of forgery affecting 946 houses and 39 councils across the country.

The engineering technologist signed off on house designs when he was not qualified to by using the identities of his supervisors.

He then submitted the documents to councils for consent.

The fraud was estimated by Hall to have cost $558,000 but Judge Greg Hollister-Jones believed the true cost to be in the millions.
