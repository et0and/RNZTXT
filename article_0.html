Live: China-backed hackers took information from Parliament network

GCSB bosses have revealed more details about the attacks, while Judith Collins says New Zealand won't introduce sanctions.

New Zealand is backing the United Kingdom in its condemnation of People's Republic of China (PRC) state-backed malicious cyber activity, but won't be introducing sanctions.

Judith Collins on Tuesday revealed a second hack in 202, in which a PRC state-sponsored group known as APT40 breached the New Zelaand Parliamentary network.

GCSB director Andrew Clark told reporters some information was removed from the networks during the attack, but that it was not sensitive or strategic in nature/
