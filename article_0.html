Watch live: Luxon gives pre-Budget speech

The Prime Minister is speaking to business leaders ahead of this month's Budget.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon is speaking to business leaders ahead of this month's Budget.

Luxon is addressing the Auckland Business Chamber. His speech will be livestreamed at the top of this page about 12.50pm.

In a series of pre-budget announcements this month, the government has set out a defence budget boost and reaffirmed its tax cut commitment.

Defence Minister Judith Collins last week announced $571 million was going to defence to improve pay for the frontline defence personnel and upgrade equipment.

Finance Minister Nicola Willis gave a major pre-Budget speech and in the process ruled out an austerity Budget and reaffirmed her commitment to tax cuts for most New Zealanders.

The prime minister and Corrections Minister Mark Mitchell earlier announced a $1.9 billion investment in more beds at Waikeria Prison, better pay and more Corrections staff recruits and rehabilitation for remand prisoners.

Associate education minister David Seymour has announced $478m in time-limited funding for free lunches in schools.
