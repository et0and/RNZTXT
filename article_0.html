'These poor babies': Man shot trying to stop car break-in

Neighbours described gunshots in at "tight-knit community" and locking themselves inside as a man tried to stop a pair breaking into cars.

Police at the scene of the shooting. Photo: RNZ / Bill Hickman

A man has been shot in the legs trying to stop people breaking into vehicles in Porirua.

A resident told RNZ they heard shots ring out, losing count of how many there were, and saw a person running up the street.

Rachel Mariu said she told her children they were fireworks to calm them down, and locked her doors and windows.

"We're all a bit of a tight-knit community, we all know each other's kids, so yeah, we were all, like, kind of on edge last night knowing that, you know, we've all got families.

Police at the scene of a shooting in Titahi Bay, Porirua. Photo: Reece Baker / RNZ

"I knew straight away where it came from, so I was like, 'Oh, these poor babies, poor whanau."

Mariu says car theft and break-ins were a constant worry in the area.

The incident took place on Monday night in Tītahi Bay, and police are appealing for information.

In a statement, police said officers were called to a Dimock Street address at about 10.20pm, with initial inquiries suggesting "the man was shot after disturbing two people attempting to break into cars on the street".

Police at the scene of a shooting in Titahi Bay, Porirua. Photo: Reece Baker / RNZ

He was taken to hospital in a serious condition.

Investigators could be seen on their hands and knees searching the grass in a cordoned-off area in front of a single-storey home.

Police ask that anyone with information call 105 and quote file number 241029/7050.
