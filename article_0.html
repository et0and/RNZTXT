Live: National Hikoi arrives in Kaitaia

Tens of thousands are expected to join as a hīkoi opposing the Treaty Principles Bill begins its journey, and leaders reject David Seymour's offer of a meeting. Audio

Kairākau lead the hikoi progress through Kaitaia. Photo: Peter de Graaf

Leaders of a hīkoi against David Seymour's Treaty Principles Bill have rejected the ACT leader's offer of a meeting as they set off for Wellington.

A dawn karakia at Te Rerenga Wairua launched the national hīkoi on Monday.

Hīkoi mō te Tiriti participants gathered for a dawn blessing ahead of a nine-day journey to Wellington. Police are preparing for 25,000 people to join, while organisers are hoping for as many as 40,000.
