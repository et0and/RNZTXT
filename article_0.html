Olympics live: Black Ferns make it two in a row

The NZ Sevens side have beaten Canada in a dominant performance, as swimmer Erika Fairweather battles for a spot in the 200m freestyle final.

New Zealand's Michaela Blyde during the women's pool A rugby sevens match between New Zealand and China during the Paris 2024 Olympic Games a 28 July 2024. Photo: AFP

Several New Zealand athletes had a tough time at the Olympics overnight.

Lulu Sun lost her first round singles match to Ukraine's Marta Kostyuk and her doubles match with Erin Routliffe was delayed again by rain.

Gymnast Georgia Rose-Brown missed out on the finals, Luuka Jones missed out on the medals in the kayak singles, and Sammie Maxwell finished 8th at the mountain biking.

Swimmer Lewis Clareburt made it to the 400m individual medley final but came 6th.

The sailors and Black Ferns had a more successful night.

The Black Ferns won their first two games of the tournament this morning - besting both China and Canda - they take on Fiji in their final pool match early on Tuesday.

New Zealand remains medal-less.
