Police officer who shot Kaoss Price used excessive force - IPCA

But the IPCA did not recommend charges be laid against the officer or an employment process take place.

A memorial to Kaoss Price at the spot on State Highway 3 where he attempted to escape police. Photo: NZME / Mike Scott

An investigation into the police shooting of Kaoss Price in 2022 has found the officer who fired the fatal shot used excessive force.

An Independent Police Conduct Authority (IPCA) report into the shooting on State Highway 3 between New Plymouth and Waitara has been released today.

Kaoss Price was shot and killed after he rammed a police dog van and attempted to hijack a member of the public's vehicle.

An officer fired six times at the 22-year-old.

The watchdog found in its 34-page report the force used was excessive, but it did not recommend charges be laid against the officer or an employment process take place.

Police have acknowledged the findings.

Central District Commander Superintendent Dion Bennett said the staff involved took swift action to keep themselves and the public safe.

"They had to make split-second decisions under extreme stress, and I'm confident they acted with professionalism, in the best interests of public safety.

"No officer wants to use lethal force, and our thoughts remain with the Price family as they too process these findings."

Police acknowledged too that the officers failed to advise police communications or their supervisor that they were armed, as police policy required.

Price was later found to be unarmed at the time of the incident.
