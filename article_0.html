Wellington to lose an electorate in proposed change

Three electorates would vanish and two new ones would be set up in the capital as lines for local seats are redrawn.

Photo: RNZ

The lower North Island will lose one electorate, under proposals from the Representation Commission.

It's proposed creating two new electorates, called Kenepuru and Kapiti, and removing the Ōhāriu, Mana, and Ōtaki electorates.

The Commission has also proposed some boundary changes, due to population changes.

In West Auckland, the New Lynn, Kelston, and Te Atatu electorates will be re-configured, with the proposed new names of Waitākere, Glendene, and Ranui.

The Tauranga and Bay of Plenty electorates will also be re-configured, with a new Mount Maunganui electorate to reflect the re-drawn boundaries.

The public will be able to make objections and counter-objections until May 21, with public hearings set down for June.

The boundaries and names will be finalised on the eighth of August.
