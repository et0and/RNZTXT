Watch live: 'The era of extreme price increases is over' - Willis

PM Christopher Luxon and Finance Minister Nicola Willis are speaking to reporters after the Reserve Bank cut the cash rate by 25 basis points.

The prime minister and finance minister are due to speak to media about 4pm. You can watch in the stream at the top of this story.

Prime Minister Christopher Luxon and Finance Minister Nicola Willis are speaking to reporters after the Reserve Bank cut the cash rate by 25 basis points.

The first reduction in the cash rates in more than four years, it has led commercial banks to start cutting their interest rates almost immediately.

However, it has not been received well by some economists, who criticised it as a significant shift from RBNZ's previous statements.

Willis, in a written statement ahead of this afternoon's media conference, said the move would be welcome relief for families and businesses.

"New Zealand has been suffering an acute cost-of-living crisis since the middle of 2021, with weekly food budgets stretched thin, mortgage repayments high and confidence in our living rooms, offices and boardrooms low," she said.

"I am pleased the Reserve Bank's decision to lower the OCR today shows it has confidence that inflation is under control and the era of extreme price increases is over."

She said it would mean New Zealanders paying less interest on mortgages and credit cards, and signalled the hard conditions faced by businesses were easing - giving those businesses confidence to invest and hire again.

She took some credit for the economic shift.

"This government has delivered on its promise to Kiwis - our careful and deliberate plan to get on top of inflation is working, and we are seeing the green shoots of recovery."
