Wayne Brown makes announcement on Auckland port's future

The Port of Auckland boss recently told RNZ they were in negotiations to sell two wharves to council.

Watch Mayor Wayne Brown and officials from Port of Auckland and Maritime Union speaking about 11am here:

Auckland Mayor Wayne Brown is set to make announcement on the future of the Port of Auckland and Auckland Council's Long-term Plan.

He will be joined by Port of Auckland chief executive Roger Gray and the Maritime Union of New Zealand Auckland Secretary Grant Williams.

A record number of people gave feedback on the Long-Term Plan, with Aucklanders having their say on options and trade-offs of activities and services delivery over the next decade.

The draft plan's central proposal included a proposed rates increase for the average household of 7.5 percent from 2024 to 2025, 3.5 percent for the following year, and 8 percent the year after that.

Last month, Gray told Checkpoint they were in negotiations to sell council the smaller Captain Cook and Marsden wharves next to Bledisloe.

That sale would be subject to a condition, he said. The port wanted consent and approval to build a wharf along the northern face of Bledisloe where the seawall was.

The port has had consent for the demolition of Marsden Wharf since 2012, due to its poor structure.

Gray said this plan would help ease the problem of ferry delays due to cruise ships arriving or departing.

The ideas were similarly outlined in 2017 as part of the port's 30-year masterplan.

A previous proposal by the port to extend the Bledisloe Wharf was opposed by residents, who said it would ruin views.
