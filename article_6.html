NRL recap: Storm trump Warriors 38-24

The Melbourne Storm have ended the Warriors' winning streak in Auckland.

Marcelo Montoya of the Warriors scores a try during the Melbourne Storm v New Zealand Warriors match during the Telstra NRL Premiership at AAMI Park, Melbourne Photo: Photosport / Scott Barbour

The Warriors came into this game on an absolute high after stringing three wins in a row over some quality opponents, whilst the Storm were under strength, missing multiple key players.

But the Warriors blew a 14-point lead to go down to the Storm 24-38.

After scoring three quickfire tries to start the game, the Warriors lost control in slippery conditions as the Storm surged back to lodge a 16th straight win over the New Zealand side.

The Warriors closed within two points in the second half but their hopes faded when wingers Marcel Montoya and Dallin Watene-Zelezniak were both sent to the sin bin, reducing them to 11 players.
