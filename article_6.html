Cricket: Black Caps rolled by Australia in first innings

The Australian bowlers have ripped through the Black Caps batting lineup on the opening day of the second cricket test at Hagley Oval in Christchurch.

Josh Hazelwood and his Australian teammates celebrate the dismissal of Darryl Mitchell. Photo: John Davidson/Image Central

The Australian bowlers have ripped through the Black Caps batting lineup on the opening day of the second cricket test at Hagley Oval in Christchurch.

New Zealand have been dismissed for 162 just before tea having been 71-3 at lunch.

Pace bowler Josh Hazelwood has been the chief destroyer, taking 5-31 his 12th five wicket haul in tests while Mitchell Starc finished with has taken 3-59.

Post lunch Daryl Mitchell (4) was the first to go, followed by Kane Williamson who made just 17 in his 100th test.

Glenn Phillips made only two while Scott Kuggeleijn's struggles on the test scene continued dismissed first ball.

Tom Blundell got through to 22 before being dismissed leaving New Zealand reeling at 107-8.

Skipper Tim Southee and Matt Henry then combined for a quickfire 55 run, ninth wicket stand before Southee was dismissed for 26 while Henry was the last to go for 26.

Australia lead the two match series 1-nil after a comprehensive win in the first test in Wellington.
