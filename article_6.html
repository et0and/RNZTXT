Super Rugby Pacific team namings: Savea to start, DMac and Beauden to fullback

Moana's new big signing will make his debut as captain, while rising star Josh Jacomb has been handed the 10 jersey for the Chiefs.

The teams for the first weekend of Super Rugby Pacific 2025 have been announced, with Ardie Savea set to make his Moana Pasifika debut as number eight and captain. They play the Force in the last game of the round, while the Crusaders host the Hurricanes in the opening match on Friday night. The feature game of the weekend sees the Blues and Chiefs play a rematch of last year's final, at Eden Park on Saturday evening.

Ardie Savea of Moana Pasifika. Photo: Andrew Cornaga/www.photosport.nz

Other talking points from the team namings:

Blues coach Vern Cotter has benched former All Black halfback Finlay Christie in favour of Taufa Funaki. He's also opted to put Beauden Barrett at fullback, with Harry Plummer at first five. An injury to Hoskins Sotutu gives Cam Suafoa a start at number right.

Damian McKenzie is also playing fullback this weekend, with rising star Josh Jacomb given the nod at 10 for the Chiefs. That bumps Emoni Narawa to the bench, so former All Blacks Sevens player Leroy Carter will debut on the wing. All Blacks Tupou Vaa'i, Samipeni Finau and Anton Lienert-Brown will be eased back into action off the bench.

Noah Hotham starts at halfback for the Crusaders, with Kyle Preston on the bench. That means there's no room for stalwart Mitch Drummond. Taha Kemara starts at 10, with former Wallaby James O'Connor on the bench.

Taine Robinson, who moved south from the Crusaders last season, has been given the starting first five spot in the Highlanders over Cam Millar. Sam Gilbert shifts to fullback to accommodate new co-captain Timoci Tavatavanawai at second five, while Southland wing Michael Manson will make his debut.

Callum Harkin was not contracted at the start of the season but will start at fullback for the Hurricanes. Another Sevens player, Fatafehi Fineanganofo, gets a start on the wing. Harry Godfrey was won the race for the starting first five spot ahead of Riley Hohepa.
