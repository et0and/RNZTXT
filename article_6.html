Black Caps' pace attack gets breakthrough against Australia

Black Caps bowlers strike three times in second session to knock off Australia's top order in first Test in Wellington.

Matt Henry has two wickets at tea. Photo: Andrew Cornaga/www.photosport.nz

The Black Caps bowlers struck three times in the second session to knock off Australia's top order and leave the visitors 147-4 at tea in the first Test in Wellington.

Australia lost three wickets for 24 runs as New Zealand took control of the session.

In his first Test in New Zealand Marnus Labuschagne did not last long.

Scott Kuggeleijn had an out-of-form Labuschagne caught in the slips by Daryl Mitchell for 1 run off 27 balls in the fourth over after lunch.

Opener Usman Khawaja was the next to go as Matt Henry was rewarded with his second wicket when he bowled him for a cautious 33.

Travis Head barely troubled the scorers getting out for 1 off six balls as Will O'Rourke got his first wicket of the series.

Earlier, Australia were 62-1 for at lunch after being sent in to bat on a green pitch in overcast conditions.

Some inaccurate bowling to start the day gave openers Steve Smith and Khawaja an opportunity to capitalise.

Smith was New Zealand's first wicket of the day when he edged a Henry delivery to wicketkeeper Tom Blundell in the 25th over be out for 31.

Khawaja survived an LBW review in the 21st over and New Zealand lost a review.

The Black Caps bowled 15 overs of pace in the first hour of the day headed by captain Tim Southee who took the new ball alongside Henry.

Newcomer O'Rourke and Kuggeleijn in his second Test were the other bowlers used in New Zealand's all-out pace attack in the first session.

New Zealand opted to include Kuggeleijn in the playing 11 in place of spinner Mitch Santner.

Australia are playing with the same 11 that lost to the West Indies in Brisbane in January.

This the first test series between the Black Caps and Australia in New Zealand since 2016.
