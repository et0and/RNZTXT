Brilliant Ravindra powers Black Caps into Champions Trophy semi-finals

A century from Rachin Ravindra guided the Black Caps to a comfortable victory over Bangladesh.

New Zealand’s Rachin Ravindra celebrates during the ICC Champions Trophy one-day international against Bangladesh , 2025. Photo: AFP

A century from Rachin Ravindra has guided the Black Caps to a comfortable victory over Bangladesh and a place in the Champions Trophy semi-finals.

New Zealand won by five wickets in Rawalpindi.

After being put into bat Bangladesh scored 236 for nine in their 50 overs with captain Najmul Hossain Shanto top scoring with 77.

Spinner Michael Bracewell took four wickets for 26 runs, while fast bowler Will O'Rourke took two for 48.

In reply New Zealand lost Will Young in the first over without scoring while Kane Williamson made just five.

They were 15 for two at one stage.

However Ravindra and Devon Conway (30) put on 57 runs for the third wicket and then in combination with Tom Latham, Ravindra helped guide the Black Caps towards victory.

Ravindra was dismissed for 112. His innings including 12 fours and one six. It was his fourth ODI century.

Tom Latham was dismissed for 55.

Glenn Phillips finished unbeaten on 21 and Michael Bracewell was 11 not out.

New Zealand got home with 23 balls to spare.

With two wins, New Zealand qualify for the semi-finals from group A along with India.

New Zealand's last group game is against India in Dubai on Sunday which will decide the top qualifier.
