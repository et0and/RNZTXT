Warriors accept Agar fine, suspension

The club says it's in their best interests "to accept the penalty and move on", following an investigation into an altercation between their assistant coach and a journalist in Vegas.

Warriors assistant coach Richard Agar (left) and head coach Andrew Webster. Photo: Andrew Cornaga/www.photosport.nz

The Warriors assistant coach Richard Agar has accepted the fine and suspension handed down by the National Rugby League following an investigation into an altercation in Las Vegas.

Agar was the subject of an Integrity Unit probe over allegations that he physically assaulted English journalist John Davidson in the media box at half-time of the Warriors season opener against the Canberra Raiders.

The breach notice issued last week proposed a $10,000 fine (half-suspended) and a three-game suspension, along with a requirement for appropriate education and training.

Warriors chief executive Cameron George said the club and Agar have accepted the penalty.

"It's in the best interests of the club, the players and the game to accept the penalty and move on," he said.

"We don't condone Richard's actions. While he has a different view on some elements of the breach notice he wants to put the club first and accept the decision."

Agar said he regrets what happened.

"I know it's not acceptable. I accept the penalty and now want to move forward and learn from this."
