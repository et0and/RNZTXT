Black Caps' pace attack gets breakthrough against Australia

Black Caps dismiss Steve Smith to get late breakthrough in first session of first Test against Australia in Wellington.

New Zealand bowler Matt Henry celebrates the wicket of Steve Smith. Photo: Andrew Cornaga/www.photosport.nz

The Black Caps got late breakthrough to have Australia 62-1 at lunch on day one of the first test in Wellington.

New Zealand won the toss and sent Australia in to bat on a green pitch in overcast conditions.

Some inaccurate bowling to start the day gave openers Steve Smith and Usman Khawaja an opportunity to capitalise.

Smith edged a Matt Henry delivery which was caught by wicketkeeper Tom Blundell in the 25th over be out for 31.

Khawaja survived an LBW review in the 21st over and New Zealand lost a review. He will resume after lunch on 28.

In his first Test in New Zealand Marnus Labuschagne will be at the crease on 1 when the second session starts.

The Black Caps bowled 15 overs of pace in the first hour of the day headed by captain Tim Southee who took the new ball alongside Henry.

Newcomer Will O'Rourke and Scott Kuggeleijn in his second Test were the other bowlers used in New Zealand's all-out pace attack in the first session.

New Zealand opted to include Kuggeleijn in the playing 11 in place of spinner Mitch Santner.

Australia are playing with the same 11 that lost to the West Indies in Brisbane in January.

This the first test series between the Black Caps and Australia in New Zealand since 2016.
