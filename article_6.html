The best photos from day 11 of the Paris Olympics

Dame Lisa Carrington and her kayaking sprint team dominated out on the water, with strong showings out on the track and climbing wall as well.

for weds am

New Zealand's Lisa Carrington, New Zealand's Alicia Hoskin, New Zealand's Olivia Brett and New Zealand's Tara Vaughan win their heat in women's kayak four 500m kayak sprint competition at Vaires-sur-Marne Nautical Stadium. Photo: OLIVIER MORIN / AFP

Dame Lisa Carrington's kayaking women's team have dominated out on the water in their heats on Tuesday.

The women's K4 500m team which consists of Carrington, Alicia Hoskin, Olivia Brett and Tara Vaughan kept their led straight from the start, advancing straight to the finals.

Carrington and Hoskin also won their K2 500m heat, moving on to the semi-finals.

The men's teams also made a strong performance, although both the K4 500m team consisting of Max Brown, Grant Clancy, Kurtis Imrie and Hamish Legarth and the K2 500m of Hamish Legarth and Kurtis Imrie, had to battle it out in the quarter-finals to advance to the semi-finals.

First-time Olympian middle-distance runner Maia Ramsden is through to the semi-final of the 1500m after coming sixth in her heat.

Climber Julian David made two personal best times in sport climbing, clinching 5.24 seconds in the first round, only to beat that in the elimination heat with a time of 5.20, defeating Iran's Reza Ali Pour.
