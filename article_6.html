English teenager beats five-times world darts champion Van Barneveld

Sixteen-year-old Luke Littler his continued his dream run at the World Darts Championship with a 4-1 victory over five-times world champion Raymond Van Barneveld in the last 16.

English darts player Luke Littler. Photo: AFP

Littler, born 21 days after 56-year-old Van Barneveld won his last world title in 2007, hit nine 180's to overpower the veteran Dutchman.

The Englishman became the youngest player to reach the World Championship quarter-finals.

"Unbelievable! My average has shown it, my double percentage has shown it - I don't know what to say!", Littler told Sky Sports.

"He (Van Barneveld) said 'you can go all the way, I hope you can go all the way'. I said 'thank you' - he's a true gentleman and I respect Raymond."

Littler will play Northern Ireland's Brendan Dolan in the last eight.
