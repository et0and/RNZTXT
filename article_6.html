Olympics 2024: What to watch on day 11 of the Paris Games

What to watch on day 11 in Paris: Canoe sprints, cycling pursuit, speed climbing, wrestling and the 1500m.

Photo: RNZ / Photosport

After gold for Finn Butcher in the kayak cross on day 10 there's more action in the water with the canoe sprints.

The action gets underway at 7.30pm but New Zealand's first paddlers aren't on until Max Brown and Grant Clancy in the men's C2 500m heats at 8.30pm, followed by Kurtis Imrie and Hamish Legarth in the men's K2 500m heat at 9.30pm.

At 10.10pm the women's K2 500m heats will see Lisa Carrington and Alicia Hoskin, and Aimee Fisher and Lucy Matehaere in action.

Each of the quarterfinals follow later in the evening if the New Zealand paddlers qualify.

First time Olympian, middle-distance runner Maia Ramsden has round one of the 1500m at 8.05pm.

An Olympic sport that debuted in Tokyo, New Zealand's competitors went into the Games on a winning streak.

Julian David gets underway with a seeding round at 11pm before the elimination round at 11.40pm.

After a silver cycling medal in the women's team sprint on Tuesday morning, the cyclists are back on the track for the women's team pursuit qualifying round at 3.30am. The eight fastest teams (from a starting 10) will qualify for round one.

The men's pursuit team is already through to the first round and will take on Belgium at 5.14am.

They're racing 4km at the National Velodrome.

More action on the water starting with Micah Wilkinson and Erica Dawson in the nacra mixed multihull races at 10pm.

Kite races for Lukas Walton-Keim (10.03pm) and Justina Kitchen (10.13pm)

New Zealand's first women to wrestle at the Olympic games, Tayla Ford will be in action in the women's 68kg freestyle repechage at 9pm.
