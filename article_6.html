Mountain Bike: Rotorua's Goombes wins Red Bull Rampage

Rotorua's Robin Goomes has won the inaugural Red Bull Rampage Women's Freeride mountain bike event in Utah.

Robin Goomes completes a back flip during the womenâ€™s final at Red Bull Rampage in Virgin, Utah. Photo: Christian Pondella / Red Bull Content Pool

It was two big backflips which saw Goomes outclass her rivals in the eyes of the judges.

In addition to her tricks, Goomes stomped her 41-foot drop, and cleanly weaved down her top technical chute.

The 24-year-old came out with 85 points from the judges, scoring the highest from the seven-strong field, with fellow New Zealander Vero Sandler in fifth place.

"It hasn't yet sunk in. It's been such a process, so I'm trying to take it all in and enjoy it. I'm so stoked for my crew, they've gone so hard," Goomes said.

Goomes receives $25,000 in prize money.

The two-week long event is often referred to as the 'Super Bowl' of mountain biking and is the first time women have competed.

The format is similar to freestyle skiing and freestyle snowboarding, where competitors are judged on their choice of lines down the hill, their technical ability and the complexity of tricks

Riders have to dig out their own courses, with each rider having three diggers to complete their competition zones.

Then there are the riggers who rope in athletes and their diggers on the steep sections during the build process.

Goomes riding on day 3 practice at Red Bull Rampage in Virgin, Utah. Photo: Christian Pondella / Red Bull Content Pool

The competition first took place in 2001 but by 2005 the organisers cancelled it due to the increasing risk competitors were taking.

It returned after a three year break in 2008.

From left, Georgia Astle, Robin Goomes and Casey Brown celebrate at Red Bull Rampage in Virgin, Utah. Photo: Robin O'Neill / Red Bull Content Pool

This was not Goomes first time competing in Utah, she took part in the Red Bull Formation in 2022.

But in the lead-up to this competition she has had to battle a concussion and a shoulder injury that kept her off the bike for a time.

"It's a lot to get to this moment, so to win is just the icing on the top," she said.
