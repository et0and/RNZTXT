Black Caps stretch lead past 250 against Australia

Some big hitting from Scott Kuggeleijn has pushed the Black Caps' lead over Australia to 251 runs in the second Test in Christchurch.

New Zealand's Scott Kuggeleijn in action during the second Test against Australia at Hagley Oval. Photo: photosport

Some big hitting from Scott Kuggeleijn has pushed the Black Caps' lead over Australia to 251 runs in the second Test in Christchurch.

New Zealand were 345 for six in their second innings at tea on day three at Hagley Oval, with Australia having claimed three quick wicket midway through the second session - including Rachin Ravindra for 82 and Daryl Mitchell for 58.

Number eight Kuggeleijn's unbeaten 38 from 36 balls - featuring four fours and two sixes - shifted momentum back in the Black Caps' favour, with Glenn Phillips not out on 16, with the pair having put on a quickfire 49 for the seventh wicket.

It could prove to be an important stand, with Australia potentially set to chase more than 300 in the final innings of the Test to seal a 2-0 series sweep.

New Zealand have a chance of squaring the series, helped by half-centuries to opener Tom Latham (73), along with Ravindra and Mitchell, who combined for 123 for the fourth wicket.

Mitchell was the first to depart, caught behind off Josh Hazlewood after striking the pace bowler for successive fours from the two previous deliveries.

Ravindra followed - also snared by wicketkeeper Alex Carey - off the first ball of a new spell from Australian captain Pat Cummins (3-49).

Tom Blundell's modest form with the bat continued when he struck a poor delivery from Cameron Green to cover to depart for nine.

Earlier, Latham added eight to his overnight score before he was caught by wicketkeeper Alex Carey off Pat Cummins' bowling.

The Australians successfully reviewed after the original decision was not out, with replays showing a fine inside edge from Latham's bat.
