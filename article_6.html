White Ferns down another player for Sri Lanka series

The White Ferns injury crisis deepens with loss of another batter before Friday's second one-dayer against Sri Lanka.

White Ferns are now replacing their replacements. Photo: PHOTOSPORT

White Ferns batter Lauren Down has been ruled out of the one-day series against Sri Lanka with a back injury, after joining the squad as an injury replacement herself last week.

Down, who has played 35 one-dayers for New Zealand, injured her back on the morning of Tuesday's washed out game in Napier and has not recovered in time to play a part in the remaining two matches, scheduled for Nelson's Saxton Oval on Friday and Sunday.

Down came into the squad for Bella James, who picked up a quadricep strain that requires six weeks of rehabilitation.

Canterbury Magicians youngster Izzy Sharp will replace Down. Sharp was originally selected for the upcoming T20 series only, but the uncapped 20-year-old will now join the ODI squad in Nelson on the eve of game two.

White Ferns coach coach Ben Sawyer said the team was feeling for Down.

"We're all really disappointed for Lauren to be ruled out of the series," he said. "Unfortunately, injuries are part of the game and we wish her a speedy recovery.

"It does provide an exciting opportunity for Izzy to join the group in Nelson for these last two matches and we're looking forward to welcoming her."

Sawyer also confirmed Otago Sparks pace-bowler Emma Black would join the squad on Saturday, as cover for Hannah Rowe, who will miss the final game of the series to be maid-of-honour at the wedding of Central Hinds teammate Mikaela Greig.
