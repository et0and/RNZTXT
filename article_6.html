New Zealand given special role ahead of Chess Olympiad

New Zealand will represent Oceania in the torch ceremony to mark the start of the 'Olympics of Chess'.

Grandmaster Vishy Anand is expected to travel to New Zealand for the torch ceremony. Photo: Johansen, Carina / NTB via AFP

New Zealand has been given a special role ahead of the Olympics of Chess, the Chess Olympiad.

The World Chess Federation, FIDE chose Aotearoa to represent Oceania in a torch ceremony to mark the start of the competition.

The Olympiad torch was being passed across the world, and was in New Zealand for a ceremony in Auckland on Sunday.

New Zealand Chess Federation Vice President Paul Spiller said high-ranking chess official Grandmaster Vishy Anand was travelling from India to attend.

He said it was exciting for the New Zealand chess community to be recognised by the governing body of chess.
