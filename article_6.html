Without a Fight wins Melbourne Cup

Without a Fight has become the first horse since 2021 to win the Melbourne Cup and the Caufield Cup in the same year.

Mark Zahra riding Without a Fight wins the Lexus Melbourne Cup during Melbourne Cup Day at Flemington Racecourse on November 07, 2023. Photo: Quinn Rooney

Without a Fight has become the first horse since 2021 to win the Melbourne Cup and the Caufield Cup in the same year.

New Zealand mare Ethereal was the last horse to achieved the feat.

It's jockey Mark Zahra's second Melbourne Cup having ridden to victory on Gold Trip last year.

Second was Soulcombe and third Sheraz.

The race worth nearly $9 million sees the winning horse get $4.8 million, with the winning jockey getting $240 thousand of that.

Second place receives $1.1 million, third $550,000, fourth $350,000, fifth $230,000, and sixth to 12th $160,000.

Zahra told Trackside immediately after the win that Without a Fight "was an absolute gentleman".

"I was on a horse that you can just sit on. He's got an electric turn of foot and he just pulled me all the way to the line and it was all over.

"I stood up in the irons giving it the two fingers for two wins, I don't what I was thinking..what an idiot"

The Melbourne Cup Photo: Jeff Crow / www.photosport.co.nz

"It's been a great race for the family, it's been a while between drinks and it's good to get another one," said trainer Sam Freedman.

"An extraordinary ride, he got him onto the rail from a wide gate, relaxed, switched him off, made a run."

Zahra is the first jockey since Glen Boss on Makybe Diva to win consecutive Melbourne Cups.

"The stars aligned for Gold Trip last year and aligned for this horse this year.

"Just a few things and the way he won the Caulfield Cup, I was confident he would run it."

The field was reduced to 23 after Cleveland was scratched.
