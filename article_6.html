Five Kiwis named in teams of the year

A trio of Black Caps and two White Ferns were named in their respective ICC Teams of the Year.

Kane Williamson was named in the Men's Test Team of the Year. Photo: Photosport / John Davidson / www.photosport.nz

Kane Williamson, Amelia Kerr, Lea Tahuhu, Darly Mitchell and Mark Chapman have all been named in their respective International Cricket Council's Teams of the Year.

Williamson was named in the Men's Test Team of the Year, Kerr was named in the Women's ODI and T20 Teams of the Year, Tahuhu was named in the Women's ODI Team of the Year, Mitchell was named in the Men's ODI Team of the Year and Chapman in the Men's T20 Team of the Year.

Teams were identified from selections submitted by the specialist panel of media representatives that make up the ICC Voting Academy.

The esteemed panel made their selections based on player performances during the calendar year, nominating their playing eleven to include a captain and a wicketkeeper.

The ICC Awards 2023 announcements will continue Wednesday and Thursday, when 13 individual award winners will be declared.
