After The Party wins big at 2024 NZ TV Awards

The TVNZ drama After The Party took home nine awards, while RNZ's NZ Wars won Best Māori Programme.

Robyn Malcolm and Kirana Gaeta in After The Party. Photo: Supplied / TVNZ

TVNZ's After the Party has taken home a record haul at the 2024 New Zealand Television Awards.

The six-part drama series, produced by Lingo Pictures and Luminous Beast, claimed nine awards at this year's ceremony, including NZ On Air Best Drama, Best Script: Drama, Best Editing: Drama and Comedy, and Best Actor and Actress wins for Peter Mullan and Robyn Malcolm.

TVNZ's 1 News also took out the 2024 award for Best News Coverage for their reportage of Cyclone Gabrielle - One Year On, while TVNZ's Q+A was named Best Current Affairs Programme with presenter Jack Tame being awarded Best Presenter: News and Current Affairs.

Jack Tame was awarded Best Presenter: News and Current Affairs. Photo: Supplied / TVNZ

Shortland Street's Bella Kalolo won the publicly voted Television Personality of the Year, while Dame Julie Christie was honoured as the 2024 Television Legend.

The awards ceremony, held at Auckland's Kiri Te Kanawa Theatre on Friday night, also saw two wins for RNZ.

Ruamata: It's More Than Hockey (Mairanga Media / RNZ) claimed Best Sports Programme on the night, while NZ Wars: Stories of Tauranga Moana (Aotearoa Media Collective / RNZ / TVNZ+) received Te Māngai Pāho Best Māori Programme award.
