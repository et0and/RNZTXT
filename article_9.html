Indie takes top prize above frontrunners at Critics Choice

Sean Baker's Anora secured a surprise best picture win at at the 30th Annual Critics Choice Awards over frontrunners The Brutalist and Emilia Pérez.

By Velvet Winter, ABC Entertainment

Sean Baker and Mikey Madison, winners of the the Best Picture award for "Anora," pose in the press room during the 30th Annual Critics Choice Awards at Barker Hangar on 7 February, 2025 in Santa Monica, California. Photo: Emma McIntyre/Getty Images for Critics Choice Association/AFP

Sean Baker's Anora secured a surprise best picture win at the 30th Annual Critics Choice Awards over frontrunners The Brutalist and Emilia Pérez.

Those films did not go home empty-handed, with Emilia Pérez picking up three awards and Adrien Brody winning best actor for The Brutalist.

Another surprise came in the form of Jon M Chu picking up best director for Wicked after being mostly locked out of wins for most of the awards season.

The blockbuster musical won three awards in total, including a nod for its impressive production design.

Over in TV, historical drama Shōgun took out four awards, including best drama series, best actor, best supporting actor and best supporting actress.

It follows the show's record-breaking 18 Emmy sweep and its four wins at the Golden Globes.

HBO comedy Hacks was close behind at three, with Baby Reindeer and The Penguin also nabbing two awards.
