Lorde joins Charli XCX on 'Girl, so confusing' remix

Lorde is known to release music on winter solstice and this year is no different.

Photo: Kevin Mazur / MTV

Lorde is known to release music on solstice and this year is no different.

On this winter solstice (summer solstice for the US), Charli XCX has announced a 'Girl, so confusing' remix with the New Zealand artist, set to release at 4pm NZT.

Lorde teased a snippet of the track on her Instagram story earlier today.

In it, she sings: "Well honestly I was speechless / when I woke up to your voice note / You told me how you've been feeling / Let's work it out on the remix."

Charli XCX has previously spoken about Lorde inspiring the original song, saying she was "super jealous" of Lorde's success when she was younger.

"She was into my music. She had big hair; I had big hair. She wore black lipstick; I once wore black lipstick. You create these parallels and think, 'Well, that could have been me'. But it couldn't have because we're completely different people."

Fans have noticed the similarities and will often greet them with a running gag - deliberately mistaking Charli for Lorde and vice versa.

Earlier this month, the '360' singer released her sixth studio album Brat, which Lorde praised, writing, "There is NO ONE like this bitch".

The 'Girl, so confusing' remix marks a trend of Lorde announcing music on solstice - her last album Solar Power was announced on summer solstice of 2021. A year later on the same date, she released the music video for 'The Path'.

On 5 June, she posted to Instagram, potentially teasing a new album.

"Use the existing tools wherever possible. If the tools do not exist you are spiritually obligated to create them," she wrote.
